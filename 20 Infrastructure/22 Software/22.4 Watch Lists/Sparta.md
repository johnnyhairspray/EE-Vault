# Sparta

A secret ICE/DHS watchlist — one of more than a dozen covert databases confirmed by two senior national security officials to journalist Ken Klippenstein in January 2026. Sparta is used to track protesters (anti-ICE and pro-Palestinian), individuals labeled "domestic terrorists," and people the Trump administration has designated as "aggressive protesters."

---

## What It Is

Sparta is a watchlist application operating within the DHS/FBI surveillance infrastructure. It generates two classified report types: **HEL-A** and **HEL-C** — the content and criteria of which have not been publicly disclosed.

The broader ecosystem of covert watchlists Sparta operates within includes codenames: **Bluekey**, **Grapevine**, **Hummingbird**, **Reaper**, **Sandcastle**, **Sienna**, and **Slipstream**. Hummingbird was created specifically to vet and track Afghan immigrants seeking to resettle in the United States.

Per Klippenstein's DHS source: these systems "aren't technically watchlists" in the strict legal sense — a "watchlist" in national security terminology specifically refers to the Terrorist Screening Dataset. The systems are described as apps, databases of specific data, investigative files, and tip-off systems. The source's own characterization: they are all "parts of an above Top Secret watchlisting enterprise." The semantic distinction is a technicality that enables official denial while the function remains identical.

---

## Relationship to ELITE

Sparta is best understood as a **data source and component node** within the operational layer [[ELITE]] provides. The architecture:

- **Sparta** (and Reaper, Grapevine, etc.) — identify and flag individuals for tracking; generate HEL-A/HEL-C reports
- **ELITE** — ingests those flags alongside Medicaid data, DMV records, utility bills, and court records to generate address confidence scores and map targets for enforcement operations
- **ImmigrationOS** — the master Palantir operational layer connecting all of it

Whether Sparta remains a discrete active system or has been absorbed into the ELITE/ImmigrationOS architecture as a data feed is not publicly confirmed. Either way it functions as a watchlist — the categorization holds regardless of its technical status, because the data it produces is used to populate a targeting system that acts on individuals as if they were on a watchlist.

## The Official Denial

DHS spokesperson Tricia McLaughlin stated: *"There is NO database of 'domestic terrorists' run by DHS."*

Acting ICE Director Todd Lyons told Congress: *"I can assure you that there is no database that's tracking United States citizens."*

Two senior national security officials directly contradicted this on record to Klippenstein. A third — a senior DHS watchlist official — privately acknowledged the systems exist while insisting they "aren't technically watchlists." The Cato Institute's Patrick Eddington: Lyons either committed perjury or doesn't know what his own agency does. Neither is reassuring.

## What It Tracks

According to the senior officials, Sparta and the broader watchlist ecosystem track:

- **Anti-ICE protesters** — including people who criticized ICE officers while operating a vehicle (the documented case of [[Renee Good]])
- **Pro-Palestinian protesters**
- People labeled "Antifa"
- Individuals designated **"aggressive protesters"** — a DHS classification for people who film ICE officers, shout at them, or approach them

Tom Homan, Trump's border czar: *"We're going to create a database where those people that are arrested for interference, impeding and assault, we're going to make them famous."*

## The Expanding Watchlisting Enterprise — February 2026

As DHS was publicly denying the existence of watchlists, ICE was simultaneously building a **new watchlisting enterprise** in Northern Virginia. Contractor Xcelerate Solutions posted a job listing for a Top Secret-cleared "Criminal Analyst" to join ICE's "watchlisting team." Xcelerate is a DHS, Pentagon, and FBI contractor involved in government personnel vetting and internal leak detection.

The system is not consolidating. It is expanding.

## The Operational Context

Sparta exists within the same enforcement architecture as [[ImmigrationOS]] — [[Palantir Technologies]]' $30M+ ICE contract providing near-real-time visibility into immigrant movements. The watch list and the operational enforcement layer are complementary: Sparta identifies targets, ImmigrationOS/ELITE tracks their movements, [[Flock Cameras]] and commercial location data via Venntel provide real-time location.

[[Stephen Miller]]'s May 2025 directive setting ICE arrest quotas at 3,000/day created operational pressure that makes dragnet watch lists like Sparta more, not less, likely to sweep in non-targeted individuals. Federal Judge Patrick Schiltz compiled a list of 96 court orders defied by ICE in Minnesota alone by January 2026.
