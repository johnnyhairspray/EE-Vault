# Sparta

A secret ICE/DHS watchlist — one of more than a dozen covert databases confirmed by two senior national security officials to journalist Ken Klippenstein in January 2026. Sparta is used to track protesters (anti-ICE and pro-Palestinian), individuals labeled "domestic terrorists," and people the Trump administration has designated as "aggressive protesters."

## What It Is

Sparta is a watch list application operated within the DHS/FBI surveillance infrastructure. It generates two classified report types: **HEL-A** and **HEL-C** reports — the content and criteria of which have not been publicly disclosed.

The broader ecosystem of covert watchlists Sparta operates within includes codenames: **Bluekey**, **Grapevine**, **Hummingbird**, **Reaper**, **Sandcastle**, **Sienna**, and **Slipstream**. Hummingbird was created specifically to vet and track Afghan immigrants seeking to resettle in the United States.

## The Official Denial

DHS spokesperson Tricia McLaughlin stated: *"There is NO database of 'domestic terrorists' run by DHS."*

Two senior national security officials directly contradicted this on record.

## What It Tracks

According to the senior officials, Sparta and the broader watchlist ecosystem are used to track:

- **Anti-ICE protesters** — including people who criticized ICE officers while operating a vehicle (the documented case of [[Renee Good]])
- **Pro-Palestinian protesters**
- People labeled "Antifa"
- Individuals designated "aggressive protesters" — a classification the Trump DHS created to describe people who film ICE officers, shout at them, or approach them

Tom Homan, Trump's border czar: *"We're going to create a database where those people that are arrested for interference, impeding and assault, we're going to make them famous."*

## The Operational Context

Sparta exists within the same enforcement architecture as [[ImmigrationOS]] — [[Palantir Technologies]]' $30M ICE contract providing near-real-time visibility into immigrant movements. The watch list and the operational enforcement layer are complementary systems: Sparta identifies targets, ImmigrationOS tracks their movements, [[Flock Cameras]] and commercial location data via [[Venntel]] provide real-time location.

[[Stephen Miller]]'s May 2025 directive setting ICE arrest quotas at 3,000/day created operational pressure that makes dragnet watch lists like Sparta more, not less, likely to sweep in non-targeted individuals. Federal Judge Patrick Schiltz compiled a list of 96 court orders defied by ICE in Minnesota alone by January 2026.

## The Surveillance Cartel Connection

Watch lists are only useful if they can be acted upon in real time. The integration layer connecting Sparta to operational capacity runs through:

- **[[Flock Cameras]]** — license plate readers creating movement trails
- **[[Venntel]]** — commercial location data purchased without warrants
- **[[PenLink]]** — Webloc location tracking, no-bid 2025 ICE contract
- **[[Palantir Technologies]]** — ImmigrationOS as the master operational layer

The watch list is the list. The surveillance cartel is the system that finds where the listed people are.
