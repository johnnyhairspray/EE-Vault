# Wolf Pack

Israel's military-run biometric profiling and surveillance ecosystem targeting Palestinians in the West Bank and Gaza. In operation since approximately 2020. Documented by Amnesty International in its May 2023 report *Automated Apartheid* and by multiple investigative outlets. Wolf Pack is the **database layer** beneath a suite of operational tools — Blue Wolf, Red Wolf, and White Wolf — that together constitute the most comprehensive population surveillance system currently deployed against a civilian population in an active conflict zone.

This system is the operational proof-of-concept for the [[Board of Peace]]'s proposed post-war Gaza governance infrastructure.

## The Architecture

**Wolf Pack** (the database): A vast repository containing all available information on Palestinians in the Occupied Palestinian Territories. Contents include: photographs, family member names and connections, home addresses, place of employment, education history, whether the individual is wanted by Israeli authorities, and behavioral/social assessments. Described by Amnesty International as profiling *"virtually all Palestinians in the West Bank."*

**Blue Wolf** (mobile app for soldiers): Installed on soldiers' smartphones and tablets. Allows soldiers to photograph Palestinians — including children — and instantly access their Wolf Pack profile. Cross-references facial scan against the database and returns a color-coded security designation. Data collection was **gamified**: units were ranked by the number of Palestinian faces photographed each week, with winning units receiving prizes such as paid leave. Israeli soldiers called it *"Facebook for Palestinians."* Soldiers also log subjective "negative impressions" of Palestinians' conduct during encounters, adding behavioral scoring to the biometric record. The photograph order was applied without requiring suspicion — any Palestinian could be photographed and enrolled.

**Red Wolf** (CCTV checkpoint system): Facial recognition cameras deployed at military checkpoints in Hebron and across Gaza. Scans faces of Palestinians passing through, compares against Wolf Pack database, and returns a color-coded status: green (pass), yellow (flag), red (detain). **Automatically enrolls any new face it scans** — expanding the database passively through civilian movement. Palestinians are not informed they are being scanned. Deployed at makeshift checkpoints during mass civilian evacuations in Gaza, resulting in documented cases of wrongful detention and reported field executions.

**White Wolf** (settler access app): Available to Israeli settlers in the West Bank — civilians, not military — allowing them to search the Wolf Pack database of Palestinians. The surveillance apparatus built for military use was extended to the settler population, giving armed civilians access to profiling data on their Palestinian neighbors.

## Operational Deployment in Gaza

Since October 7, 2023, Wolf Pack and its associated tools have been deployed at scale across Gaza:

- Facial recognition cameras set up at evacuation checkpoints during the forced displacement of northern Gaza's population. Palestinians ordered south at gunpoint were individually scanned before being allowed to proceed
- Quadcopter drones used to scan faces from distance, identify individuals in crowds
- Military stormed displaced persons shelters (including UN-run schools), corralled men into ditches dug by bulldozers, processed them individually through facial recognition
- After scans, soldiers revealed personal data to individuals including *"name, age, work, family members and names, place of residence, and even their personal activities"*
- Individuals whose relatives belonged to resistance movements reportedly taken away and not seen again, or returned in body bags
- Whistleblower testimonies confirmed biometric systems flagged civilians as wanted militants, leading to wrongful arrests and interrogations

## The AI Targeting Integration

Wolf Pack is the **biometric intake layer** for AI targeting systems [[Lavender AI]] and [[Gospel AI]]. The data pipeline:

Wolf Pack (identity and biometric data) → Lavender (generates target recommendations) → Gospel (locates targets) → "Where's Daddy" (tracks targets to home for strike) → strike authorization

Within the first six weeks after October 7, 2023, Lavender generated approximately **37,000 target recommendations**. Human review of each recommendation was minimal — operators described approving targets in seconds, treating Lavender's output as authoritative. Multiple documented cases of strikes on civilian homes when targets were present with family members.

[[Unit 8200]] — Israel's elite cyber-intelligence unit — operated the facial recognition program, using tools including **Corsight** (Israeli AI company) and **Google Photos** to *"pick out faces from crowds and grainy drone footage."*

## The Gaza Governance Connection

The Wolf Pack infrastructure is directly relevant to the proposed [[Board of Peace]] post-war Gaza governance framework. The leaked GITA (Gaza International Transitional Authority) procurement documents reviewed by Byline Times show the proposed digital governance backbone — covering identity verification, border control, and movement management — **matching the Oracle-Palantir technology stack** currently used in Israel's defense network.

Wolf Pack is the proof of concept. The Gaza reconstruction governance plan is the scaling proposal. The same biometric population control architecture used to manage Palestinians under occupation is the template for governing post-war Gaza under the Board of Peace.

## The Export Problem

Israel's surveillance industry — built on the Wolf Pack/Blue Wolf/Red Wolf architecture and Unit 8200's technical expertise — is a global export product. The "8200-to-tech pipeline" sends Unit 8200 veterans directly into surveillance and military technology companies globally. Tools tested on Palestinian populations are marketed internationally.

[[Peter Thiel]]'s [[Palantir Technologies]] announced a *"strategic partnership with Israel's Ministry of Defense"* in January 2024 to supply operational software for the Gaza campaign. Palantir's global surveillance infrastructure and Wolf Pack's population profiling architecture share a common operational logic: ingest biometric data, build persistent profiles, enable targeting.

## Legal Status

Amnesty International (May 2023): Documented Wolf Pack as part of Israel's system of apartheid. The forcible collection of biometric data violates Article 31 of the Fourth Geneva Convention — *"No physical or moral coercion shall be exercised against protected persons."*

UN experts (2024): Deplored Israel's use of AI to commit crimes against humanity in Gaza.

EU: Has banned social scoring technology of the type deployed in Wolf Pack. Israel signed the Council of Europe's global AI treaty in 2024 — while continuing to operate these systems.
