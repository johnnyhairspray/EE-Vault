# Palantir Gotham

Palantir's law enforcement and intelligence analytics platform. Originally developed for U.S. military and intelligence community use in Afghanistan and Iraq; adapted for domestic law enforcement beginning approximately 2011 with the LAPD deployment.

## Core Function

Gotham integrates disparate databases — criminal records, arrest history, social media, license plate data, field interview cards, surveillance footage — into a unified visual interface showing network connections between individuals, locations, and incidents.

Operators can search across all integrated data simultaneously: who a person associates with, where a vehicle has been seen, what addresses have been linked to past incidents. The system surfaces connections not visible in any single database.

## The LAPD Deployment and Operation LASER

Gotham was the software backbone of [[Operation LASER]] (2011–2019) — the LAPD's predictive policing program. Gotham automated the creation of "Chronic Offender Bulletins" by scoring individuals based on police contacts, parole status, and inferred gang affiliation. The circularity problem (more police contact → higher score → more contact) was structural to Gotham's operation, not incidental.

LAPD officially ended LASER in 2019 after a city audit identified data inconsistencies and civil rights concerns. The algorithmic logic migrated to federal infrastructure.

## The Federal Deployment

Gotham underpins Palantir's federal contracts including ImmigrationOS for ICE — the platform aggregating IRS, Social Security, passport, and commercial location data to generate immigration enforcement targets. The same data fusion and network mapping architecture LAPD used for street policing, ICE uses for mass deportation.

## The Military Deployment

Gotham is also the platform underlying [[Project Maven]] — the Pentagon's AI targeting system. The same software company provides the targeting intelligence for U.S. military strikes and the targeting intelligence for domestic immigration enforcement.

## The AI Evolution (2026)

Gotham has incorporated AI-powered predictive policing — analyzing historical arrest patterns to anticipate where specific individuals will appear. By 2026, this extends to "agentic" suggestions: not just identifying targets but recommending specific actions.
