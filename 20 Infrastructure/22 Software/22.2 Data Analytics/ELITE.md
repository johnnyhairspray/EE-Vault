# ELITE

**Enhanced Leads Identification and Targeting for Enforcement.** Palantir Technologies' geospatial targeting tool deployed for ICE immigration enforcement. Maps enforcement targets, generates per-person dossiers, and produces Address Confidence Scores indicating probability of a subject's current location.

**Contract:** Part of a $29.9 million contract that started September 2025, running through at least 2026. This builds on ImmigrationOS ($30M, July 2025). Palantir has made over $60 million from ICE in the past year in documented contracts alone.

---

## How It Works

Per the user guide obtained by 404 Media and court testimony in Oregon:

- Populates a digital map with potential deportation targets
- Each target has a dossier: name, date of birth, Alien Registration Number, photograph
- Generates a "confidence score" out of 100 for the target's current address
- Agents draw shapes on the map to select areas for raids
- An ICE agent testified under oath the tool works "kind of like Google Maps" — showing neighborhoods where a person might be located — while acknowledging the tool could be wrong even when expressing high confidence

Palantir's own documentation describes ELITE as "a targeting tool designed to improve capabilities for identifying and prioritizing high-value targets through advanced analytics."

---

## The Medicaid Data Problem

ELITE incorporates address data from HHS — specifically Medicaid enrollment records — to refine location predictions for enforcement targets. ICE and the Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services signed a data-sharing agreement giving ICE access to the personal data of **nearly 80 million Medicaid patients**.

The use of public health enrollment data for immigration enforcement creates a documented chilling effect: individuals eligible for Medicaid may avoid enrollment to avoid generating a data trail accessible to ICE. People updating their address for medical care are feeding their confidence score.

One ICE officer testified that ELITE "tells you how many people are living in this area and what's the likelihood of them actually being there" — a system generating probabilistic assessments of where enforcement targets will be found, fed by healthcare enrollment.

Palantir's official response disputed characterizations of the Medicaid data integration, stating the tool doesn't access patient medical records — a technically narrow claim that doesn't address the use of enrollment address data.

## Congressional Scrutiny — April 2026

Congressional Democrats sent a formal letter to DHS Secretary Markwayne Mullin and acting ICE Director Todd Lyons demanding answers about ELITE and Palantir's broader surveillance infrastructure.

Specific demands:
- A comprehensive report on the ELITE application: what data categories it uses and how many DHS personnel are authorized to use it
- Whether DHS has collected or processed information about people **peacefully observing, documenting, or protesting** immigration operations
- A list of all private contractors providing surveillance or analytics technologies to DHS
- All internal guidance, memos, policies, and assessments governing these systems

The lawmakers noted a direct contradiction: DHS/ICE officials testified at February House and Senate oversight hearings that DHS does not have a database tracking U.S. citizens — while ELITE court testimony and reporting documents exactly that capability.

Civil liberties groups have warned that systems like ELITE could easily expand to target any American — not just immigrants.

---

## The U.S. Citizen and Dissident Monitoring

Court testimony and field reporting in early 2026 confirmed ELITE was tracking not only immigration enforcement targets but U.S. citizens categorized as "activists," "agitators," or "dissidents."

In a March 2026 encounter in Maine, a federal agent told a protester she had been added to a "nice little database" used to monitor vocal critics of ICE. This is not an isolated anecdote — it reflects documented expansion of ELITE beyond its stated immigration enforcement function.

ICE has also used ELITE-integrated administrative subpoena workflows to demand personal data from Meta and Google to unmask anonymous social media accounts associated with protest activity — using immigration enforcement legal authority to identify political critics.

---

## The Domestic Surveillance Expansion

The pattern is documented: a platform built for immigration enforcement expanding to monitor U.S. citizens engaged in constitutionally protected political activity. The technical infrastructure doesn't distinguish between targets by citizenship status or activity type — it processes data and generates profiles regardless.

The DHS AI Use Case Inventory portrays ELITE's AI-driven outputs as "relatively limited" and promises "human oversight and additional verification steps." The 404 Media investigation indicates this significantly understates the scope and power of the tool. Promises of humans-in-the-loop consistently fall away in practice.

ELITE is the operational node where immigration enforcement infrastructure directly intersects with First Amendment activity.

---

## The Extraction Engine Connection

ELITE operationalizes the Medicaid data pipeline as a surveillance mechanism — health data collected under one legal authority repurposed for immigration enforcement. This is the cross-agency data fusion the Privatized Panopticon documents: systems originally justified for narrow purposes deployed across the full spectrum of social control. The person who updated their address at a clinic to receive care now has a higher confidence score for enforcement. Administrative extraction meets surveillance infrastructure.
