# Where's Daddy?

AI geolocation and tracking system developed by [[Unit 8200]] (IDF Signals Intelligence). One of three documented AI-assisted targeting systems used in the Gaza conflict alongside Lavender AI and The Gospel.

## Function

Where's Daddy? is a "find-fix-track" system — its role is not to identify targets (that is Lavender's function) but to locate them in real time. Specifically designed to detect when a profiled individual enters a private residence or family home.

The system draws on:
- Cell tower triangulation
- WiFi signal mapping
- The Wolf Pack database (IDF biometric and behavioral database of Palestinian residents)
- Mass surveillance data collected across Gaza

When a targeted individual enters a designated home location, the system notifies operators — enabling strikes timed to when the target is at a residential address. The name reflects the operational logic: waiting for a commander to return home.

## The +972 Magazine Documentation

The April 2024 +972 Magazine and Local Call investigation documented Where's Daddy? alongside Lavender AI as part of Israel's AI-assisted targeting infrastructure. The investigation described the system being used to generate thousands of targeting decisions in the early months of the Gaza conflict — with human operators spending seconds rather than minutes reviewing each automated designation.

The UN Special Committee report (February 2026) documented "domicide" — the systematic destruction of housing — as a documented pattern in Gaza operations, a context in which residential-address targeting logic is particularly significant.

## The Palantir Connection

In 2024, Unit 8200's parent structures signed a strategic partnership with Palantir Technologies to integrate IDF AI systems with Palantir's commercial platform. The partnership raises the question of whether targeting logic developed for Gaza operations influences Palantir's domestic and commercial AI products — or vice versa. The technical architecture is shared even if the operational context differs.
