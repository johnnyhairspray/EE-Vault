# Project Maven (Maven Smart System)

Pentagon AI program launched 2017 as a pilot to automate drone footage analysis using computer vision. Designated as a permanent Program of Record in late 2023. The U.S. military's primary AI-assisted targeting and intelligence fusion platform.

## History

**2017:** Launched by the Department of Defense as an AI accelerator project. Initial goal: automate analysis of drone footage to identify objects, vehicles, and people at scale. Google was the initial technology partner — but withdrew in 2018 after internal employee protests over the company's involvement in weapons development.

**2018–2023:** Continued development under DOD, with Palantir Technologies becoming the primary platform integrator.

**Late 2023:** Designated as a permanent Program of Record — moving from pilot/experimental status to permanent funded defense program. This locked Palantir's Gotham as the permanent targeting platform.

**March 2026:** New defense contract expanded Palantir's role further. The CIA and NGA fully integrated Maven into their operational workflow.

## Current Capabilities (2026)

Maven fuses real-time data from 150+ sensor types — satellites, UAVs, ground radar, signals intelligence — into a unified targeting interface. Key capabilities:

- **Computer vision targeting:** Automated detection, classification, and prioritization of objects and individuals across video feeds
- **AI-assisted targeting:** The system suggests targets to human operators rather than replacing human decision-making — though the pace of suggestion (reported up to 1,000/hour) effectively reduces human review to cursory approval
- **Agentic Effects Agents (early 2026):** U.S. Army integrated AI agents that autonomously recommend specific weapon systems, ordnance, and delivery platforms for identified targets
- **NGA integration:** The National Geospatial-Intelligence Agency expects Maven to transmit 100% machine-generated intelligence summaries directly to combatant commanders by mid-2026

## The Google Exit and the Protest

In 2018, approximately 4,000 Google employees signed a letter opposing the company's Project Maven contract, describing it as weaponization of AI. Google CEO Sundar Pichai announced the company would not renew the contract. Google subsequently published AI ethics principles stating the company would not develop AI for weapons.

This was the first major documented instance of tech workers using collective action to influence their company's government AI contracts. The protest succeeded — and was subsequently used as a cautionary lesson by other defense contractors and tech companies about internal AI ethics governance.

## The Domestic Architecture Connection

Maven's object detection and data fusion architecture is the technical predecessor to domestic surveillance applications. Palantir's Gotham platform — which powers Maven's military targeting — is the same platform deployed as ImmigrationOS for ICE. The distinction between military targeting abroad and law enforcement targeting domestically is organizational and legal, not technical.
