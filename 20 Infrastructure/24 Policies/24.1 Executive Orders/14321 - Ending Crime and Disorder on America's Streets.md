# Executive Order 14321

**Full title:** "Ending Crime and Disorder on America's Streets"

Signed July 24, 2025. Reframes homelessness and addiction as national security and public disorder threats; establishes the legal and policy framework for mass involuntary institutionalization and the replacement of Housing First doctrine with "accountability-based" approaches.

## What It Does

**Terminates Housing First:** The federal doctrine that stable housing is the prerequisite for addressing addiction and mental health — used since the late 1980s and supported by substantial research evidence — is replaced. The order mandates "accountability-based" approaches requiring behavioral compliance as a condition of receiving services.

**Challenges consent decrees:** Directs the Attorney General to challenge existing judicial consent decrees and legal precedents — specifically *Martin v. Boise* (Ninth Circuit, 2018), which held that cities cannot criminally enforce anti-camping ordinances against homeless individuals when no shelter space is available.

**Federal land utilization:** Directs GSA to identify federal lands for large-scale "Accountability Centers" — institutionalization facilities bypassing local zoning and environmental review processes.

**Conditional funding:** Conditions HHS and HUD federal grants on state enforcement of anti-vagrancy laws — creating financial coercion for states to comply with the federal removal mandate or lose billions in housing and health funding.

## The Civil Commitment Framework

The order's most significant long-term implication: by establishing "civil commitment" frameworks for homelessness and addiction — detention based on risk assessment rather than criminal conviction — it creates a parallel detention track that bypasses the criminal justice system's procedural protections including the Sixth Amendment right to counsel and speedy trial.

Civil commitment has traditionally been reserved for individuals with severe mental illness who are imminently and specifically dangerous. EO 14321 extends the doctrine to anyone characterized as creating "public disorder" — an expansive and subjectively applied standard.

## The NSPM-7 Intersection

NSPM-7 (September 2025) designated anti-capitalism as a terrorism indicator. EO 14321 established civil commitment infrastructure. Together they create the legal framework for institutionalizing political dissenters under the guise of public order — using mental health and public safety authorities rather than criminal prosecution, which requires higher evidentiary standards.
