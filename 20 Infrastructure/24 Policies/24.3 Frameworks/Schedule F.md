# Schedule F (Schedule Policy/Career)

Federal employment reclassification framework. Originally created by EO 13957 (October 2020), revoked by Biden (January 2021), reinstated by EO 14171 (January 20, 2025), finalized as OPM rule in February 2026, effective March 9, 2026.

Rebranded "Schedule Policy/Career" (Schedule P/C) to utilize existing statutory exemptions under the Civil Service Reform Act of 1978 — avoiding the need for new legislation.

## What It Does

Reclassifies approximately 50,000 career federal civil service positions in "policy-influencing" roles to at-will employment status. Career employees in reclassified positions:

- Lose termination protections requiring cause for removal
- Lose the right to appeal to the Merit Systems Protection Board (MSPB)
- Have whistleblower protections moved from the independent Office of Special Counsel to Agency General Counsel — attorneys who are administration appointees

**The rolling expansion:** Agencies identify "policy-influencing" roles on a department-by-department basis, OPM reviews, and the President issues specific EOs per batch. The reclassification expands continuously.

## The Yarvin / RAGE Connection

Schedule F is the legal mechanism for Curtis Yarvin's RAGE (Retire All Government Employees) framework — the NRx proposal to break the administrative state by replacing career civil servants with political loyalists. The framework doesn't require firing everyone; it requires making everyone fireable. The threat of removal without cause produces compliance without execution.

Washington Post confirmed in May 2025 that DOGE advisers cited Yarvin's framework directly: "Yarvin is the brain." Schedule F is the legal scaffolding that makes the personnel layer of DOGE's project durable — even after the mass firings are contested in court, the at-will employment status remains for reclassified positions.

## The Whistleblower Protection Problem

Moving whistleblower protections from the independent Office of Special Counsel to Agency General Counsel is the most structurally significant provision. The OSC is a standalone agency designed specifically to investigate whistleblower complaints against the executive branch — it has no line of accountability to the agencies it investigates. Agency General Counsel reports to agency leadership, which reports to political appointees.

A whistleblower filing a complaint about their agency's political appointee now files with a lawyer who works for that agency's political appointee.

## Legal Challenges

Schedule F has faced legal challenges in federal courts. The administration has maintained that Article II provides the President broad authority over the federal workforce. Courts have issued mixed rulings on the scope of that authority. As of April 2026, legal challenges are ongoing.
