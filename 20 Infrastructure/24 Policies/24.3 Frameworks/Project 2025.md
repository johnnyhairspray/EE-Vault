# Project 2025

Published April 2023 by [[The Heritage Foundation]] in coordination with 80-110 conservative organizations. A 900-page governing blueprint — formally titled **Mandate for Leadership: The Conservative Promise** — designed to be implemented beginning at noon on January 20, 2025. Explicitly addressed to the incoming president: *"By opening this book, you are now a part of it."*

Four operational pillars:
1. **Mandate for Leadership** — 900-page policy blueprint covering every federal agency
2. **Personnel Database** — "Conservative LinkedIn," loyalty-tested résumé pool for staffing the administration
3. **Presidential Administration Academy** — training program for conservative appointees on seizing agency power
4. **180-Day Playbook** — agency-by-agency implementation plan, deliberately kept from public release

## What It Does

At its core: **replace the administrative state with a loyalty apparatus**. The civil service — career professionals insulated from political pressure — is reframed as "the deep state" and targeted for elimination via [[Schedule F]], which Mandate co-architect [[Russ Vought]] explicitly designed to "destroy the administrative state and fire and traumatize federal workers."

Key provisions:
- Replace career civil servants with political loyalists vetted for ideological conformity
- Seize partisan control of DOJ, FBI, FTC, Department of Commerce
- Abolish the Department of Education
- Eliminate USAID (implemented via [[DOGE]])
- Curtail refugee admission, increase immigration enforcement using Palantir-adjacent data systems
- Remove "gender equality," "sexual orientation," and related language from all federal legislation
- Fund faith-based programs; defund LGBTQ+ programs
- Invoke Insurrection Act for domestic law enforcement and border enforcement
- Subordinate independent regulatory agencies to direct presidential control

Kevin Roberts at the RNC, July 2024: **"We are in the process of the second American Revolution, which will remain bloodless if the left allows it to be."**

## The Christian Nationalist Layer

Project 2025 is not a secular governance document with religious overtones. It is a **Dominionist policy framework** expressed in constitutional language.

The theological root is R.J. Rushdoony's **Reconstructionism** — the doctrine that all human institutions must be brought under biblical law. This evolved into **[[Dominionism]]** and the **[[Seven Mountains Mandate]]** — the belief that Christians must seize control of all seven spheres of societal influence: family, religion, education, media, arts/entertainment, business, and government. Project 2025 is the governmental mountain's implementation manual.

Specific markers in the text:
- Rights framed as "God-given" throughout — theological claim embedded in governance language
- "How much risk mitigation is worth the price of shutting down churches... What is the proper balance of lives saved versus souls saved?" (p. 453)
- HHS chapter (Roger Severino): "Families comprised of a married mother, father, and their children are the foundation of a well-ordered nation"
- Preamble commits to "deleting the terms sexual orientation and gender identity" from federal law
- Faith-based adoption agencies empowered to reject qualified parents on religious grounds
- Christian nationalism explicitly identified by Russ Vought's Center for Renewing America as a "top priority" for Trump's second term

This is not incidental. The theological goal is the displacement of secular democratic governance by Christian nationalist governance — using the state apparatus as the enforcement mechanism for Dominionist social order.

## The NRx Convergence

The Yarvin-to-Project 2025 pipeline is the same pipeline documented in the [[DOGE]] note. [[Schedule F]] is RAGE in policy form. The personnel database is the loyalty replacement apparatus Yarvin described. The framing differs — Yarvin uses secular CEO-king language, Project 2025 uses Christian nationalist language — but both arrive at the same structural outcome: the end of an accountable civil service, replaced by ideological loyalists.

These are **supply chain partners**, not ideological allies. Yarvin's network doesn't share the Christian nationalist theology. The Heritage Foundation doesn't share the monarchist framework. But they share the customer: an administrative state they both want destroyed, and a personnel apparatus they both want replaced.

## Implementation Status (as of early 2026)

Trump publicly disavowed Project 2025 during the 2024 campaign. Vought confirmed this was "just politics." By January 2026:

- Schedule F reinstated by executive order January 20, 2025
- 38+ Project 2025 authors and contributors placed in administration positions
- USAID dismantled via DOGE
- Personnel database actively used to staff agencies
- 180-Day Playbook implemented though kept from public release
- Center for Renewing America (Vought) drafted executive orders pre-inauguration

Trump embraced 64% of the 2016 Mandate's recommendations in his first term. The 2025 version was designed to close that gap.
