# Sandia National Laboratories

Lead design agent for the [[C-HGB]] — the Common Hypersonic Glide Body at the core of America's only operational hypersonic weapon system. Headquartered at Kirtland Air Force Base in Albuquerque, New Mexico, with a secondary facility at Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory in California.

Owned by the federal government, managed by National Technology and Engineering Solutions of Sandia, a Honeywell subsidiary. One of three research and development laboratories under the National Nuclear Security Administration.

Sandia designed the C-HGB guidance and control systems, navigation algorithms, and thermal protection architecture. The Cyber Threat Center developed the security-enhanced bootloader for the hypersonic weapon system. Dr. Scott Kowalchuk leads the Navigation, Guidance, and Control department responsible for C-HGB targeting software.

In February 2026, DOGE fired 177 NNSA employees — then reversed course, rehiring all but approximately 27. The firing gave DOGE access to Sandia personnel rosters at the precise moment the C-HGB program was transitioning to operational deployment.

[[William McCasland]] vanished from Albuquerque — home of Kirtland AFB and Sandia — on February 27, 2026.
