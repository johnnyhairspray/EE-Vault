# Los Alamos National Laboratory

Birthplace of the atomic bomb. One of three National Nuclear Security Administration laboratories, alongside [[Sandia National Laboratories]] and Lawrence Livermore. Located in Los Alamos, New Mexico — 35 miles northwest of Santa Fe.

Managed by Triad National Security LLC. Primary mission spans nuclear deterrence, nonproliferation, and national security science. Among the most sensitive research facilities in the United States.

Two personnel with LANL connections vanished in 2025: [[Melissa Casias]], an administrative assistant who disappeared June 26, 2025, and [[Anthony Chavez]], a former LANL employee who disappeared May 4, 2025. Neither has been found. No new information in either case as of early 2026.

New Mexico concentration: LANL, [[Sandia National Laboratories]] at Kirtland AFB, and [[William McCasland]]'s Albuquerque home form a tight geographic cluster — the second node of the defense aerospace research triangle.
