# LexID

Proprietary persistent identity key developed by LexisNexis Risk Solutions. Assigns a unique numeric identifier to individuals by linking disparate records across 84+ billion data points — maintaining coverage of approximately 98% of the U.S. adult population.

## How It Works

Unlike a Social Security Number (which is assigned and self-reported), a LexID is derived — computed from aggregated public and private records including addresses, credit data, utilities, court records, professional licenses, and any other data point linked to an individual. Because it is derived rather than assigned, it:

- **Survives changes in name** (marriage, legal name change, alias)
- **Survives changes in address** (moves, homelessness)
- **Survives changes in legal status** (arrest, expungement, naturalization)
- **Cannot be opted out of** — no mechanism exists to remove yourself from the LexID database

**Transitive linking:** LexID maps relationships not directly stated in records — shared historical addresses, family connections, employer affiliations, co-applicants on documents. A person with no criminal record can be linked to individuals who have records through shared historical addresses.

## The Government Deployment

LexID is the primary identity resolution layer for federal background checks and vetting operations. The DCSA (Defense Counterintelligence and Security Agency) $23.5M contract with LexisNexis Special Services (March 2026) uses LexID as the foundational identity anchor for federal personnel security clearances.

ImmigrationOS uses LexID to resolve fragmented immigration records into unified enforcement targets — connecting a person's various historical names, addresses, and identifiers into a single actionable profile.

**The March 2026 IDVerse integration:** LexisNexis integrated the IDVerse AI identity verification platform into the LexID framework, adding real-time biometric authentication and deepfake detection — anchoring the digital persistent key to a biometric "ground truth."

## The Coverage Problem

98% coverage of the U.S. adult population means approximately 2.6 million adults are not in the system. The inverse is the more significant statistic: approximately 260 million adults have a LexID profile they did not consent to, cannot access, and cannot remove themselves from — containing linkages to associates they may not know about.
