# Flock Safety Cameras

## 🔍 Summary
A pervasive network of AI-powered surveillance sensors that capture, catalog, and alert on vehicle and acoustic data. In 2026, Flock manages the largest fixed ALPR network in the U.S., with billions of monthly plate reads and a "Shared Network" that allows local, state, and federal agencies to "Hotlist" individuals across jurisdictional lines.

## 🧬 The "Detection" Piston

### 1. Vehicle Fingerprint® Technology
* **Beyond the Plate:** Flock cameras do not just read license plates; they use machine learning to catalog unique vehicle features (make, model, color, roof racks, bumper stickers, and damage).
* **FreeForm™ Search:** In 2026, investigators can search the entire national grid for a "red truck with a broken tail light" even if no plate was captured, essentially creating a searchable "Physical Identity" for vehicles.

### 2. Raven (Acoustic BIOS)
* **Distress Detection:** The **Raven** audio sensor has been upgraded in 2026. Beyond gunshot detection (90% accuracy), it now utilizes machine learning to alert police to "human distress" sounds, including screaming and tire screeches (Sideshow Detection).
* **Proximity Analytics:** When a "distress" event is triggered, Raven automatically "collides" the timestamp with nearby ALPR cameras to generate an immediate suspect list of every vehicle in the vicinity.

### 3. Condor & Drone Integration (DFR)
* **The Condor PTZ:** A solar-powered, LTE-connected pan-tilt-zoom camera used for live-streaming and 30-day evidence storage.
* **Drone as First Responder (DFR):** In 2026, FlockOS integrates directly with drone fleets. When a camera or Raven sensor triggers an alert, a drone is automatically dispatched to follow the "Fingerprinted" vehicle from 400ft, providing a continuous "eye in the sky" until ground extraction occurs.

## 🔗 The "Federal Extraction" Loop
* **The "Backdoor" Friction:** Despite official denials of direct federal contracts, 2026 reports (e.g., Mountain View, CA) revealed that Flock's "National Lookup" feature inadvertently shared local data with hundreds of out-of-state and federal agencies, including **HSI** and **CBP**.
* **Data Persistence:** While most images are deleted after 30 days, any "Hotlisted" vehicle data is extracted into permanent investigative files within **[[Palantir]]** or **[[Accurint]]**.
