# Dark Eagle (Long-Range Hypersonic Weapon / LRHW)

The U.S. Army's operational hypersonic weapons system. Delivers the [[C-HGB]] (Common Hypersonic Glide Body) via a two-stage booster to the edge of space, where the glide body detaches and maneuvers to its target at Mach 5+. Range: 2,775+ km (1,725+ miles).

**First full battery operational:** March 20, 2026. Declared at Joint Base Lewis-McChord, Washington. The Army announced this as the closure of the "hypersonic gap" — the period during which Russia and China fielded operational hypersonic weapons while the U.S. did not.

**Systems integrator:** [[Sandia National Laboratories]], the lead C-HGB design agent.

**Materials dependency:** The C-HGB's ability to survive atmospheric re-entry at Mach 5+ depends on [[Mondaloy]] — the nickel superalloy co-invented by [[Monica Reza]], who disappeared June 22, 2025. The funding authority for the Mondaloy program, [[William McCasland]], disappeared February 27, 2026 — one day before [[Operation Epic Fury]].

## The Companion System

The Navy's equivalent is the **Conventional Prompt Strike (CPS)** — the same [[C-HGB]] payload delivered from Zumwalt-class destroyers. See [[C-HGB]] for the technical specifications shared between both platforms.
