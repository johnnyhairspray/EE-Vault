# White Supremacy

The belief system holding that white people constitute a biologically and/or culturally superior group whose dominance of political, social, and economic institutions is natural, divinely ordained, or scientifically justified. It is the ideological root from which [[Great Replacement Theory]] derives its urgency, and the theological scaffolding from which [[Christian Reconstructionism]] derives its racial hierarchy.

This note documents white supremacy specifically as it operates within the networks mapped in this vault — through coded academic frameworks, policy infrastructure, and theological lineage — not as an enumeration of explicit hate groups.

---

## The Theological Lineage

[[R.J. Rushdoony]]'s 1973 *Institutes of Biblical Law* is the foundational text of [[Christian Reconstructionism]] and the theological root of the [[Seven Mountains Mandate]]. It is also a racial document. Rushdoony:

- Applied the "curse of Ham" theology historically used to justify chattel slavery
- Viewed the 13th, 14th, and 15th Amendments as constitutional disasters
- Argued that slavery was not inherently evil and that the Emancipation Proclamation was a "disaster for enslaved people"
- Called for Mosaic Law enforcement including death penalties that would apply disproportionately along racial, sexual orientation, and religious lines

This is not incidental to his theology. It is structural. The Christian nation he envisioned was explicitly a racially and sexually ordered hierarchy governed by biblical law. The Americans United documentation of Project 2025's Confederate constitutional parallels traces directly to this lineage — both invoke God-given rights in the service of a racially tiered social order.

## The NRx Pseudo-Scientific Layer

White supremacy's most dangerous contemporary form is not the explicit variety. It is the academic-aesthetic version circulating in the NRx / Dark Enlightenment network that provides intellectual legitimacy to the same racial hierarchy claims while avoiding the PR liabilities of explicit white nationalism.

**[[Curtis Yarvin]]**: Has written that white people "on average, have higher IQs than Black people." Opposes U.S. civil rights programs. Has proposed "private welfare and prison reforms that resembled slavery." Historian Joshua Tait summarized his contradictions: advocates hierarchy while resenting cultural elites; speaks the language of monarchy while appealing to libertarianism. The racial claims are not peripheral to his project — they undergird the legitimacy of meritocratic sorting in the neocameralist patchwork, where exit replaces political voice and capital determines belonging.

**[[Nick Land]]**: Has written explicitly on what he called **"hyper-racism"** — an embrace of scientific racism and eugenic thinking within his broader accelerationist framework. Has posted race-science adjacent content and white ethnostate ideology on X. Like Yarvin, frames racial hierarchy in terms of IQ distribution and evolutionary fitness rather than explicit supremacist language — the effect is identical, the aesthetics are academic.

The structural function of the pseudo-scientific layer: it makes white supremacy feel *rational* to technically-minded audiences rather than merely tribal. When demographic dominance can be justified through bell curves and exit rights rather than burning crosses, it becomes legible to Silicon Valley.
