# Great Replacement Theory

The conspiracy theory — derived from [[White Supremacy|white supremacist]] ideology — that white people are being deliberately and systematically replaced by non-white immigrants through a coordinated elite agenda, with the ultimate goal of eliminating white political, cultural, and demographic dominance. In its terrorist form it has animated multiple mass casualty attacks. In its soft political form it circulates through mainstream conservative media, White House policy memos, and [[Seven Mountains Mandate|NAR theology]] as demographic panic dressed in the language of national sovereignty and Christian civilization.

---

## The Texts

**Jean Raspail's *The Camp of the Saints*** (1973, France) — a novel depicting the violent "invasion" of France and the collapse of white civilization by a flotilla of South Asian migrants. Widely considered the founding fiction of the Great Replacement worldview. The book's framing — demographic overwhelm as civilizational death — became the template for white nationalist demographic panic globally.

**Renaud Camus's *Le Grand Remplacement*** (2011, France) — coined the phrase "Great Replacement" as a political term. Camus argued that French and European elites were deliberately facilitating mass immigration to replace the white European population. The phrase spread globally through alt-right networks from 2015 onward.

## The U.S. Operational Channel

**[[Stephen Miller]]** is the documented link between Great Replacement theory and active federal immigration policy.

In 2015-2016, while shaping Trump's immigration platform as a Jeff Sessions staffer and then Trump campaign adviser, Miller sent 900+ emails to Breitbart editors documented by the SPLC's Hatewatch. Specific Great Replacement content:

- Recommended Raspail's *Camp of the Saints* to Breitbart: *"Someone should point out the parallels to real life."* Breitbart's Julia Hahn ran the story; Steve Bannon subsequently called the book "prophetic" about *"the invasion at the border"*
- Shared articles from **VDARE** — a website that traffics explicitly in the Great Replacement / white genocide conspiracy — directing Breitbart editors to source from it
- Lionized the **Immigration Act of 1924**, a eugenics-based law praised by Adolf Hitler, for its restriction of "replacement" immigration from Southern and Eastern Europe, Asia, and Africa
- Referenced Calvin Coolidge's immigration restriction approvingly: *"Like Coolidge did"*

More than **80% of Miller's reviewed emails** relate to race or immigration.

Historian Kathleen Belew: *"This is clear evidence that this is a person who is immersed in trafficking in white nationalist ideology... this is a deliberate and intentional attempt to propagate that kind of a system."*

**[[Steve Bannon]]**: *Camp of the Saints* became a reference point in Bannon's framing of the border as "invasion" — language that migrated from the novel directly into Trump's political messaging and eventually into official White House communications.

## The Media Mainstreaming

**Tucker Carlson** was the primary mass delivery mechanism for Great Replacement theory to mainstream American audiences during his Fox News tenure (2016-2023). Carlson broadcast replacement theory content in varying degrees of explicitness hundreds of times, including explicit statements that Democrats support immigration in order to "replace" existing American voters. The Anti-Defamation League called for his firing in 2021; Fox News defended him. He was terminated in April 2023.

The NAR network wraps Great Replacement in theological language: demographic change as **demonic spiritual warfare** against the Christian nation. [[Lance Wallnau]] and the broader [[Seven Mountains Mandate]] movement frame immigration not in racial terms explicitly but in spiritual ones — the incoming demographic is a vector of demonic influence that must be spiritually and politically resisted. The policy implications are identical to secular replacement theory; the permission structure is theological rather than biological.

## The Terrorist Manifestos

Great Replacement theory has been cited explicitly in the manifestos of:

- **Christchurch mosque shootings** (New Zealand, March 2019) — 51 killed. Manifesto titled *The Great Replacement*; shooter livestreamed attack
- **El Paso Walmart shooting** (Texas, August 2019) — 22 killed, targeting Latino shoppers. Manifesto cited the "Hispanic invasion of Texas" and demographic replacement
- **Pittsburgh Tree of Life synagogue shooting** (Pennsylvania, October 2018) — 11 killed. Shooter believed Jews were orchestrating replacement through refugee resettlement
- **Buffalo Tops supermarket shooting** (New York, May 2022) — 10 killed, targeting Black shoppers. 180-page manifesto cited replacement theory extensively

The ideological chain: Raspail (fiction) → Camus (political theory) → VDARE/Breitbart (media distribution) → Miller (policy) → Carlson (mass media) → individual actors (terrorism).

## The Soft Political Form

In mainstream conservative politics, Great Replacement operates without the explicit language:

- *"Invasion"* at the southern border
- *"They're replacing us"* / *"replacement of American voters"*
- *"Heritage Americans"* — terminology that emerged in TPUSA circles after Charlie Kirk's assassination, used to challenge non-Christian conservatives like Ramaswamy on whether they are "real Americans"
- *"Demographic winter"* — the birth-rate panic version: white birth rates declining = civilizational collapse, used to justify abortion bans as demographic policy
- The [[Project 2025]] framing of immigration enforcement as civilizational defense; its personnel database screening for "rock-solid conservatives" as a demographic filtering mechanism

## The Theological Overlay

[[Christian Zionism / Eschatological Accelerationism|Christian Zionist]] and [[Seven Mountains Mandate|NAR]] theology provides a parallel permission structure: demographic change is not just a racial threat — it is a **spiritual invasion**. Immigrants bring demonic cultural influence. Islam is explicitly identified as an enemy force in NAR spiritual warfare doctrine. The border is a spiritual battlefield.

This framing is more politically durable than explicit replacement theory because it is immune to racism charges — it is framed as spiritual discernment, not racial animus. The policy outputs are identical.
