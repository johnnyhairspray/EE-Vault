# Dominionism

Christian theological-political framework holding that Christians are called by God to exercise dominion over all earthly institutions — government, law, education, media, arts, business, and family — bringing them into alignment with biblical principles. Derived from the Genesis mandate to have "dominion" over the earth.

Not a single unified movement. An umbrella covering several related but distinct strains:

## Reconstructionism (Theonomy)

Founded by R.J. Rushdoony in the 1960s-70s. The most explicitly theocratic form: advocates replacing civil law with Mosaic law (Old Testament legal code), including the death penalty for adultery, homosexuality, apostasy, and blasphemy. Rushdoony's *Institutes of Biblical Law* (1973) is the foundational text.

Rushdoony is also the intellectual ancestor of modern Christian homeschooling as a strategy for raising a generation outside secular influence — specifically to eventually take political power. His influence on the Heritage Foundation's education policy agenda is documented.

## The Seven Mountains Mandate

The contemporary and politically operational form of Dominionism. Developed by Bill Bright (Campus Crusade for Christ) and Loren Cunningham (Youth With a Mission) in 1975 — the same year they claim to have both independently received the same "revelation" from God. The "seven mountains" are the seven spheres of cultural influence: family, religion, education, media, arts/entertainment, business, and government.

Christians are called to "take the mountain" — achieve dominance — in each sphere. The political mountain is the most explicitly contested. See [[Seven Mountains Mandate]] for the operational implementation.

## The Project 2025 Connection

Project 2025's governing philosophy is Dominionism expressed in constitutional language. The document frames rights as "God-given" throughout, calls for eliminating gender equality and LGBTQ+ protections from federal law, empowers faith-based institutions to apply religious criteria in taxpayer-funded programs, and frames the state as an instrument of moral order rather than secular governance.

Roger Severino's HHS chapter: *"Families comprised of a married mother, father, and their children are the foundation of a well-ordered nation."* This is Dominionist family theology, not secular policy analysis.

## The NRx Convergence

The intellectual convergence between Dominionism and the NRx (Dark Enlightenment) network is not ideological — it is structural. Both frameworks arrive at post-democratic hierarchical governance through different routes. Dominionism arrives via theology (God's law supersedes democratic majorities). NRx arrives via neocameralism (shareholder sovereignty supersedes democratic majorities). The Heritage Foundation serves as the institutional node where both tendencies share a goal: destroying liberal democratic governance.
