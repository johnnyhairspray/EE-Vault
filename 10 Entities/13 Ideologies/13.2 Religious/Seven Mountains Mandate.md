# Seven Mountains Mandate

Dominionist conservative Christian ideology holding that believers are divinely mandated to seize control of seven key spheres of society: **Family, Religion, Education, Media, Arts & Entertainment, Business, and Government**. Whoever controls these mountains controls the direction of civilization. The goal is not coexistence or influence — it is **dominion**.

A 2024 poll found 41% of American Christians believe in 7MM. It is no longer a fringe theology.

## Origins and Lineage

**1973:** R.J. Rushdoony publishes *The Institutes of Biblical Law* — the foundational text of Christian Reconstructionism. Argues all human institutions must be brought under biblical law. Advocates theocracy, Mosaic Law enforcement, death penalty for homosexuality, rejection of the 13th/14th/15th Amendments. Rushdoony is the intellectual grandfather of everything that follows.

**1975:** Bill Bright (Campus Crusade for Christ) and Loren Cunningham (Youth With a Mission) independently receive what they describe as divine visions of "seven spheres" of societal influence that Christians must transform.

**~2005:** Lance Wallnau reframes "seven spheres" as **"seven mountains"** — a militarized metaphor shift. Mountains are not spheres to influence. Mountains are **territory to be conquered**. Through Wallnau's connection to C. Peter Wagner's [[New Apostolic Reformation]] network, 7MM goes viral globally.

**2008:** C. Peter Wagner publishes *Dominion!* — formally endorsing 7MM as the operational strategy of NAR: *"Our theological bedrock is what has been known as Dominion Theology. This means that our divine mandate is to do whatever is necessary, by the power of the Holy Spirit, to retake the dominion of God's creation which Adam forfeited to Satan."*

**2013:** Wallnau and Bill Johnson publish *Invading Babylon: The 7 Mountain Mandate* — the movement's primary contemporary text.

## The Theology

7MM is a **postmillennialist** framework — unlike Rapture-focused dispensationalism, it holds that Christians must *build* God's kingdom on earth *before* Christ returns. The Second Coming is preceded by Christian dominion, not preceded by tribulation and escape.

This is theologically and politically significant: it removes the passive waiting inherent in Rapture theology and replaces it with **militant, political obligation**. God won't fix it. Christians must fix it — by seizing power.

The "mountains" are not metaphors. They are literal battlegrounds between divine truth and what NAR theology identifies as demonic forces. Every institution not under Christian control is, in this framework, under satanic control. This is the permission structure for total culture war without compromise.

The [[New Apostolic Reformation]] adds a further layer: it holds that God is currently speaking through living apostles and prophets who receive direct divine revelation. This creates an authority structure entirely outside democratic or institutional accountability — the apostle speaks for God; the apostle's word overrides civil law, democratic consensus, and traditional denominational authority.

## The Trump Connection

Trump is irrelevant to the theology. He is a vessel — what NAR calls a "Cyrus figure," after the pagan Persian king who served God's purposes without being a believer. Wallnau explicitly framed Trump this way in 2016 and has maintained it. The theology does not require the vessel to be holy. It requires the vessel to be useful.

What Trump provided: access to the government mountain. NAR delivered a disciplined voting bloc. The transaction is documented and explicit.

**Paula White** — Trump's personal spiritual adviser, NAR leader — appointed head of the White House Faith Office in both terms. Prayed publicly for "every demonic network aligned against the purpose, against the calling of President Trump" to be destroyed. Leads the National Faith Advisory Board in Trump's second term.

**Mike Johnson** — Speaker of the House. Aligned with 7MM theology. Part of a church connected to Douglas Wilson's Christian Reconstructionist network.

**Russ Vought** — Center for Renewing America, Project 2025 architect. Explicitly identified Christian nationalism as a top priority. [[Project 2025]] is, in Dominionist terms, the policy implementation of the government mountain conquest.

**Pete Hegseth** — Secretary of War. Member of a church connected to Douglas Wilson.

**Charlie Kirk** — Turning Point USA founder. Assassinated September 10, 2025. Kirk was the movement's operational heir — the organizer who transformed 7MM from a theological movement into a mass political machine with campus chapters, media operations, and direct White House access. At 2020 CPAC: *"Finally we have a president that understands the seven mountains of cultural influence."* Kirk admitted he wants to use "state power" to put Christianity in government.

## The Project 2025 Bridge

[[Project 2025]] is the government mountain's implementation document. The personnel database is the staffing mechanism. [[Schedule F]] is the enforcement tool. The theological goal — Christian governance of the state — and the policy mechanism — civil service replacement with ideological loyalists — are the same operation expressed in different languages.

[[Russ Vought]]'s Center for Renewing America drafted both Project 2025 chapters and pre-inauguration executive orders. The National Christian Foundation gave $134M to 32 Project 2025 organizations between 2016-2023.

## The NRx Convergence — Again

7MM and [[Neocameralism]] arrive at structurally identical governance outcomes through opposite theological routes. Yarvin wants a CEO-king through secular logic. The NAR wants a Cyrus-king through divine mandate. Both produce the same thing: an unaccountable executive, a dismantled administrative state, and loyalty replacing merit as the organizing principle of governance.

Neither needs the other. Both feed the same customer.
