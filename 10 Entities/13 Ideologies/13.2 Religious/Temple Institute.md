# Temple Institute (Third Temple Preparation Infrastructure)

Founded 1987 by Rabbi Yisrael Ariel in Jerusalem's Old City Jewish Quarter. Mission statement: rebuild the Holy Temple on Temple Mount. Not a fringe cult — a legally registered Israeli institution with government ministry funding, diplomatic airport ceremonies, and a $3M gold menorah displayed publicly in Jerusalem's Jewish Quarter. The infrastructure of eschatological preparation is operational and partially state-funded.

## The Gold Furniture

The Temple Institute has completed over **60 sacred vessels** built to precise biblical specifications using authentic materials — not replicas. According to the Institute, these are *"fit and ready for use in the service of the Holy Temple."*

Completed items on display at the Holy Temple Museum, Old City:

- **The Golden Menorah** — seven-branched candelabra, 43 kilograms of 24-karat gold electroplated onto bronze, approximately 1.8 meters tall. Value estimated at $3 million. Displayed publicly in the central square of the Jewish Quarter, overlooking Temple Mount. Funded by Ukrainian Jewish community leader Vadim Rabinovitch. Designed by craftsman Chaim Odem after years of halakhic research.
- **The Golden Incense Altar (Mizbach HaZahav)** — wood overlaid with pure gold, completed to Exodus specifications
- **The Table of Showbread (Shulchan)** — wood overlaid with gold
- **Priestly garments** — complete set including the Kohen Gadol (High Priest) breastplate with twelve gemstones representing the twelve tribes, investigated by professional gemologists across 30+ sources
- **Gold and silver-plated shofars**, silver trumpets, lyre, harp
- **All cleaning, incense, and sacrificial service instruments**
- **A portable stone altar** — scaled-down, transportable, built to Torah law specifications, described by the Institute as "completely ready for use on the Temple Mount"
- **Architectural blueprints** for the full Third Temple structure, reconciling ancient mandates with modern building codes

The sole obstacle stated explicitly by the Institute: the Dome of the Rock and Al-Aqsa Mosque currently occupy the site.

## The Red Heifers

The parah adumah — Numbers 19 — requires the ashes of a perfectly red, unblemished heifer never yoked, bred, or milked, burned and mixed with spring water, to purify priests and the temple site from contact with death. Without this purification, temple service cannot begin. Jewish tradition holds only nine valid red heifers have existed in history; the tenth is expected in the Messianic era.

**The Texas Import:**

September 2022: Five red yearling heifers were transported from a Texas ranch to Israel. The farmer, Byron Stinson, a devout evangelical Christian associated with the organization **Boneh Israel** (a U.S.-Israel evangelical bridge group), raised them deliberately for this purpose after years of selective breeding: *"The prophecies came true, and the Jews are back in Israel. Now they need to build a Temple. The red heifer is the key."* Purchase and transport cost: **$700,000**. Transportation method: flown as "pets" aboard an American Airlines Boeing 777 to circumvent Israeli restrictions on livestock imports from non-approved countries.

**Government Involvement:**

The Israeli Ministry of Agriculture allegedly bypassed standard import regulations to enable the transfer. The Ministry of Jerusalem Affairs and Heritage sent its Director General, Netanel Isaac, to deliver a speech at the airport welcome ceremony — and disclosed the ministry had been **funding development of the Mount of Olives site** where the heifer ritual is planned. The Ministry later transferred the heifers to a visitor center and farm at Tel Shiloh archaeological site in the occupied West Bank. The land for the planned Mount of Olives ritual site was purchased in 2011 by Rabbi Tzachi Mamo — land originally owned by Palestinians, registered under an unknown company.

**Hamas took the heifers seriously.** One hundred days after October 7, a Hamas spokesman delivered a speech explicitly objecting to Israel's possession of the red heifers as an act of aggression — framing the cattle as material preparation to destroy Al-Aqsa.

**The Disqualification:**

As the heifers matured, they developed blemishes and non-red hairs. By late 2024: only two remained preliminarily qualified. By mid-2025: **all five were officially disqualified** by the Temple Institute. The Institute's Facebook post acknowledged they "can't always be fully transparent" about the animals' condition. The four remaining cattle entered Israel's red heifer breeding program; their genetics described as "99% pure."

**The Contested Ceremony:**

On July 1, 2025, Byron Stinson released video of what he described as a legitimate red heifer ceremony conducted "with a Cohen, a priest" in Samaria — not on the Mount of Olives. The Temple Institute disputed the ceremony's validity: the Mishnah specifies the Mount of Olives as the required site, and rabbinical consensus holds that a non-Mount of Olives ceremony cannot be authoritative. Reports circulated that ashes from the Samaria ceremony were retained. Whether this was a practice run, an unauthorized ritual, or something more significant remains unresolved. The Temple Institute has not confirmed. The search continues.

## IDF Soldier Patches

Beginning in 2023-2024, a **widespread phenomenon** emerged of Israeli soldiers attaching Third Temple and messianic patches to combat uniforms and equipment. This is documented, photographed, and officially addressed — not disputed.

**The Patches:**

- **High On The Har / Mikdash Market** — an Israeli organization running the "Temple Patch Project," distributing velcro patches depicting the Third Temple (Beit HaMikdash) with the Hebrew inscription **"בקרוב בימינו" — "Soon in our days"**. Over **15,000 patches distributed free to IDF soldiers** on the front lines. For every patch purchased commercially, ten are sent to combat soldiers.
- **Messiah (Moshiach) patches** — a crown with "Messiah" in Hebrew, associated with Chabad-Lubavitch messianic Zionism and the radical Hilltop Youth settler movement
- **Greater Israel patches** — maps showing Israeli territory extending beyond current borders

**Times of Israel, February 2, 2024** — photographed an IDF soldier outside Gaza City with patches depicting the Holy Temple, text reading "Soon in our days." The IDF notably did not blur the patches in the released image — a departure from past practice.

**October 29, 2025** — social media posts showed IDF soldiers displaying Third Temple flags inside Gaza.

**The IDF Response:**

April 2024: IDF published new guidelines banning non-military symbols on uniforms after the phenomenon became widespread. October 2024: Chief of Staff Lt.-Gen. Herzi Halevi personally visited Golani Brigade combat forces in Lebanon, encountered a soldier wearing a Messiah patch, **physically removed it from the soldier's uniform** and placed it in the soldier's pocket: *"Only military insignia on the uniform."* The symbols persist regardless. The IDF cannot regulate conviction.
