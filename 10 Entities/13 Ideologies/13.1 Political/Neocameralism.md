# Neocameralism

## 🔍 Summary
The belief that a state should be run exactly like a joint-stock corporation. In this model, the "Citizens" are customers or employees, and the "Sovereign" is a CEO with absolute authority to maximize the "value" (order/profit) of the territory.

---

### 🧬 The "Convergence" Logic
1. **The CEO-Sovereign:** Directly informs the 2026 view of the Presidency as an "at-will" executive office. This is the philosophical "BIOS" behind **[[Schedule Policy-Career]]**, where government employees are treated as replaceable corporate staff.
2. **The Sovcorp Rail:** Proposes that "Patches" of land should be owned by **Sovcorps** (Sovereign Corporations). This is manifesting in 2026 through the "Company Towns" being built around **[[The Stargate Project]]** hubs, where the tech provider (Oracle/OpenAI) provides the security and law.
3. **The "Voice vs. Exit" Piston:** Yarvin argues that "democracy" (voice) is inefficient. Instead, users should have "Exit"—the right to leave a Sovcorp if they don't like the service. In 2026, this justifies the "Extraction" of those who don't align with the corporate state.
