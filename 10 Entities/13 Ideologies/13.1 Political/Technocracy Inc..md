# Technocracy Inc.

American political movement founded 1933 by Howard Scott and M. King Hubbert. Proposed replacing democratic government and the price system with a "Technical Alliance" of engineers and scientists managing continental resources through energy accounting (Energy Certificates) rather than money.

## The Core Ideology

Technocracy's foundational argument: the price system (market capitalism) is irrational because it does not accurately account for the energy required to produce goods and services. Democratic politics is corrupt and inefficient because elected officials lack technical expertise. The solution: governance by a scientific and technical elite who would manage the continental resource base through rationalized production and distribution.

Key tenets:
- Replace money with "Energy Certificates" — a non-accumulating currency tied to energy production
- Govern through a "Technical Alliance" of engineers, not elected officials
- Abolish political parties and representative democracy
- Define the governed territory by its natural resource boundaries rather than political borders

## The Technate Map

Technocracy Inc. produced a detailed "Technate of America" map in the 1930s defining the optimal continental territory for its proposed governance system. The territory was defined by its natural resource boundaries and included: the contiguous United States, Canada, Alaska, Mexico, Central America, and Greenland.

This map — produced in 1937 — matches territory-for-territory the lands Trump's expansionism claims in 2025–2026: Canada as 51st state, Greenland acquisition (with force not ruled out), Panama Canal, and Mexico border zone. The justification in Technocracy Inc.'s 1937 literature is nearly identical to the Trump administration's 2025 framing: *"The natural resources and natural boundaries of this area make it an independent, self-sustaining geographical unit."*

## Joshua Haldeman and the Canadian Network

[[Joshua Haldeman]] — Elon Musk's maternal grandfather — was national director of Technocracy Inc. in Canada from 1936 to 1941. He was arrested by the Royal Canadian Mounted Police in 1940 under the Defence of Canada Regulations, with the government arguing that Technocracy Inc.'s anti-democratic agenda was incompatible with Canada's war effort. The charges were dropped after a public campaign by Haldeman and other members.

Haldeman organized Technocracy chapters across Canada and wrote extensively about the movement's goals before emigrating to South Africa in 1950.

## The NRx Parallel

Technocracy Inc. and the NRx/neocameralist tradition documented in this vault arrive at nearly identical governance conclusions through different intellectual routes:

- **Technocracy:** Replace democratic politics with technical expertise; govern the continental resource base through a scientific alliance
- **Neocameralism (Yarvin):** Replace democratic governance with a corporate CEO-king; treat the state as a joint-stock corporation owned by its shareholders

Both eliminate democratic accountability. Both concentrate governance in a technical/managerial elite. Both arrived at anti-democratic conclusions before the internet era. The convergence in the current Trump administration — DOGE as mass civil service purge, Stargate as continental compute infrastructure, territorial expansionism matching the 1937 Technate map — is the contemporary manifestation of both traditions running in parallel.
