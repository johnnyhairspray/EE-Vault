# Antifa

Decentralized anti-fascist movement with no formal membership, hierarchy, or organizational structure. Rooted in the anti-fascist movements of 1930s Europe — specifically the German *Antifaschistische Aktion* formed in 1932. Contemporary U.S. usage refers broadly to individuals and loosely organized groups who engage in counter-protest and direct action against far-right and fascist organizing.

**⚠️ Epistemic note:** "Antifa" is not an organization. It has no members, no leadership, no treasury, no bylaws, and no centralized communications. Treating it as a unified organization misrepresents its actual structure. The Trump administration's designation nonetheless treats it as one.

## The September 2025 Executive Order

On September 22, 2025, Trump signed an executive order designating Antifa as a domestic terrorist organization. The order characterized Antifa as a "militarist, anarchist enterprise" and directed federal agencies to investigate, disrupt, and dismantle its operations and funding sources.

Legal scholars immediately noted the structural problem: the existing domestic terrorism statute (18 U.S.C. § 2331) has no formal "designation" mechanism equivalent to the State Department's foreign terrorist organization list. There is no legal process by which a loosely affiliated domestic ideological movement without centralized structure can be formally "designated." The executive order creates a political label without a legal framework — but that label then filters into enforcement decisions made by FBI, DHS, and DOJ.

## The NSPM-7 Mechanism

[[NSPM-7]] (September 2025) operationalizes the designation by expanding the domestic terrorism priority list to include:

- Organized campaigns of targeted intimidation
- Doxing
- Civil disorder
- **Anti-capitalism as an ideology** — the document explicitly flags anti-capitalist sentiment as a terrorism indicator

The "indicia" rule: under NSPM-7 guidance, FBI and DOJ field offices can flag individuals based on "ideological markers" — protest attendance, anti-capitalist rhetoric, specific attire — to populate federal watchlists including Sparta, Reaper, and Grapevine (ICE watchlists documented by Ken Klippenstein, January 2026).

## The First Amendment Problem

The designation creates a mechanism by which ideological belief and protest participation can be treated as "material support" for a terrorist enterprise — even though the First Amendment explicitly protects political association and expression.

The enforcement gap between the legal scholars' objection and the operational reality: FBI agents and ICE officers making individual enforcement decisions will apply the designation label regardless of its legal ambiguity. The label changes enforcement behavior without requiring a court to validate it.
