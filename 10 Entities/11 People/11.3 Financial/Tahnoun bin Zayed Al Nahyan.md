# Tahnoun bin Zayed Al Nahyan

Born December 4, 1968. Brother of UAE President Mohamed bin Zayed Al Nahyan. UAE National Security Advisor since 2016. Simultaneously manages a **$1.3 trillion business portfolio** operating as the investment arm of Abu Dhabi's national security apparatus. Known in Western intelligence circles as the "spy sheikh."

TIME100 AI 2025.

## The Portfolio

**[[G42]]** — Abu Dhabi AI conglomerate. Documented historical ties to Huawei and Chinese technology firms. Under Biden, blocked from acquiring advanced U.S. AI chips over concerns technology would be diverted to China. Under Trump, restrictions reversed after Tahnoun's WLF investment.

**[[MGX]]** — Abu Dhabi state-backed investment vehicle. Used as financial instrument for the USD1/Binance transaction ($2B in May 2025).

**[[World Liberty Financial]]** — Via shell vehicle Aryam Investment 1, Tahnoun's interests purchased **49% of WLF for $500 million**, four days before Trump's January 2025 inauguration. Deal never publicly disclosed. His lieutenants Martin Edelman (G42 general counsel) and Peng Xiao (G42 CEO) were placed on WLF's board without disclosure. $187 million of the upfront payment flowed directly to Trump family entities; at least $31 million to Witkoff family entities.

**Abu Dhabi Developmental Holding Company (ADQ)** — chairs this sovereign development fund. Linked to a smear campaign against Paris Saint-Germain/Nasser Al-Khelaifi, amplified via Altice France (Patrick Drahi).

**International Resources Holding (IRH)** — unit of IHC. Acquired Zambia's Mopani copper mine. UN investigators allege IRH had meetings with gold smugglers from Democratic Republic of Congo. Most smuggled gold reportedly ends up in UAE.

## Key Actions

### Pre-Inauguration WLF Buy-In (January 16, 2025)
Four days before Trump's inauguration, Aryam Investment 1 signed a contract with Eric Trump to purchase 49% of WLF for $500 million. $250M upfront; $187M to Trump entities; $31M+ to Witkoff entities. Edelman and Xiao placed on WLF board. Deal concealed from public. Trump later claimed: *"I know nothing about it."*

### Binance/USD1 Play (May 2025)
MGX used $2B of USD1 to finance investment in Binance — establishing USD1 as institutional settlement currency ($5B+ market cap, ~$80M/year in reserve returns for WLF). Shortly after: Trump pardoned Binance founder Changpeng Zhao, who had been serving a prison sentence for money laundering compliance failures. Congressional investigators demanded documentation on whether WLF personnel were involved in discussions preceding the pardon.

### AI Chip Deal (May–November 2025)
Following the WLF investment, the Trump administration reversed Biden-era chip restrictions and approved sales equivalent to 35,000 Nvidia GB300 processors to G42 in November 2025, with a pledge of 500,000 advanced AI chips annually — enough to build one of the world's largest AI data center networks. Multiple NSC officials raised national security concerns about G42's Chinese ties. Trump fired 6 NSC officials in April 2025. David Sacks completed the deal over remaining objections.

### White House Dinner (March 18, 2025)
Trump hosted Tahnoun for a banquet dinner. Tahnoun met with the CIA director, Treasury Secretary, Commerce Secretary, and tech CEOs including Jeff Bezos. Leaked closed-door agenda:
- Shield UAE from international sanctions and ICJ investigation into alleged support for Sudan's RSF militia
- Back Israeli airstrikes in Syria aimed at regional destabilization
- **Facilitate plans to transform Gaza into an Emirati-controlled economic zone under the guise of reconstruction**
- Secure AI chip access

### Board of Peace (January 2026)
Abu Dhabi embedded as capital member. USD1 — 49% owned by Tahnoun — designated as Board of Peace payment instrument for Gaza reconstruction. Steve Witkoff, co-founder of WLF and Tahnoun's business partner, is simultaneously Trump's Special Envoy to the Middle East and a BoP Executive Board member.

## The Emoluments Problem

Legal experts have described the Tahnoun/WLF transaction as a potential violation of the Foreign Emoluments Clause, which prohibits the president from receiving payments from foreign governments without congressional consent. The White House defense: Trump's assets are in a blind trust managed by his children. The trustee is Eric Trump — who signed the deal.

Senator Elizabeth Warren and others called for formal investigations. House committee demanded records by March 1, 2026. WLF has not complied.
