# Jared Kushner

Born January 10, 1981. Son-in-law of Donald Trump (married Ivanka Trump 2009). Harvard BA 2003, NYU JD 2007. Owner, Kushner Companies (inherited from father Charles Kushner, convicted of tax evasion, witness tampering, and illegal campaign contributions). Senior White House Advisor, Trump's first term (2017–2021). Founder and sole owner of **Affinity Partners** (2021). Unpaid volunteer advisor and informal envoy in Trump's second term, simultaneously running a private equity firm dependent on the goodwill of the same governments he negotiates with.

Net worth: ~$900M+ (up 180% from $320M when he entered the White House in 2017).

## Affinity Partners: The Structure

**Affinity Partners** is a Miami-based private equity firm established January 2021 — immediately after Trump's first term ended. Kushner had no prior private equity experience. His most significant prior business experience was nearly bankrupting Kushner Companies by purchasing 666 Fifth Avenue for $1.8 billion in 2007, a deal that required a bailout.

**Capital raised:** By March 2026, Affinity manages **$4.8 billion AUM** after a Gulf cash injection.

**Where the money comes from:**
- **Saudi Public Investment Fund (PIF):** $2 billion — by far the largest investor. The PIF's own screening committee recommended rejection, citing "inexperience of the Affinity Fund management," "excessive fees," and "public relations risks." Crown Prince Mohammed bin Salman personally overruled the committee.
- **UAE sovereign wealth:** Hundreds of millions
- **Qatar Investment Authority:** Hundreds of millions

**What investors receive:** Nothing. Through July 2024, Affinity had invested only ~$1.1B of the capital raised. The fund had returned **zero profits** to investors.

**What Kushner receives:**
- Saudi Arabia pays 1.25% annually in management fees regardless of performance
- Other investors pay 2% annually
- Total management fees collected: **$157.5 million** (including $87M from Saudi Arabia alone) through 2024
- Scheduled to collect another $90M through 2026

**Senator Wyden's conclusion:** "Affinity's investors may not be motivated by commercial considerations, but rather the opportunity to funnel foreign government money to members of President Trump's family." The Senate Finance Committee referred the matter to the Attorney General, suggesting Kushner may be operating as **an unregistered foreign agent of Saudi Arabia** under FARA — using an investment firm loophole that bypasses normal disclosure requirements.

## Non-Obvious Connections

### The Electronic Arts Play (September 2025)
Affinity Partners led the consortium acquiring **Electronic Arts** in a **$52.5 billion leveraged buyout** — the largest LBO on record. Partners: Saudi PIF (majority control) and Silver Lake. Affinity holds only ~5% equity despite brokering the deal.

The vault angle: EA has **150+ million monthly active users**, predominantly younger demographics, with extensive behavioral data and in-app purchase patterns. Saudi Arabia's PIF acquiring majority control of a gaming platform this large — with Kushner providing political cover to bypass CFIUS review — represents sovereign capture of a major youth-facing data and cultural infrastructure asset. Kushner's involvement is documented as specifically designed to "ease the deal's path through CFIUS." His 5% stake generates fees; Saudi Arabia gets the asset.

Contrast: TikTok was subjected to years of national security scrutiny and forced divestiture for Chinese ownership of a social platform. EA faces no comparable review under Saudi majority ownership because Kushner's presence implies administration approval.

### Brain Co. (September 2025)
Affinity Partners **incubated Brain Co.** with investor Elad Gil. Co-founders include **Luis Videgaray** — former Mexican Foreign Minister — and Eric Wu of Opendoor. San Francisco-based startup targeting large businesses and governments for AI deployment.

The vault angle: Kushner — already operating as a conduit between Gulf sovereign wealth and US assets — is now incubating an AI company that sells to governments, co-founded with a former foreign minister. The overlap between Gulf capital, government AI deployment, and the surveillance infrastructure layer is direct.

### Albania and Serbia Real Estate
Affinity Partners has committed **over $1 billion** to real estate developments in Albania and Serbia — both countries where the government controls permitting, licensing, and taxation over Kushner's projects.

In Albania, the government granted Kushner development rights to a stretch of **Mediterranean coastline** that an Albanian family claims was unjustly taken from them after the fall of the Soviet Union. A Cold War-era military island was also granted for development.

The structure creates permanent leverage: Kushner's fund survives only if the Albanian and Serbian governments remain cooperative. He is financially dependent on foreign governments he has no leverage over — exactly the posture FARA is designed to flag.

### The Russia-Ukraine Connection
Kushner served alongside Witkoff in Iran negotiations prior to Operation Epic Fury and has been cited as co-leading Middle East diplomacy during Trump's second term. Congressional investigators noted Kushner "led backroom talks" on multiple geopolitical fronts while simultaneously running Affinity's fundraising operations with the same governments.

### The WLF/Board of Peace Thread
Kushner is an Executive Board member of the Board of Peace — the same body that uses USD1 (49% owned by Tahnoun bin Zayed) as its payment instrument for Gaza reconstruction. Kushner negotiated the Abraham Accords during Trump's first term, establishing the UAE/Israel normalization that the Board of Peace now operationalizes. He has stated his goal is to open an "investment corridor between Saudi Arabia and Israel." The BoP is that corridor, with Kushner at the table and Gulf sovereign capital as the fuel.
