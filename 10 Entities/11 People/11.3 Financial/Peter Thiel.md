# Peter Thiel

Born: 10/11/1967
Died: NA

German-American venture capitalist, co-founder of [[PayPal]] and [[Palantir Technologies]], and the central coordinating node between tech-libertarian accelerationism and Christian nationalist political infrastructure. Forbes estimated net worth $11.2 billion (2024).

The central paradox: openly gay, married to Matt Danzeisen since 2017, attends a conservative Protestant church whose doctrine considers homosexuality sinful, and has channeled $120M+ through dark money networks to fund organizations explicitly dedicated to eliminating LGBTQ rights and establishing Christian nationalist governance. This is not contradiction — it is vertical integration. He is not funding the ideology. He is funding the customer.

## Formation

Raised in Foster City, California in an Evangelical Christian household. B.A. Philosophy, Stanford University, 1989 — where he co-founded *The Stanford Review* (conservative student newspaper) and studied under **[[René Girard]]**, whose mimetic theory — that human desire is imitative, leading inevitably to rivalry and violence, resolved only through the scapegoat mechanism — became the philosophical substrate of Thiel's worldview. Girard's framework explains Thiel's apparent contradictions: he is not operating from ideology, he is operating from a theory of power in which the scapegoat mechanism can be deliberately engineered and exploited.

J.D., Stanford Law School, 1992. Met [[David Sacks]] at Stanford — the PayPal network begins here.

## The PayPal Mafia

Co-founded [[PayPal]] 1998 with [[Elon Musk]], [[David Sacks]], and others. Sold to eBay for $1.5 billion, 2002. The PayPal alumni network — the "PayPal Mafia" — became the seed capital and personnel network for Silicon Valley's next generation: Musk (Tesla, SpaceX, now DOGE), Sacks (AI/Crypto Czar, White House), [[Joe Lonsdale]] (Palantir co-founder), [[Ken Howery]] (Founders Fund, Ambassador to Denmark under Trump), [[Reid Hoffman]] (LinkedIn).

First outside investor in [[Facebook]], 2004 — $500,000 for 10.2% stake. Served on Facebook's board 2004-2022. On the board during the 2014 emotional contagion experiment (689,003 users' News Feeds manipulated without consent to study emotional influence) and the 2016-2018 Cambridge Analytica scandal. Advised Zuckerberg against fact-checking political ads throughout. Left board May 2022.

## Palantir: Surveillance as Business Model

Co-founded [[Palantir Technologies]] in 2003 with [[Alex Karp]] and [[Joe Lonsdale]]. [[In-Q-Tel]] (CIA's venture arm) provided $2M seed funding in 2004. Thiel contributed $30M in 2005. Zero revenue through stealth pilot phase — built entirely on government intelligence contracts.

Palantir is not a tech company that sells to governments. It is a surveillance infrastructure company whose business model requires authoritarian policy expansion. The loop is explicit: [[Stephen Miller]] sets mass arrest quotas → ICE uses Palantir's ImmigrationOS → arrests surge → Palantir stock rises → Miller's portfolio (he holds Palantir stock, per financial disclosures) grows → repeat.

By 2025: $900M+ in Trump administration contracts. ImmigrationOS: full tracking pipeline from identification to removal. Data integrations added under Trump 2.0: IRS, Social Security Administration, license plate readers. Deployed in Israel (Lavender AI: 37,000+ automated targeting designations), UAE (Aither joint venture with Dubai Holding), and multiple Board of Peace member nations.

[[Persona]] — biometric age verification company funded by [[Founders Fund]] — deployed on Roblox (151M daily users, 40% under 13), Reddit, and OpenAI. Uses 269 verification checks including terrorism and espionage screening. Code found on U.S. government servers. The deployment pipeline: Afghanistan checkpoints (2007) → U.S. border (2013) → LAPD (2018) → children's gaming platforms (2026).
