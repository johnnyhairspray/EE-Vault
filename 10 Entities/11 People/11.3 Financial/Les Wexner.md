# Les Wexner

Born September 8, 1937. Founder of L Brands — the retail conglomerate that owned Victoria's Secret, Bath & Body Works, Abercrombie & Fitch, and other major retail properties. One of the wealthiest individuals in Ohio. Major philanthropist; the Wexner Foundation funds Jewish leadership development; significant donor to Ohio State University (the Wexner Medical Center bears his name).

His relevance to this vault: he was Jeffrey Epstein's primary financial patron — arguably the single most important enabler of Epstein's network — and the relationship has never been legally resolved.

---

## The Epstein Relationship

Wexner and Epstein's relationship began in the late 1980s. What Wexner gave Epstein was not just money — it was the infrastructure of a major American billionaire's financial and social life:

**The Manhattan townhouse:** Wexner gave Epstein his property at 9 East 71st Street in Manhattan — a seven-story, 40-room townhouse valued at approximately $77 million. This property became the primary location for Epstein's trafficking operation in New York. Epstein used it for decades. The mechanism by which a convicted sex offender retained a $77M property gifted by one of America's wealthiest men has never been legally explained.

**Power of attorney:** Epstein held power of attorney over Wexner's estate for an extended period — giving him effective control over Wexner's financial affairs.

**Network access:** Wexner's social and philanthropic network — Ohio State University, the Wexner Foundation, the broader Jewish philanthropic infrastructure — provided Epstein with the social legitimacy and access he required to operate.

**The claimed break (2007):** Wexner publicly stated he cut ties with Epstein in 2007 after discovering Epstein had "misappropriated vast sums" from his accounts. The timing — 2007, the year of Epstein's first arrest — has been widely questioned. Wexner was never charged with any crime. No civil recovery action was publicly pursued. The amount allegedly misappropriated has never been confirmed.

## The Central Unanswered Question

How does the relationship actually work?

Epstein had no obvious source of wealth before Wexner. His investment advisory business had essentially one client of significance: Wexner. The $77M Manhattan mansion was a gift. The power of attorney gave Epstein control over the finances. The social network gave Epstein his cover.

The standard explanation — that Wexner was a naive mark who didn't realize what Epstein was doing until 2007 — requires accepting that one of America's most successful businessmen, who built a multi-billion dollar retail empire, was comprehensively deceived for nearly two decades by a man he had given his house and control of his finances to.

The alternative explanation — that the relationship was transactional in ways that haven't been publicly established — remains unverified but has circulated in investigative reporting for years.

Neither has been legally tested. Wexner was not charged, not deposed in the criminal cases, and not meaningfully scrutinized by the 2025-2026 document releases in publicly reported material.
