# Larry Fink

Born November 2, 1952. Chairman and CEO of BlackRock — the world's largest asset manager with approximately $10+ trillion in assets under management. Co-founder of BlackRock (1988).

## The Tokenization Pivot

Fink has become the most prominent institutional advocate for tokenization of real-world assets — the process of representing physical assets (real estate, bonds, commodities, private equity) as digital tokens on blockchain infrastructure.

BlackRock's BUIDL fund — launched March 2024 — is a tokenized money market fund running on public blockchain infrastructure via [[Securitize]] as the transfer agent. BUIDL is the institutional credibility layer that [[World Liberty Financial]] leverages: WLF's RWA tokens are compatible with the BUIDL infrastructure, allowing accredited investors to redeem tokenized Trump-branded assets for institutional-grade US Treasuries.

Fink's 2026 annual chairman's letter focused substantially on AI and energy infrastructure as investment opportunities — framing the build-out of data center capacity and AI compute as the defining capital allocation decision of the decade.

## The Stakeholder Capitalism Reversal

Fink spent years championing "stakeholder capitalism" — the ESG framework arguing corporations should optimize for employees, communities, and the environment alongside shareholders. Under political pressure from the right, BlackRock withdrew from several climate-focused investment coalitions in 2023–2024 and Fink publicly distanced himself from the "ESG" label.

The reversal is relevant to the vault's Mirror Hypothesis: the WEF/ESG framework and the MAGA/sovcorp framework are rhetorical opposites but functional complements — both route massive capital flows through unaccountable institutional intermediaries. Fink pivoted from one frame to the other without changing the underlying mechanism.

## BlackRock in the Network

BlackRock is one of the three largest institutional shareholders in virtually every major company documented in this vault — Amazon, Microsoft, Google, Meta, Palantir, Oracle. The "Big Three" institutional investors (BlackRock, Vanguard, State Street) hold overlapping majority stakes across the tech sector, creating systemic alignment of incentives across ostensibly competing companies.
