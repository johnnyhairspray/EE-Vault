# Steve Witkoff

Born March 15, 1957, Bronx, New York. BA Political Science and JD, Hofstra University. Founder and CEO of the **Witkoff Group** (1997) — a real estate development firm specializing in distressed asset acquisition, renovation, and disposition. Net worth: ~$2B (Forbes, May 2025). Personal friend of Donald Trump since the mid-1990s; relationship began when Witkoff was a real estate attorney and Trump was a client.

Current roles: Special Envoy to the Middle East (January 2025–), Special Envoy for Peace Missions (July 2025–), Co-founder of World Liberty Financial, Executive Board member of the Board of Peace. No diplomatic training or experience prior to appointment. Does not take a government salary. Has not fully divested from the Witkoff Group.

## The Witkoff Group and Gulf Capital

Before becoming Trump's envoy, Witkoff built significant Gulf financial relationships through his real estate business:

- **2023: Park Lane Hotel** — Sold Manhattan's Park Lane Hotel to the **Qatar Investment Authority** (Qatar's sovereign wealth fund) for **$623 million**. Abu Dhabi's sovereign wealth fund also took a stake in the transaction. This predates his diplomatic role but establishes direct financial relationships with the same Gulf sovereigns he later negotiates with.
- **Shell Bay (Miami):** Luxury golf development near Miami, condos from $1.9M. Gulf sovereign capital involvement.

The pattern is established before he enters government: Witkoff has pre-existing financial entanglements with Qatar and Abu Dhabi — the same entities central to his diplomatic portfolio.

Witkoff is a **co-founder of World Liberty Financial** alongside his son **Zach Witkoff** (CEO). The WLF co-founder structure means Witkoff personally benefits from the same stablecoin infrastructure he administers as a diplomat:

- WLF received $500M from Tahnoun bin Zayed (Abu Dhabi NSA) four days before inauguration
- At least **$31 million of upfront payments flowed to Witkoff family entities**

In April 2026, **Forbes reported Witkoff's wealth increased 15%** over one year in the Trump administration.

## The Son Problem

When Steve Witkoff became Special Envoy, **son Alex Witkoff** took over the Witkoff Group. A New York Times investigation found:

- While Steve conducted ceasefire negotiations between Israel and Hamas, Alex was **simultaneously soliciting billions from the same Gulf governments** whose representatives were involved in peace talks
- Alex approached **Qatar** — the key Hamas mediator — for investment in a fund focused on commercial real estate in post-conflict areas
- Alex solicited UAE and Saudi entities while Steve was actively negotiating with their representatives

Steve had "not even fully divested" from the Witkoff Group as of the NYT investigation. He sold a **$120 million stake** in September 2025 following press scrutiny — not before.

## Non-Obvious Connections

### The Russia-Ukraine Thread
Witkoff became Trump's **de facto envoy to Vladimir Putin** — the main channel of communication between the Trump administration and the Russian presidency as of March 2025. Key findings:

- Drafted a **28-point Ukraine peace plan** with Russian envoy **Kirill Dmitriev** (November 2025). The English document contained phrases suggesting it was **originally drafted in Russian** before translation — raising questions about whether Witkoff was co-authoring Russian-originated policy.
- The plan called for significant Ukrainian concessions: ceding territory, limiting armed forces, renouncing NATO membership
- Included provisions using **Russian state assets frozen in the EU** to fund US-led reconstruction, with the US receiving up to 50% of investment profits — critics called this war profiteering
- Witkoff "conducted key meetings in ways that breached standard diplomatic protocol," with concerns about "accuracy, trustworthiness, and effectiveness"

The vault angle: Witkoff is simultaneously co-founder of WLF (which receives Abu Dhabi sovereign capital), Board of Peace executive, Middle East envoy, AND the primary US-Russia channel — all while his family actively solicits the same Gulf sovereigns he negotiates with.

### "Israeli Assets" Accusation
A Gulf diplomat, speaking to The Guardian, described Witkoff and Kushner as acting like **"Israeli assets"** — accusing them of manipulating Trump into the 2026 Iran war and engaging in "unorthodox and destructive diplomacy" during nuclear talks that were subsequently ended by Operation Epic Fury.

The context: Witkoff and Kushner were conducting Iran nuclear negotiations that were broken off by the US-Israeli strike on February 28, 2026 — launching the war. Whether they were manipulated, complicit, or simply ineffective, the outcome served Israeli and UAE strategic interests, both of which Witkoff has pre-existing financial entanglements with.

### The Kirill Dmitriev Connection
Dmitriev is CEO of the **Russian Direct Investment Fund (RDIF)** — Russia's sovereign wealth fund. The 28-point peace plan was co-authored between Witkoff and Dmitriev. RDIF has documented connections to Western financial networks and has been used as a vehicle for Russian strategic capital deployment in Western markets. A US diplomat co-authoring policy documents with a Russian sovereign wealth fund CEO — in documents that appear to have been originally drafted in Russian — is a significant intelligence concern not widely covered in the vault.

Witkoff is simultaneously:
1. US Special Envoy to the Middle East (government role)

His son Alex solicited Gulf investment in post-conflict real estate development. His co-founder Tahnoun bin Zayed sought Gaza as an Emirati economic zone in his White House dinner agenda.
