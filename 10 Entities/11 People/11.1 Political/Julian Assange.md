# Julian Assange

Born: 07/03/1971
Died: NA

Australian publisher and founder of [[WikiLeaks]], the disclosure platform responsible for some of the most consequential intelligence leaks in modern history. Pleaded guilty June 2024 to a single Espionage Act count of conspiring to obtain and disclose national defense information — sentenced to time served after 5 years in Belmarsh Prison — and returned to Australia a free man.

## WikiLeaks and Its Releases

WikiLeaks published over 10 million documents across its operational life, including:

- **2010 Collateral Murder video** — U.S. Apache helicopter attack in Baghdad killing 12 civilians including two Reuters journalists
- **Afghan War Logs / Iraq War Logs** — 700,000+ classified documents on U.S. military operations
- **250,000 State Department diplomatic cables** — global diplomatic intelligence
- **2016 DNC and Podesta emails** — obtained by Russian GRU unit Fancy Bear via phishing, released through WikiLeaks during the U.S. election

The Senate Intelligence Committee concluded WikiLeaks "actively sought, and played, a key role in the Russian intelligence campaign." Assange denied Russia as the source throughout.

## The Podesta Emails and the McCasland Connection

The 2016 Podesta email dump is the direct connection between Assange's operation and this vault's research network.

Among the 20,000+ emails: a January 25, 2016 message from Blink-182's Tom DeLonge to [[John Podesta]] with the subject line **"General McCasland"** — identifying [[William McCasland]] as a privately-informed insider on UAP programs with first-hand knowledge of classified aerospace materials at Wright-Patterson Air Force Base.

DeLonge wrote to Podesta: *"General McCasland was in charge of that exact laboratory up to a couple years ago. He not only knows what I'm trying to achieve, he helped assemble my advisory team. He's a very important man."*

This email, published by WikiLeaks, is the primary source establishing McCasland's documented connection to UAP research — and the chain of custody that makes his February 2026 disappearance significant beyond a routine missing persons case.

Without WikiLeaks publishing those emails, McCasland's connection to classified aerospace programs and UAP disclosure networks would not be part of the public record.

## The Trump Pardon Offer

In 2017, Republican congressman Dana Rohrabacher visited Assange at the Ecuadorian Embassy and offered a pardon in exchange for Assange publicly stating Russia was not the source of the DNC/Podesta emails. Assange's defense team alleged in court this offer was made "on instructions from the president." Trump and Rohrabacher subsequently denied it.

The offer — if real — is structurally significant: it would have been a presidential attempt to use Assange as an instrument to obscure Russian election interference. Whether accepted or not, it documents a transactional relationship between the Trump network and WikiLeaks extending beyond the 2016 campaign.

## Relevance to This Vault

Assange's primary relevance here is not as an actor in the current network but as the **mechanism** through which classified connections surfaced. His direct node connections:

- **[[William McCasland]]** — named in Podesta emails WikiLeaks published; McCasland's UAP connections documented only because of WikiLeaks release
- **[[Donald Trump]]** — alleged pardon offer; Trump campaign had multiple WikiLeaks contacts documented by Mueller report
- **[[Roger Stone]]** — Trump adviser with documented WikiLeaks coordination

Assange himself is not part of the operational network mapping end-stage capitalism or its architects. He is the instrument through which one critical evidentiary thread — the McCasland-UAP-AFRL connection — entered the public record.
