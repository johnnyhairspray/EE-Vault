# Stephen Miller

Born August 23, 1985, Santa Monica, California. Deputy Chief of Staff for Policy and Homeland Security Advisor in Trump's second term (January 20, 2025–present). Previously Senior Advisor to the President, Trump's first term (2017–2021). Founder of America First Legal (2021). The primary architect of Trump's immigration agenda across both terms and the operational link between [[White Supremacy|white supremacist ideology]], [[Great Replacement Theory]], and active federal policy.

## Formation

Raised in a liberal Jewish family in Santa Monica — a biographical detail his critics invoke because the immigration restrictions he champions would have barred his own great-grandparents' entry. Became politically radicalized in high school after reading NRA CEO Wayne LaPierre's *Guns, Crime, and Freedom*. By high school was appearing on conservative radio and pushing his school to restore the Pledge of Allegiance.

Duke University: political science, president of Students for Academic Freedom, columnist for *The Chronicle*. Co-organized a debate with **Richard Spencer** — then emerging as the primary alt-right white nationalist spokesman — between pro-immigrant author Peter Laufer and anti-immigration writer Peter Brimelow, whose *Alien Nation* Miller would later echo in policy arguments.

Post-graduation career ladder: press secretary for Rep. Michele Bachmann → Rep. John Shadegg → joined Sen. **Jeff Sessions** in 2009 as communications director. Sessions was the most vocal immigration restrictionist in the Senate. Miller became his intellectual engine — and his connection to [[Steve Bannon]]'s Breitbart network.

## The Bannon-Sessions-Miller Axis

The three men formed the ideological nucleus that built Trump's 2016 immigration platform before Trump was taken seriously as a candidate. Bannon called them all indispensable: *"You just can't wing it. Immigration is too important. You need policy people on this."*

From 2013: Miller used Breitbart to orchestrate the collapse of the bipartisan Gang of Eight immigration reform bill — coordinating with Bannon, sharing white nationalist source material, building a populist pressure campaign against compromise. Sessions and Bannon gave Miller institutional credibility and a media megaphone. Miller gave them a policy apparatus.

Bannon recommended Miller to senior Trump campaign officials in January 2016. From that point: Miller wrote Trump's RNC speech, his inaugural address (*"American carnage"*), and most of his major immigration policy speeches. The "America First" formulation in Trump's rhetoric is substantially Miller's product.

## The 900 Emails

Between March 2015 and June 2016, Miller sent 900+ emails to Breitbart editors — documented and released by SPLC's Hatewatch via former editor Katie McHugh, who has since renounced the far right.

Content:
- Recommended Jean Raspail's *The Camp of the Saints* (1973) — founding fiction of [[Great Replacement Theory]]: *"Someone should point out the parallels to real life."* Bannon subsequently called the book "prophetic" about "the invasion at the border"
- Shared articles from **VDARE** — a white nationalist website trafficking in Great Replacement / white genocide theory — directing Breitbart to source from it
- Lionized Calvin Coolidge and the **Immigration Act of 1924** — a eugenics-based law praised by Adolf Hitler as a model for Nazi Germany
- Referenced Coolidge's immigration restriction: *"Like Coolidge did"*
- More than **80% of reviewed emails** relate to race or immigration

SPLC: *"It's clear that he believes people of color are a danger to our country and should not be allowed in."*

Historian Kathleen Belew (University of Chicago): *"This is clear evidence that this is a person who is immersed in trafficking in white nationalist ideology... this is a deliberate and intentional attempt to propagate that kind of a system."*

The White House has never denied the emails' content.

## Policy Output: First Term (2017–2021)

Miller's ideological formation has a direct, documented policy output chain:

- **Muslim travel ban** — three versions, ultimately upheld by Supreme Court. Primary architect with Bannon
- **Family separation / zero-tolerance** — children taken from parents at the border. Direct Miller product
- **DACA cancellation** — Deferred Action for Childhood Arrivals ended; litigation ongoing
- **Refugee admissions slashed** — from 110,000/year ceiling to 18,000
- **Gang of Eight defeat** — Miller was the primary operational force stopping bipartisan immigration reform from 2013 onward
- **January 6 involvement** — under scrutiny for role in efforts to overturn the 2020 election; investigated by House Select Committee

## America First Legal (2021–2025)

After Trump's 2020 loss, Miller founded AFL as a litigation vehicle — *"a conservative answer to the ACLU."* AFL:
- Filed dozens of lawsuits claiming discrimination against white people
- Challenged affirmative action, DEI programs, voting rights expansions
- **Gene Hamilton**, AFL's Executive Vice President, wrote the Project 2025 Department of Justice chapter
- Miller appeared in Project 2025's "Presidential Administration Academy" training video
- AFL was listed on the Project 2025 advisory board — removed themselves in July 2024 as scrutiny increased during the campaign. Miller denied connection. The connection is documented

## Policy Output: Second Term (2025–present)

Miller returned January 20, 2025 as Deputy Chief of Staff for Policy — a role requiring no Senate confirmation, giving him direct executive access without confirmation hearings.

**Deportation architecture:**
- Set "[[ICE]]" daily arrest quota of **3,000** in May 2025 — nearly triple previous levels — directing agents to conduct dragnet operations at churches, schools, and courthouses
- Goal of deporting **1 million immigrants in 2025** — analysts described as unrealistic given funding, staffing, and legal constraints
- Directed "[[ICE]]" to defiance of at least 96 court orders in Minnesota alone (compiled by Federal Judge Patrick Schiltz)
- Approximately **2 million people had fled the country** through self-deportation as of January 2026

**Denaturalization campaign:**
- Revived "Operation Second Look" — a "turbocharged" program to strip citizenship from naturalized Americans
- December 2025: new USCIS guidance directing field offices to supply DOJ with **100-200 denaturalization cases per month** in FY2026 — potentially thousands of cases annually versus 120 total filed since 2017
- Targets: naturalized citizens for "trivial or decades-old paperwork discrepancies" — potentially including American-born children if parents are denaturalized
- Analogy: denaturalization was last used at scale during the McCarthy era (~22,000 cases/year), struck down by the Supreme Court in 1967 as creating "two classes of citizenship"

**The Kilmar Abrego Garcia case:**
The clearest documented instance of Miller's operational power overriding judicial authority. Abrego Garcia, a Salvadoran national with a court protection order against deportation, was unlawfully deported to El Salvador's CECOT prison — the Trump administration admitted it was an *"administrative error."* A federal court ordered his return. The Supreme Court 9-0 affirmed. Miller told Fox News *"We won the Supreme Court case, clearly, 9-0"* — described by conservative legal analysts as a *"brazen misrepresentation."* Trump adopted Miller's spin. Abrego Garcia was not returned.

**Territorial expansion alignment:**
January 2026 — after the American capture of Nicolás Maduro — Miller publicly called for an American invasion and occupation of Greenland: *"Nobody is going to fight the United States militarily over the future of Greenland"* and *"obviously Greenland should be part of the US."* This aligns Miller directly with the [[The Technate Redux|Technate Redux]] territorial logic — though Miller's framing is nationalist rather than technocratic. The policy destination is the same.

## The Palantir Conflict

Miller held **$100,000–$250,000** in Palantir stock while serving as the primary architect of immigration enforcement policy that Palantir's **ImmigrationOS** executes. Palantir holds a $30M contract with "[[ICE]]" for near-real-time visibility into immigrants' movements. Palantir's overall U.S. government revenue was 45% higher in Q1 2025 versus Q1 2024 — driven substantially by immigration enforcement contracts.

The conflict is structural: Miller designs the policy, Palantir builds the infrastructure to execute it, and Miller profits from Palantir's stock appreciation as that infrastructure is deployed. [[Peter Thiel]] co-founded Palantir. Thiel backed Trump through [[J.D. Vance]]. Miller executes the agenda. The financial loop is closed.

Even Steve Bannon — Miller's original ideological ally — expressed discomfort: *"The MAGA base is not happy about some of the Palantir stuff."*
