# Nickolay Mladenov

Born April 9, 1972, Sofia, Bulgaria. Diplomat and politician. High Representative for Gaza under the [[Board of Peace]], managing day-to-day operations of the Gaza governance transition. The operational counterpart to Trump's chairmanship — Trump sets policy direction and holds lifetime authority; Mladenov manages implementation.

## Background

Extensive UN and EU diplomatic career: UN Special Coordinator for the Middle East Peace Process (2015–2020), UN Special Representative for Iraq (2014–2015), Foreign Minister of Bulgaria (2009–2010), Member of the European Parliament. Known as a technically competent operator with Middle East regional expertise.

His appointment provides the Board of Peace with institutional legitimacy — framing what is structurally a private governance body as a continuation of the UN-led peace process he previously led.

## Role in the Vault Framework

Mladenov is the interface between the Board of Peace's private governance architecture and the international community. He interacts with Egypt, Jordan, Arab League states, and European governments as though the BoP is a conventional international body — legitimizing a governance structure that is accountable to no democratic body.

The analogy in the [[Sovcorp Overlay]] framework: Mladenov is the professional manager (CEO equivalent) of the Gaza sovcorp, while Trump is the chairman and the Gulf sovereign wealth funds are the equity holders.
