# Kash Patel

FBI Director under Trump's second term. Former chief of staff at the Department of Defense, former National Security Council official. Controversial appointment — confirmed February 2025 after significant Senate resistance.

---

## Key Action: Location Data Admission (March 18, 2026)

Testified under oath before the Senate Intelligence Committee's annual Worldwide Threats hearing. When pressed by Sen. Ron Wyden (D-OR) whether the FBI was purchasing Americans' location data, Patel confirmed:

> *"The FBI uses all tools to do our mission. We do purchase commercially available information that's consistent with the Constitution and the laws under the Electronic Communications Privacy Act, and it has led to some valuable intelligence for us to be utilized with our private and partner sectors."*

This reversed the position of predecessor Christopher Wray, who told the same committee in 2023 that the FBI had stopped purchasing location data. Patel refused to commit to stopping the practice when asked directly.

Wyden's response: *"So you're saying that the agency will buy Americans' location data? I believe that's what you said in kind of intelligence lingo... Doing that without a warrant is an outrageous end run around the Fourth Amendment. It's particularly dangerous given the use of artificial intelligence to comb through massive amounts of private information."*

DIA Director James Adams III confirmed at the same hearing that the Defense Intelligence Agency also purchases commercially available location data.

## The Pipeline

Commercial apps → ad networks → data brokers (incl. [[Venntell]]) → FBI/DHS/DIA → AI analysis

No warrant required. The 2018 *Carpenter v. United States* ruling requires a warrant for cell carrier location data — but purchasing the same data from a broker legally circumvents it. Tom Cotton (R-AR) defended the practice by analogy to trash on a public curb.

Known use case: FBI and DHS purchased location data from [[Venntell]] to identify and track undocumented immigrants.
