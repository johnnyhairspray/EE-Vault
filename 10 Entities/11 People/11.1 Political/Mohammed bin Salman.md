# Mohammed bin Salman (MBS)

Born August 31, 1985. Crown Prince and Prime Minister of Saudi Arabia. De facto ruler since 2017 when he consolidated power from his predecessor. Son of King Salman bin Abdulaziz.

## Relevance to the Vault

**[[Affinity Partners]] / Jared Kushner:** MBS personally overruled the Saudi PIF's own screening committee to approve the $2 billion investment in Kushner's private equity firm — after the committee recommended rejection for "inexperience," "excessive fees," and "public relations risks." By 2026 that investment had returned zero profits to Saudi Arabia while generating $87M+ in management fees for Kushner.

The structural question: why does the Crown Prince of Saudi Arabia personally overrule his own advisors to give $2 billion to Jared Kushner's firm with no return requirements? Senator Wyden's investigation concluded the likely explanation is that the investment is not a commercial transaction but a payment mechanism — purchasing ongoing access to and influence over the Trump family.

**The Khashoggi killing:** U.S. intelligence (CIA assessment, 2021) concluded MBS personally approved the assassination of Washington Post journalist Jamal Khashoggi in the Saudi consulate in Istanbul in October 2018. Khashoggi was killed and dismembered. Trump's response during his first term: declined to sanction MBS directly. Biden's administration released the CIA assessment but also declined meaningful punitive action.

**Abraham Accords:** Kushner architected the Abraham Accords during Trump's first term — normalizing relations between Israel and UAE, Bahrain, Sudan, and Morocco. Saudi normalization was a central but unrealized goal. The Board of Peace's Gaza governance architecture is partly designed to create conditions for Saudi-Israel normalization, which MBS has expressed conditional interest in.

**Operation Epic Fury:** Saudi Arabia's position during the Iran strikes is documented as complex — publicly non-committal while privately allowing some operational coordination through established channels. The Hormuz Strait risk affected Saudi oil export infrastructure.
