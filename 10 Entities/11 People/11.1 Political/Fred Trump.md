# Fred Trump

Born October 11, 1905. Died June 25, 1999. Real estate developer; father of Donald Trump. Built a portfolio of 14,000+ apartment units in Brooklyn and Queens through federally backed FHA construction loans.

## The Housing Discrimination Case

In 1973, the DOJ sued Fred and Donald Trump under the Fair Housing Act for systematic racial discrimination across their rental portfolio. Evidence included internal practices where employees marked applications "C" for "Colored" to screen out Black applicants, and testimony from Trump Management employees about deliberate exclusion.

The Trumps settled without admission of guilt in 1975, agreeing to advertise vacancies in Black newspapers and provide the Urban League with a list of vacancies. A 1978 follow-up investigation found ongoing violations.

The 1973 case is the documented origin point of Donald Trump's public legal history.

## The 1927 Arrest

Fred Trump was arrested at a 1927 Ku Klux Klan march in Queens, New York — documented in a May 1927 New York Times report listing him among those arrested. He denied participation in the KKK. The circumstances of the arrest — whether he was a participant or bystander — were disputed and never definitively resolved.

## The FHA Model

Fred Trump's business model was built on government-backed FHA loans for construction, combined with systematic resistance to government oversight of tenant selection. The pattern — extracting public subsidy while evading public accountability — is documented across his career.
