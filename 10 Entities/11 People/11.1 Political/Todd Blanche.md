# Todd Blanche

Acting Attorney General of the United States as of April 2, 2026, succeeding [[Pam Bondi]]. Previously served as Deputy Attorney General (2025–2026). Before his government role, Blanche was Trump's personal defense attorney — representing him in the New York hush money trial that resulted in Trump's 34-count felony conviction in May 2024.

The appointment of Trump's personal defense lawyer as the nation's chief law enforcement officer is structurally significant: the same person who defended Trump against criminal prosecution now heads the agency responsible for federal prosecution decisions.

## DOJ Role

Blanche was confirmed as Deputy AG and elevated to Acting AG upon Bondi's departure. His tenure has continued the DOJ's orientation toward immigration enforcement, domestic terrorism designation under [[NSPM-7]], and support for ICE operations.

**NSPM-7 enforcement:** Issued guidance to Joint Terrorism Task Forces prioritizing "organized doxing, swatting, and civil disorder" as domestic terrorism priorities, operationalizing the anti-capitalism terrorism indicator documented in NSPM-7.

**Individual accountability doctrine:** Developed prosecutorial framework targeting individual donors and leadership of activist organizations labeled as domestic terrorism-adjacent — rather than focusing primarily on organizational entities.

## The Personal Lawyer → AG Pipeline

Blanche's trajectory is the clearest documented example of the loyalty pipeline in the Trump legal infrastructure: personal defense attorney → Deputy AG → Acting AG. The professional relationship predates and grounds his government role. His incentive structure is not institutionally aligned with DOJ independence — it is personally aligned with Trump.
