# JD Vance

Born August 2, 1984, Middletown, Ohio. Born James Donald Bowman — later James David Hamel after adoption, adopted his maternal grandparents' surname Vance in April 2013. 50th Vice President of the United States (January 20, 2025–present). Former U.S. Senator, Ohio (January 2023–January 2025). Author, venture capitalist, co-founder of Narya Capital and the Rockbridge Network.

The vault's single most analytically significant **bridge node** — the only figure who simultaneously inhabits, speaks the language of, and operationally connects all four circles mapped in this vault: the NRx/Dark Enlightenment tech network, the Christian nationalist political apparatus, the populist working-class base, and the venture capital infrastructure of the new right. Positioned as the designated heir to the MAGA movement, with ~50% RealClearPolitics support for the 2028 Republican nomination as of January 2026.

---

## The Formation

**Origins**: Raised in Middletown, Ohio — a former industrial city gutted by deindustrialization. Chaotic upbringing with drug-addicted mother, raised substantially by grandparents ("Mamaw" and "Papaw"). Enlisted in the U.S. Marine Corps 2003-2007, served as a military journalist in public affairs. Ohio State University, then Yale Law School. Brief career in corporate law before entering the Thiel orbit.

**Hillbilly Elegy (2016)**: His memoir about white working-class decline in the Rust Belt became a national phenomenon — read by liberal elites trying to understand Trump voters, embraced by conservatives as cultural diagnosis. The book positioned Vance as the acceptable voice of the forgotten working class: Yale-trained, self-made, sympathetic but not threatening. It was the credential that put him in rooms he couldn't otherwise have accessed.

**The Thiel Entry Point**: [[Peter Thiel]]'s venture capital firm **Mithril Capital** hired Vance post-Yale. This was not incidental. Thiel was looking for political vehicles. Vance was looking for a patron. The memoir had provided the cover story — the Hillbilly Elegy persona — that made Thiel's investment in Vance legible to a mass audience as authenticity rather than elite capture.

**Catholic Conversion (August 2019)**: Vance was baptized and confirmed at St. Gertrude Priory in Cincinnati by a Dominican friar. He chose **Augustine of Hippo** as his confirmation saint — *The City of God*, the theology of Christian civilization against the declining secular empire. **Rod Dreher** — author of *The Benedict Option*, the foundational text of Christian civilizational retreat-and-rebuild strategy — attended the baptism. Dreher is listed among Vance's documented intellectual influences.

Vance explained the conversion: *"I became persuaded over time that Catholicism was true... Augustine gave me a way to understand Christian faith in a strongly intellectual way."* He explicitly noted its alignment with his political views and that the people who mattered most to him were Catholic.

**April 2026**: Vance announced *Communion: Finding My Way Back to Faith* (HarperCollins, June 16, 2026) — a book about his faith journey. Presidential hopefuls release faith books before campaigns. He is the overwhelming 2028 frontrunner.

## The Thiel-Vance Financial Architecture

**The $15M+ investment**: Thiel's funding of Vance's 2022 Ohio Senate campaign was the **largest single donation to a Senate candidate** in that cycle:
- $10 million to Protect Ohio Values (Vance super PAC)
- $3.5 million additional
- $1.5 million further
- David Sacks (PayPal Mafia): $1 million to pro-Vance super PAC

Thiel also **personally introduced Trump to Vance** in 2021 and lobbied for Vance's VP selection in 2024. Others lobbying for Vance: Elon Musk, David Sacks, Tucker Carlson. The Heritage Foundation — which drafted [[Project 2025]] — privately advocated for Vance's selection.

**Narya Capital (2019)**: Vance co-founded venture capital firm Narya Capital in Cincinnati with backing from **[[Peter Thiel]], Eric Schmidt, and [[Marc Andreessen]]**. Raised $93 million in 2020. Narya invested in **Rumble** — the right-wing video platform — with Thiel and former Trump adviser Darren Blanton.

**Rockbridge Network (2019)**: Described as Vance's "brainchild" — a $100,000/year minimum membership donor network. Major donors include Thiel and Rebekah Mercer. Speakers at Rockbridge events: Vance, Thiel, Trump, Donald Trump Jr., David Sacks, Tucker Carlson. **This is the operational mechanism** connecting Silicon Valley money, Christian nationalist infrastructure, and Trump's political apparatus. It is the bridge institutionalized.

## The Yarvin Connection

This is the most politically significant and least-covered intellectual relationship in the Vance file.

**September 2021 podcast**: Vance publicly stated: *"There's this guy, Curtis Yarvin, who has written about some of these things..."* — the entry point to mainstreaming Yarvin's ideas.

**The vocabulary match**: Vance has used the phrases:
- *"Fire every single midlevel bureaucrat, every civil servant in the administrative state, replace them with our people"* — Yarvin's RAGE framework, nearly verbatim
- *"Reboot"* government — the title of Yarvin's 2012 Y Combinator speech
- *"De-Ba'athification"* and *"Denazification"* as analogies for purging civil servants — the same framing Balaji Srinivasan used, traced directly to Yarvin's vocabulary

**The NRx to Schedule F pipeline**: Yarvin theorizes the purge → Vance absorbs and amplifies it → [[Schedule F]] operationalizes it. [[DOGE]] executes it. This is the intellectual lineage of the administrative state deconstruction project made policy.

**Politico's documented influences** (August 2024): Listed Vance's seven intellectual influences as Patrick Deneen, [[Peter Thiel]], [[Curtis Yarvin]], René Girard, Sohrab Ahmari, Rod Dreher, and the Claremont Institute.

**Yarvin's reaction**: At Trump's January 2025 inauguration gala — which Yarvin attended as an informal guest of honor — Yarvin called Vance *"perfect."* By March 2025, Yarvin had downgraded to a C-grade for the overall DOGE project, calling it a *"chimpanzee orchestra."* He did not withdraw his support for the broader project.

**The "late republican period" framing**: In a 2022 podcast Vance said: *"We are in a late republican period... If we're going to push back against it, we're going to have to get pretty wild, and pretty far out there, and go in directions that a lot of conservatives right now are uncomfortable with."* This is Roman Empire / Caesarist language — the idea that liberal democracy is the late republic and what comes next is the strong executive. The same framing runs through Yarvin, through [[Steve Bannon]], through [[Kevin Roberts]]'s *"second American revolution"* rhetoric.

## The Christian Nationalist Architecture

Vance is not a casual Christian nationalist. He is the political leadership of a specific, sophisticated postliberal Catholic intellectual tradition that is theologically distinct from evangelical Protestant Christian nationalism but arrives at nearly identical governance outcomes.

**Patrick Deneen**: Author of *Why Liberalism Failed* and *Regime Change* — the latter literally calling for the installation of a new aristocratic conservative elite. Vance's most cited intellectual influence alongside Thiel and Yarvin.

**Rod Dreher**: Author of *The Benedict Option* (Christian community formation in anticipation of civilizational collapse) and *Live Not by Lies* (Christian resistance to soft totalitarianism). Present at Vance's baptism. Documented influence.

**René Girard**: Catholic philosopher of mimetic desire and scapegoating — the intellectual framework Vance uses to understand cultural conflict. Thiel is also a documented Girard follower (Stanford, personal relationship with Girard before his death). This is a shared intellectual framework between Vance and Thiel that is rarely noted.

**Kevin Roberts / Heritage Foundation**: Vance wrote the foreword to Roberts's *Dawn's Early Light: Taking Back Washington to Save America* — calling it *"an essential weapon"* for battles ahead requiring *"offensive conservatism."* When Trump selected Vance, Roberts told reporters: *"You may know that we're good friends. Privately we were really rooting for him."*

**The Christian Nation speech (TPUSA AmFest 2025)**: Speaking to 30,000+ young conservatives after Charlie Kirk's assassination, Vance declared: *"The only thing that has truly served as an anchor of the United States of America is that we have been and, by the grace of God, we always will be a Christian nation."* He described Christianity as providing *"a shared moral language"* and framed the preceding decades as a *"war"* on Christianity by the left.

**On Christian nationalism**: When the label was raised, Vance dismissed it as *"meant to be very scary"* — but then proceeded to describe exactly the Christian nationalist project: Christianity as the answer to questions of American identity, Christian faith as the organizing principle of public life.

**Ordo amoris and immigration**: When Pope Francis (and later Pope Leo XIV) criticized Vance's immigration positions, Vance invoked the Catholic concept of *ordo amoris* — the ordered hierarchy of love, which he interprets as placing love of one's co-nationals above love of foreigners. This is not folk theology. It is a sophisticated theological argument for ethnic nationalism dressed in Augustinian language. The same Augustine he chose as his confirmation saint.

## The Bridge: How He Speaks Both Languages

The vault's core analytical observation about Vance is that he is the **only figure who can speak both languages** fluently and without apparent contradiction:

**To the tech elite (documented):**

- Used Yarvin's "reboot" and "De-Ba'athification" framing
- Advocated patchwork sovereignty concepts
- Created the Rockbridge Network as the financial bridge
- Follows Bronze Age Pervert, Raw Egg Nationalist, and other far-right online figures

**To the Christian nationalists (documented):**
- Catholic convert with sophisticated theological formation
- Foreword to Kevin Roberts's Heritage Foundation manifesto
- ISI lecture on "civilizational crisis" promoting Christian nationalism
- TPUSA AmFest 2025: *"we always will be a Christian nation"*
- Pronatalism as explicit policy: *"We need more babies in the United States of America"*
- Invoked ordo amoris against papal criticism of immigration policy
- Patron saint: Augustine. Baptism witness: Rod Dreher.

**He is not contradicting himself.** The Yarvin framework and the Deneen framework arrive at the same destination: the dismantling of liberal democratic accountability, the installation of a hierarchical order, and the concentration of power in a virtuous elite. Yarvin calls it the CEO-monarch. Deneen calls it a new aristocracy rooted in common good conservatism. Vance calls it *"offensive conservatism."* Roberts calls it a *"second American Revolution."*

The populations they govern in both frameworks are the same: people who will be told what's good for them by people who know better. The tech elite's version is enforced by algorithmic control and exit barriers. The Christian nationalist version is enforced by moral law and family structure. Both versions have no democratic accountability.

**The Rockbridge Network is where the two elites meet.** Silicon Valley money and Christian nationalist infrastructure in the same room, $100,000 entry fee, Thiel and Roberts and Trump all on the same speaker list.

## As Vice President (2025-2026)

Assessed by observers as the **most powerful vice president in modern history** — *"180 degrees removed from the spit bucket"* in one analyst's phrase, surpassing Cheney's influence by early 2026.

**Greenland**: March 2025 — first sitting U.S. vice president to visit Greenland. Stood at **Pituffik Space Base** and declared to Denmark: *"You have not done a good job by the people of Greenland."* Warned of Chinese and Russian encroachment. The U.S. Space Force base commander who pledged *"all of our flags will fly proudly — together"* was subsequently fired. Vance is the diplomatic sharp edge of the [[The Technate Redux|Technate Redux]] territorial agenda.

**Iran war**: Vance defended striking Iran before [[Operation Epic Fury]] (February 28, 2026). Supported the war after it began — despite his earlier stated opposition to overseas military interventions. The isolationist populist persona dissolved when the moment came.

**Venezuela**: Declared the 2026 U.S. strikes in Venezuela justified because Maduro was *"a wanted fugitive."* Declared *"the stolen oil must be returned"* — the resource extraction framing of the Technate Redux in plain language.

**India-Pakistan**: Mediated ceasefire deal alongside Marco Rubio, May 2025.

**Armenia**: February 2026 — first sitting U.S. VP to visit Armenia. Brokered a multibillion-dollar civil nuclear energy deal aimed at reducing Russian influence.

**UAE**: Attended dinner with Sheikh Tahnoun bin Zayed Al Nahyan in March 2025; UAE subsequently announced $1.4 trillion in U.S. investment over 10 years.

**Israel policy divide**: March 2026 — reports emerged of a **policy divide between Trump and Vance on U.S.-Israel relations**. Details remain sparse but suggest Vance is navigating the same tension visible across the coalition: America First isolationism vs. Zionist eschatological commitments. This is the fault line that has no clean resolution.

**RNC Finance Chair**: Vance serves as finance chair of the Republican National Committee during his VP tenure — structural control of the party's money that no VP has previously held.
