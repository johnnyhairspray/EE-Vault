# Steve Bannon

Born November 27, 1953. Former Goldman Sachs investment banker, Hollywood film producer, Breitbart News executive chairman, White House Chief Strategist (2017), and host of the *War Room* podcast. The primary architect of the ideological and communications infrastructure of Trumpism. The connective tissue between the NRx intellectual network, the Christian nationalist political apparatus, the global populist movement, and the operational machinery of the Trump administration.

## Background: Before the Movement

Bannon's populist persona is a rebrand. He spent the 1980s as a Goldman Sachs investment banker — the institution he now frames as a symbol of globalist predation. After Goldman he founded his own media and finance firm, worked with Westinghouse to acquire a stake in Castle Rock Entertainment (producer of *Seinfeld*, from whose royalties he collected passive income), and produced a series of political documentaries designed to generate right-wing cultural influence.

He became executive chairman of Breitbart News in 2012, transforming it from a conservative media outlet into the primary distribution infrastructure for the alt-right, white nationalist, and nativist coalition that would become Trump's base. His framing of Breitbart: *"a platform for the alt-right."*

He is described by associates as a voracious reader of history and political theory — *"the most well-read person in Washington"* — who devours books *"in like an hour"* and circulates texts to colleagues and allies. The Rain Man of nationalism, in one associate's phrase. What he reads shapes what his network believes.

## The Bannon-Sessions-Miller Axis

The foundational political relationship in Trump's ideological formation is the triangular alliance between Bannon, [[Jeff Sessions]], and [[Stephen Miller]].

From 2013: Bannon at Breitbart provided the media platform; Sessions in the Senate provided the institutional legitimacy; Miller as Sessions' communications director provided the policy architecture. Together they orchestrated the defeat of the bipartisan Gang of Eight immigration reform bill — Bannon praised Sessions and Miller's role as *"like the civil-rights movement in the nineteen-sixties."*

When Trump emerged as a candidate, Bannon identified him as the vehicle. He recommended Miller to senior Trump campaign officials specifically because of his immigration policy expertise: *"You just can't wing it. Immigration is too important. You need policy people on this."* The Bannon-Miller collaboration on the travel ban and *"America First"* messaging in Trump's first term was the operational core of the administration's early policy agenda.

## Ideological Formation

Bannon is not a systematic thinker. He is a synthesizer — absorbing texts from multiple traditions and recombining them into an emotionally resonant worldview aimed at a mass audience. His intellectual influences, documented across multiple sources:

**[[Curtis Yarvin]] / Neocameralism**: Bannon has read and admired Yarvin's work. He reportedly communicated with Yarvin through an intermediary during the first Trump administration — Yarvin denied direct communication. Bannon's core project — *"deconstruction of the administrative state"* — is structurally identical to Yarvin's RAGE (Retire All Government Employees) framework, even if Bannon arrived at it through different reading. Dark Enlightenment researchers note that Bannon later considered Yarvin an enemy, though Yarvin did not reciprocate.

**Nassim Nicholas Taleb / Antifragile**: Bannon circulated Taleb's 2014 *Antifragile* among aides — a broadside against big government that argues suppressing volatility makes institutions fragile. This provided the theoretical justification for chaos as a governing strategy: the *"deconstruction"* of the administrative state as a form of deliberate volatility designed to produce a more resilient (or at least different) system.

**Michael Anton / "The Flight 93 Election"**: Anton's 2016 pseudonymous essay — arguing that not voting Trump was equivalent to letting hijackers fly the plane into the building — provided the apocalyptic permission structure for the 2016 coalition. Anton later became State Department Director of Policy Planning. Yarvin has claimed to have given staffing recommendations to Anton.

**Edmund Burke**: Bannon frames himself as a Burkean traditionalist defending organic social institutions against rootless cosmopolitan disruption. The framing is genuine in aesthetic terms — Bannon genuinely loathes the Enlightenment rationalism he associates with progressive modernity — though his methods are anything but conservative in the sense Burke would recognize.

**Julius Evola / Traditionalism**: Bannon has referenced Evola and the broader Traditionalist school — the anti-modern metaphysical tradition that also influenced European fascism. Benjamin Teitelbaum's analysis of Bannon's Traditionalism documents the influence. Bannon wishes, in Teitelbaum's framing, to *"splinter the world into fragments and turn those fragments into heavily bordered nation-states ruled by a theonomic, theocratic, neo-Puritan hierarchy."*

**Christian Nationalism**: Bannon presents himself as a defender of Judeo-Christian civilization against secular globalism, though scholars note his version of this is functionally Catholic-nationalist rather than broadly ecumenical. His 2014 Vatican conference speech criticized Wall Street for the 2008 financial crisis and framed the coming conflict as a civilizational defense of the Christian West. He later plotted with [[Jeffrey Epstein]] to remove Pope Francis from leadership of the Catholic Church — framing Francis as too accommodating to nationalist political opponents.

**The Camp of the Saints**: Bannon repeatedly cited Raspail's white nationalist novel after [[Stephen Miller]] recommended it to Breitbart, calling it *"prophetic"* about *"the invasion at the border."* This is the direct link from the 900 Breitbart emails to Bannon's public rhetoric — Miller seeded it, Bannon amplified it.

## The Global Populist Network

Bannon's most significant and least covered project: building a transnational nationalist infrastructure designed to outlast any single electoral cycle.

After his August 2017 firing from the White House, Bannon pivoted from domestic to international. He founded **The Movement** — a Brussels-based organization intended to coordinate European nationalist and populist parties: Alternative for Germany (AfD), Italy's Brothers of Italy, Marine Le Pen's National Rally, Viktor Orbán's Fidesz, and others.

His framing to [[Jeffrey Epstein]] in 2018: **"reverse Alabama"** — *"Populist/Nationalist first; Conservative Christians (Catholic/Evangelical) next."* This is the sequencing strategy: build the populist coalition first through economic grievance and immigration anxiety, then consolidate it with Christian nationalist infrastructure. The Alabama reference is to Roy Moore's 2017 loss — Bannon had backed Moore and saw the defeat as evidence that the Christian Right alone couldn't win without the populist base.

Bannon took credit in text messages to Epstein for the fall of the Belgian government (*"5 hours after my speech"* in Brussels) and predicted nationalist gains across Great Britain, France, and the European Parliament. Some of this proved accurate: 2019 European Parliament elections saw significant nationalist gains, though not enough for a majority.

His geopolitical chess extended further. Epstein introduced Bannon to potential intermediaries for Slovakian diplomat Miroslav Lajčák, wrote about the political future of Austrian Chancellor Sebastian Kurz, and offered to connect Bannon with figures across the Middle East. The two operated *"like pieces on a Risk board"* per CNN's sourcing.

At CPAC February 2025, Bannon celebrated January 6 participants, called AfD *"the MAGA of Germany,"* and ended his speech with a salute that observers characterized as a Nazi gesture. Bannon denied that characterization.

## The Epstein Connection

Bannon's relationship with [[Jeffrey Epstein]] is one of the most significant and underreported connections in the vault.

**Timeline:**
- **Early 2018**: Michael Wolff — who had used Epstein as a source for *Fire and Fury* and had 100 hours of Epstein recordings — connected Bannon and Epstein after Bannon's White House departure
- **2018-2019**: Sustained relationship documented across 15 hours of taped interviews, thousands of text messages and emails released by the House Oversight Committee in December 2025, and a 2026 document release including communications about international geopolitics
- Epstein offered Bannon use of a Paris apartment, a Palm Beach house, and his private plane on multiple occasions. The two dined together frequently
- Epstein reserved a charter jet to fly Bannon to the United Kingdom to speak at the Oxford Union
- Bannon taped 15 hours of interviews with Epstein for an unannounced documentary. He proposed a Epstein-funded counter-documentary to the Netflix *Jeffrey Epstein: Filthy Rich* production, to be filmed on Little Saint James — Epstein's private island used as a trafficking base. Bannon described the project's goal as *"to humanize the monster"* — Epstein's own phrase
- Bannon prepped Epstein for a *60 Minutes* interview that ultimately never occurred
- Bannon privately described public scrutiny of Epstein as a *"crazed jihad"* and advised Epstein on avoiding legal repercussions
- Bannon described negative media coverage of Epstein as a *"sophisticated op"*
- Epstein told a Dubai businessman: *"We have become friends"*
- The two discussed removing Pope Francis, shaping European political coalitions, pressuring China, and forging business ties in the Middle East
- Epstein told Bannon his government *"will fall this week — as planned"* about Lajčák's Slovakia in March 2018

**What this establishes**: Bannon was not merely acquainted with Epstein. He was engaged in a sustained, operationally significant relationship with a convicted sex offender actively facing new legal exposure, for whom Bannon was providing reputation management and strategic communications services, in exchange for travel, accommodation, and access to Epstein's global network of political and financial contacts. The relationship was mutually beneficial: Epstein got rehabilitation; Bannon got a well-resourced intelligence-adjacent node for his global populist network.

## Legal Record

**Federal fraud — We Build the Wall (2020)**: Arrested aboard a Chinese billionaire's yacht. Charged with conspiracy to commit mail fraud and money laundering. The campaign raised $25M+ promising all funds would go to private border wall construction; prosecutors alleged Bannon and co-defendants enriched themselves. Bannon pleaded not guilty. Trump pardoned him January 19, 2021 — one of the final acts of his first term. Three co-defendants who were not pardoned received prison sentences.

**Contempt of Congress (2021-2022)**: Subpoenaed by the House Select Committee investigating January 6. Bannon refused to comply citing *"executive privilege."* Convicted of two felony counts July 2022. Served four months in federal prison in Connecticut, released October 2024 — one week before Election Day, immediately resumed political activity.

**New York state fraud — We Build the Wall (2022-2025)**: Manhattan DA Alvin Bragg brought state charges after the federal pardon. Presidential pardons don't cover state offenses. February 11, 2025: Bannon pleaded guilty to one count of scheme to defraud — a felony. Sentenced to three years conditional discharge. No prison time. Cannot raise money or serve as officer/director of charities in New York. Left court: *"I feel like a million bucks."*

## War Room

Launched 2020 as *War Room: Pandemic*, now *Bannon's War Room*. Millions of daily listeners. The primary communications infrastructure of the MAGA movement outside of Trump's own channels. Bannon uses it to:
- Set the populist agenda ahead of Trump's own messaging
- Amplify European nationalist voices — AfD, Orbán, Le Pen adjacents
- Platform fringe figures before they enter mainstream conservative media
- Drive pressure campaigns against Republican members of Congress deemed insufficiently loyal
- Spread election fraud narratives, January 6 revisionism, and Great Reset conspiracy framing

ADL: *"War Room is a vehicle for far-right conspiracy theories."* Among its more consequential outputs: the *"Green Bay Sweep"* strategy for January 6 — a plan to have Mike Pence reject electoral votes — was discussed on War Room before the event.

## The Analytical Frame

Bannon is the vault's most significant **distribution node** — the person who takes ideological content from the NRx intellectual network, the Christian nationalist theological apparatus, and the global populist organizing infrastructure, and converts it into mass political energy.

He is not the architect of the ideology. He is its translator and amplifier. Yarvin writes for 10,000 readers; Bannon broadcasts to millions. Miller designs the policy; Bannon generates the permission structure for it. The Bannon-Sessions-Miller axis is the pipeline from fringe intellectual production to federal policy execution.

The Epstein relationship adds a dimension the vault must hold carefully: Bannon was willing to provide reputation services to a convicted child sex offender in exchange for access to his intelligence-adjacent global network. This is not peripheral to Bannon's project — it is evidence of how the project operates. Access, utility, and leverage. Ideology is the product. Network is the infrastructure.

His own documented fraud — raising $25M from working-class Trump supporters for a border wall while enriching himself — is the clearest evidence of the gap between the populist performance and the Goldman Sachs operator underneath it.
