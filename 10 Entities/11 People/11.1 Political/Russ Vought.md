# Russ Vought

Born March 6, 1976. Director of the Office of Management and Budget under Trump's second term. Previously OMB Director during Trump's first term (2019–2021). Founder of the Center for Renewing America, a Heritage-adjacent think tank that drafted executive orders pre-inauguration and serves as the operational bridge between Project 2025's policy agenda and its implementation.

## The Administrative State Project

Vought is the primary operative for dismantling the U.S. administrative state — the career civil service that provides continuity of government across administrations. His stated goal, documented in public interviews and internal communications: **"We need to be ungovernable."** On Schedule F and civil service workers: he explicitly stated the goal is to "destroy the administrative state and fire and traumatize federal workers."

This is the NRx-to-policy pipeline made concrete: [[Curtis Yarvin]]'s RAGE (Retire All Government Employees) framework expressed as OMB operational doctrine. Vought is the bureaucratic executor of what Yarvin theorized.

## Schedule F Architecture

Vought co-designed [[Schedule F]] — the executive order reclassifying career civil servants in "policy-influencing" positions as at-will employees. He held internal meetings specifically on Schedule F implementation in the weeks before Trump's first-term departure, attempting to lock in as many reclassifications as possible before Biden could reverse it. Biden reversed it on January 20, 2021. Trump reinstated it on January 20, 2025, with Vought back at OMB.

## Project 2025 / Center for Renewing America

Vought's Center for Renewing America co-authored significant portions of Project 2025's Mandate for Leadership, specifically the OMB and executive power sections. CRA identified Christian nationalism as a "top priority" for Trump's second term. The theological framing is not cosmetic — Vought has described himself as a "Christian nationalist" in public statements and frames the dismantling of the administrative state as consistent with Christian governance principles.

The convergence: [[Dominionism]] provides the theological mandate, [[Curtis Yarvin]]'s NRx framework provides the tactical blueprint, Vought provides the operational execution at OMB.

## The Budget as a Weapon

At OMB, Vought has used the budget process to defund federal programs, impound congressionally-appropriated funds, and restructure agency operations without legislation. Impoundment — refusing to spend money Congress has authorized — was ruled illegal after Nixon. The second Trump administration is testing whether courts will enforce that precedent given the expanded executive power theory underlying Schedule F and EO 14171.
