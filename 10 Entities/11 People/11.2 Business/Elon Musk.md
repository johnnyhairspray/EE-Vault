# Elon Musk

Born June 28, 1971, Pretoria, South Africa. World's wealthiest individual as of 2025-2026. CEO of Tesla and SpaceX; founder of xAI and Neuralink; co-founder of PayPal. Appointed Special Government Employee heading DOGE January 20, 2025; departed May 28-30, 2025 after 130-day SGE limit.

## The Haldeman Lineage

The ideological resonance between Musk's governance project and his grandfather's political history is documented, not speculative.

**[[Joshua Haldeman]]** — Musk's maternal grandfather — was the national director of Technocracy Inc. in Canada from 1936 to 1941. Technocracy Inc. proposed replacing elected governments with a "Technical Alliance" of engineers and scientists managing continental resources through energy accounting rather than the price system. The movement produced a detailed map of the "Technate of America" — a continental territory whose boundaries match territory-for-territory the lands Trump's expansionism claims: Canada as 51st state, Greenland, Panama Canal zone.

Musk has publicly quoted Technocracy Inc. language without attribution. His 2020 statement — *"Government is simply the largest corporation"* — is Yarvin's neocameralism and Technocracy Inc.'s governance-as-management framework, word for word.

**The Mars Technocracy quote (2019):** Musk tweeted *"accelerating Starship development to build the Martian Technocracy."* This is not metaphor or shorthand — it is the explicit use of the ideological term his grandfather organized his life around, applied to the colony Musk is building.

**The Mars governance post (December 29, 2024):** Musk posted on X: *"The Martians will decide how they are ruled. I recommend direct, rather than representative, democracy."* Whoever controls access to Mars controls who participates in that democracy. Direct democracy with a SpaceX-controlled immigration system is Technocracy Inc.'s model: the experts control the territory, the residents govern within the experts' constraints.

The loop closes: Haldeman's grandfather dreamed of a Technate of America governed by engineers. His grandson is building a Technate of Mars.

CBC documentary *The Making of Musk* (December 2025) documented the Haldeman-Technocracy connection directly.

## DOGE

Appointed de facto head of the Department of Government Efficiency as Special Government Employee — a designation limiting him to 130 days of service per year, chosen specifically to minimize disclosure requirements.

DOGE's operational blueprint is Curtis Yarvin's **RAGE** (Retire All Government Employees) framework. Washington Post confirmed in May 2025 that Yarvin is the intellectual architect.

**What DOGE accomplished in 130 days:**
- Eliminated 250,000+ federal jobs
- Seized access to Treasury payment systems, IRS databases, Social Security Administration records
- Attempted to dismantle USAID entirely (blocked by federal courts)
- Fired 177 NNSA employees including those at Sandia National Laboratories; reversed, rehiring all but ~27
- Staffed agencies with young Musk/Thiel network loyalists as Special Government Employees
- Placed former Palantir employees in key positions across multiple agencies

Tesla Q1 2025 profits fell 71% during Musk's DOGE tenure. He departed May 28-30, 2025, citing disagreement with Trump over the One Big Beautiful Bill. Trump gave him a ceremonial key on departure.

Yarvin's public assessment of DOGE in March 2025: a C-minus. *"A chimpanzee orchestra trying to play Wagner."*

## SpaceX / xAI Merger and AI Stack Consolidation

### The Merger (February 2026)

SpaceX and xAI merged into a single entity with a projected $1.25 trillion valuation, vertically integrating Starlink's global satellite internet infrastructure with xAI's Grok large language model. The combined entity controls:
- Global low-latency internet access (Starlink, ~6,000+ satellites)
- A frontier AI model (Grok) with direct access to X real-time data
- Rocket launch capability and government satellite contracts

No regulatory review was conducted.

### The Cursor Deal (April 2026)

On April 21, 2026, SpaceX/xAI announced a deal with **Cursor** — the dominant AI coding tool — giving SpaceX the option to either:
- Acquire Cursor outright for **$60 billion** later in 2026, or
- Pay **$10 billion** for their collaborative work together

Cursor's valuation trajectory: $2.5B (January 2025) → $9B (May 2025) → $29.3B (November 2025 Series D) → $50-60B (April 2026). A ~20x increase in 18 months.

**Why this matters for the vault:**

Cursor currently dominates the developer tools market — the layer where code is actually written. It relies entirely on reselling models from Anthropic and OpenAI rather than proprietary models. The acquisition would:
1. Pull Cursor off Anthropic/OpenAI model dependency onto Grok/xAI
2. Give xAI the developer market position it currently lacks
3. Add a major asset to the SpaceX IPO story — likely to be the largest IPO in history
4. Create direct competition with Altman's OpenAI at the coding infrastructure layer

The timing is deliberate: the announcement came less than a week before the **Musk v. Altman trial** begins — the high-profile legal case between Musk and OpenAI CEO Sam Altman, whose company was an early investor in Cursor.

Two senior Cursor engineers, Andrew Milich and Jason Ginsberg, had already departed to join xAI directly before the deal was announced — reporting to Musk personally, indicating integration planning was underway before the public announcement.

**The consolidation pattern:** X (Twitter, 2025) + xAI (February 2026) + Cursor (April 2026 option) = control of the communications platform, the frontier AI model, and the developer tools layer. This is the same vertical integration logic as Palantir — own the interface, own the infrastructure, own the data.

Neither Cursor nor xAI has models that can currently match Anthropic or OpenAI. The deal reveals competitive weakness as much as ambition — it is an attempt to buy market position rather than build model capability.
