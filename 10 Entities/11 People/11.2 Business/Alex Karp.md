# Alex Karp

Born August 2, 1967. CEO and co-founder of [[Palantir Technologies]]. Unlike co-founder [[Peter Thiel]], Karp is the public face of Palantir and its ideological spokesman.

Holds a PhD in neoclassical social theory from Goethe University Frankfurt — studied under Jürgen Habermas, the preeminent theorist of communicative rationality and democratic deliberation. The irony is not incidental: Karp's entire project inverts Habermas's framework, replacing communicative rationality with decision-dominance and democratic deliberation with AI kill chains.

---

## Ideology — The Technological Republic

Co-authored *The Technological Republic: Hard Power, Soft Belief, and the Future of the West* (2025) with Palantir head of corporate affairs Nicholas Zamiska. On April 18, 2026, Palantir posted a 22-point summary to X. See [[The Technological Republic]] for full ideological analysis.

Core claims: Silicon Valley has a moral obligation to arm the nation. Hard power this century requires software. AI deterrence replaces nuclear deterrence. National service as universal duty. Some cultures produce vital advances; others are dysfunctional and regressive. Reject hollow pluralism.

On Palantir's mission (earnings call): *"to scare enemies, and on occasion, kill them."*

Palantir self-describes as providing "an AI-powered kill chain" enabling "decision dominance from space to mud."

The manifesto was described as "technofascism" (Mark Coeckelbergh, University of Vienna), "an AI death drive" (Novara Media), and — by Alexander Dugin — as "much more important than Trump."

---

## Divergence from Thiel

Karp and Thiel co-founded Palantir but hold structurally different ideological positions. Thiel is NRx-adjacent — backs exit, network states, Praxis, post-democratic patchwork. Karp is an aggressive nationalist who believes tech should *serve and arm* the American nation-state, not replace it.

The same surveillance company was built by a post-nationalist exit theorist and a nationalist defend-the-West theorist simultaneously. Their business interests align even when their ideologies point in opposite directions.
