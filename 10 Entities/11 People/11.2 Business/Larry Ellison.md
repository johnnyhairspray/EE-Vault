# Larry Ellison

Born August 17, 1944. Co-founder, Executive Chairman, and CTO of Oracle Corporation. Co-founder of Stargate LLC. Net worth approximately $250–350 billion, briefly the world's richest person in 2025 before Oracle's stock softened. The most consequential tech billionaire most Americans cannot name — operating through a surveillance infrastructure empire, a media acquisition strategy, a political donor network, and a personal relationship with Benjamin Netanyahu that connects the vault's Israel, Gaza, and Board of Peace threads directly to its U.S. surveillance and AI infrastructure threads.

## Oracle: The Foundation

Oracle was founded in 1977. Its first customer was the CIA. The company is named after a CIA project. This is not conspiracy — it is documented institutional history. The intelligence community relationship is structural, not incidental, and has defined Oracle's trajectory for nearly five decades.

Oracle's current footprint across U.S. government infrastructure:

- Every federal cabinet agency runs on Oracle infrastructure
- Top Secret/SCI cloud environments for the Department of Defense and Intelligence Community
- $9 billion JWCC military cloud contract
- $222.53 million U.S. Army cloud deal in 2025
- $88 million Air Force contract for Top Secret workloads in 2026
- $11 million in annual federal lobbying with former lawmakers on payroll
- 25% of American hospitals run Oracle Health (formerly Cerner, acquired for $28.3 billion)
- The entire Veterans Affairs medical system
- Department of Defense medical records

Ellison owns more than 40% of Oracle's stock. His personal net worth is almost entirely concentrated in Oracle equity — meaning his financial interest and Oracle's institutional interests are effectively identical.

## The Surveillance Infrastructure: BlueKai

Oracle's data collection operation — assembled through acquisitions of BlueKai, Datalogix, AddThis, and Crosswise — compiled approximately **5 billion individual dossiers** on people globally. This apparatus tracked browsing behavior, purchasing patterns, and demographic data at a scale no other private entity has publicly acknowledged.

In 2024, Oracle settled with the FTC for **$115 million** over BlueKai's data practices. Oracle subsequently announced the apparatus was "shut down."

The vault's epistemic position: databases compiled at that scale and depth do not disappear after a fine. The data exists. The question is where and under whose operational control.

This is the private-sector data layer that sits beneath Oracle's government infrastructure contracts. Oracle knows the government's data and, through BlueKai, knew the population's data. The combination produces the most comprehensive surveillance architecture in private hands anywhere on earth.

## The Surveillance Vision

September 2024 Oracle keynote: Ellison articulated a detailed vision of AI-enabled ubiquitous surveillance that he described as beneficial. Body cameras on police officers and citizens, always on — including during bathroom breaks, with footage stored and accessible via court order. AI processing the footage from *"cameras placed on everything from car dashboards and front doors to security systems."* Citizens *"will be on their best behaviour"* once digital ID systems are introduced.

His framing: this is not dystopia, it is accountability. The vault's framing: this is the [[The Mirror Hypothesis|Mirror Hypothesis]] made explicit — the same infrastructure the Great Reset critics warned about, built by a Trump ally, described as freedom.

Ellison also stated at the Stargate announcement that AI would enable the design of mRNA cancer vaccines *"in about 48 hours"* — framing AI infrastructure as a humanitarian project while simultaneously describing its surveillance applications.

## The Oracle-Palantir Axis

April 2024: Oracle and [[Palantir Technologies]] announced a deep **strategic partnership** to deliver *"mission-critical AI solutions to governments and businesses."*

This was not new territory. In 2017, Ellison had held exploratory talks with [[Peter Thiel]] about **acquiring Palantir outright**. Those talks did not result in acquisition, but the relationship continued.

By July 2024: Palantir and Oracle jointly unveiled deployment guides showing Palantir's Foundry and AI platforms running on Oracle's sovereign, government, and air-gapped clouds, specifically tailored for national security clients.

June 2025: Oracle launched its Defence Ecosystem including *"Palantir for Builders."*

The structural significance: Palantir provides the AI analytics and targeting layer; Oracle provides the cloud infrastructure and data storage layer. Together they form the complete stack for AI-enabled government surveillance, military targeting, and immigration enforcement. The same infrastructure stack is deployed in the U.S. via [[ICE]] and ImmigrationOS, and in Israel via Palantir's strategic partnership with Israel's Ministry of Defense.

## Stargate LLC

January 21, 2025: Trump announced Stargate at a White House press conference alongside Ellison, [[Sam Altman]] (OpenAI), and Masayoshi Son (SoftBank). Trump called it *"the largest AI infrastructure project in history."*

Stargate is a joint venture: OpenAI, SoftBank, Oracle, and MGX. Up to $500 billion in AI infrastructure investment by 2029. Trump invoked emergency declarations to expedite energy infrastructure for the project.

As of August 2025: Bloomberg reported the project had not started and no funds had been raised to meet the initial $500 billion budget — attributing delays to market uncertainty, trade policy, and AI hardware valuations.

Stargate's geographic expansion: UAE (announced May 2025, in partnership with G42, Oracle, SoftBank, OpenAI, Nvidia, Cisco), with sites in Texas, New Mexico, Ohio, and a midwestern location TBD.

The Stargate/Oracle/Palantir convergence represents the complete merger of commercial AI infrastructure, government surveillance capability, and defense targeting into a single privately controlled apparatus backed by the Trump administration.

The Ellison family is consolidating control over what Americans see and hear at a scale not seen since the Hearst era:

Under Paramount/Skydance:
- CBS News
- Paramount Pictures
- Nickelodeon, MTV, Showtime
- UFC broadcast rights

Ellison installed **Bari Weiss** as CBS News editor-in-chief. Weiss is a staunch Zionist whose Free Press has been criticized for pro-Israel editorial framing including what The Intercept called *"genocide denial"* content about Palestinian children. Trump praised the hire on the same *60 Minutes* episode that had cost Paramount $16 million.

**TikTok**: Oracle holds approximately 15% of TikTok's U.S. entity and serves as its *"security provider"* — overseeing algorithms and data security for 170 million American users. Netanyahu told social media influencers in September 2025 that Israel's most important *"weapons"* were *"on social media"* and described the TikTok deal as *"the most important purchase that is going on right now."* Trump joked he'd like to make TikTok's algorithm *"100% MAGA."*

**Warner Bros. Discovery bid**: February 2026 — Skydance-Paramount won the bidding war for all of Warner Bros. Discovery, beating Netflix, with Ellison personally providing a $40.4 billion financing guarantee. That's CNN, HBO, and the entire Warner Bros. film library under one family's umbrella. The bid was ultimately rejected in favor of Netflix.

**Reported OracleVision project**: Three executives familiar with early briefings described a project combining Oracle's AI division with engineers from OpenAI, Palantir, and TikTok's algorithm teams — designed to map and predict public sentiment across all major platforms in real time. *"A newsroom that listens to America in real time."* Neither Oracle nor Paramount has confirmed the project. Neither has denied it.

## Netanyahu and Israel

Ellison's relationship with Benjamin Netanyahu is personal, financial, and institutional — and documented across multiple sourcing levels.

**Personal**: Netanyahu has vacationed at Ellison's privately owned Hawaiian island. Ellison hosted the Israeli Prime Minister while being named as a potential witness in Netanyahu's ongoing corruption trial in Israel.

**Financial**: Ellison gave $16.6 million to the Friends of the Israel Defense Forces in 2017 — then the largest gift in the organization's history. Total FIDF contributions exceed $26 million. He contributed $500,000 to fortify a community center in Sderot, near the Gaza border.

**Institutional**: Oracle built Israel's first cloud services. Oracle's partner Rafael Systems is one of Israel's primary defense contractors. Oracle has underground data centers in Israel. Oracle CEO Safra Catz (Israeli-born) donated millions to FIDF.

**Political**: 2015 hacked emails (released by Dropsite News) show Ellison coordinating with then-Israeli Ambassador to the UN Ron Prosor. Ellison vetted Marco Rubio for *"fealty to Israel"* — emailing after a meeting: *"Great meeting with Marco Rubio. I set him up to meet with Tony Blair. Marco will be a great friend for Israel."* Ellison subsequently donated $5 million to the Conservative Solutions PAC supporting Rubio's 2016 presidential campaign.

Ellison reportedly offered Netanyahu a paid seat on Oracle's board of directors. Netanyahu in turn invited Ellison to buy major Israeli media assets. The quid pro quo — if accurate — is the clearest documented instance of the personal-financial-geopolitical loop this vault tracks across multiple nodes.

Ellison also maneuvered to push Israeli billionaire Arnon Milchan to allow his star attorney to represent Netanyahu in a corruption case. Milchan *"relented after pressure from Ellison"* per the Times of Israel.

## The Tony Blair Institute Connection

Since 2021, Ellison has donated or pledged between **$476 million and $538 million** to the Tony Blair Institute for Global Change — making him by far its largest donor, dwarfing all others combined. The funding grew TBI from 200 to approaching 1,000 staff.

TBI has effectively become, in the words of UK tech expert Rachel Coldicutt, *"an Oracle dealership"* rather than a think tank. Joint retreats between TBI and Oracle staff are documented. Senior TBI employees have been hosted at Oracle's Austin headquarters. TBI's reports include Oracle as a strategic partner.

Blair is simultaneously: Ellison's primary political vehicle in the UK and Global South; a proposed member of Trump's **[[Board of Peace]]** for post-war Gaza governance; and the person through whom Oracle's digital governance agenda is being embedded in African, Middle Eastern, and Eastern European government ministries.

Blair has no salary from TBI. The institute nonetheless has an annual turnover of $145 million, substantially funded by Ellison.

Oracle holds 185 active UK government contracts including a **£710 million ten-year deal** to build a digital back office for the Home Office, Department for Work and Pensions, Ministry of Justice, and Department for the Environment. Oracle is also building biometric ID systems for the UK — the same category of systems Congress has banned domestically.
