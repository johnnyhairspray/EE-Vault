# Sam Altman

Born April 22, 1985. CEO of OpenAI. Former president of Y Combinator. Co-founder of Worldcoin (now World). Early investor and board member in numerous tech companies.

## OpenAI and the Stargate Project

Altman was the primary OpenAI representative at the January 21, 2026 White House announcement of [[The Stargate Project]] — the $500 billion joint venture between OpenAI, SoftBank, Oracle, and MGX (Abu Dhabi) to build AI infrastructure across the United States. He appeared alongside Trump, Larry Ellison, and SoftBank's Masayoshi Son. The announcement positioned OpenAI as the AI model provider for the national infrastructure project.

Altman's stated framing: ensuring "America wins" the AGI race against China. He has consistently aligned OpenAI's commercial interests with the national security framing that justifies the scale of public-private investment in AI infrastructure.

## The Government Alignment

Despite OpenAI's stated safety mission, Altman has navigated the Trump administration relationship pragmatically — appearing at the White House, supporting Stargate, and avoiding public conflict with the administration while other Silicon Valley figures have been more explicitly accommodationist.

In February 2026, Altman made widely-circulated comments suggesting AI might eventually replace human CEOs — framed as self-aware candor rather than advocacy, but consistent with the accelerationist tech culture in which he operates.

## The Praxis Connection

Altman is documented as an investor in [[Praxis]] alongside Peter Thiel, Joe Lonsdale, Marc Andreessen, and the Winklevoss twins. Praxis is the Network State project exploring Greenland as territory for a private oligarch-governed city-state. Altman's Praxis investment is not incidental — it places him in the same ideological and financial network as the broader sovcorp project documented in this vault.

## Worldcoin / World

Altman co-founded Worldcoin (rebranded World) — a biometric identity and cryptocurrency project that uses iris-scanning devices ("Orbs") to create a global biometric database in exchange for cryptocurrency. Framed as providing universal basic income infrastructure for a post-AGI world, it has faced regulatory scrutiny in multiple countries for its data collection practices.

The project is relevant to vault themes: a private company building a global biometric identity layer, deploying in the Global South, funded by Silicon Valley capital.
