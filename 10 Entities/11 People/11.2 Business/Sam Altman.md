# Sam Altman

Born April 22, 1985. CEO of OpenAI. Former president of Y Combinator. Co-founder of Worldcoin (now World). Early investor and board member in numerous tech companies.

---

## The Onstage Subpoena — November 2025

One of the more unusual documented events involving a major tech CEO. During a live public conversation at the **Sydney Goldstein Theater in San Francisco**, while onstage with Golden State Warriors head coach Steve Kerr, an investigator from the **San Francisco Public Defender's Office** climbed onto the stage and declared: *"I have a subpoena for Sam Altman."*

Altman did not physically take the document. The evening's host, Manny Yekutiel, intervened and passed it to a security guard. Under California law, a subpoena is considered legally served even if the person does not physically receive it.

The subpoena was real. The SF Public Defender's Office confirmed it was a lawful service, noting investigators had "first made several prior attempts to serve the subpoena at Altman's company headquarters and via its online portal."

**The legal context:** The subpoena was connected to a criminal case against members of **Stop AI** — an activist group that staged protests blocking OpenAI employees from entering their San Francisco headquarters in February 2025. Stop AI members faced criminal charges for blocking building entrances. Altman was called as a potential witness.

Stop AI's stated mission: banning the development of artificial superintelligence. Their framing of the trial: *"This will be the first time in human history where a jury of normal people are asked about the extinction threat that AI poses to humanity."*

### The Stop AI Unraveling

After the subpoena incident, Stop AI's own internal structure collapsed. Co-founder **Sam Kirchner** disappeared following an alleged assault on another Stop AI leader, **Matthew Hall**, during an internal dispute in which Kirchner reportedly suggested abandoning nonviolence. Kirchner remains missing. Stop AI subsequently announced both Kirchner and co-founder Guido Reichstadter had been removed from the organization.

Kirchner had previously made national news separately — in October 2025, he appeared at OpenAI headquarters and reportedly threatened to "murder people," prompting employees to shelter in place. This was before his disappearance.

Stop AI insists it "has always adhered to nonviolent activism."

## Attempted Firebombing — April 2026

Separate from Stop AI and unconnected to the subpoena incident: in April 2026, **Daniel Moreno-Gama**, a 20-year-old, allegedly attempted to firebomb Altman's San Francisco home. Moreno-Gama also went to OpenAI's headquarters and reportedly attempted to shatter the building's glass doors with a chair. He had no documented connection to Stop AI or Pause AI.

The incident drew renewed attention to the landscape of anti-AI activism and its divergent factions.

## OpenAI and the Stargate Project

Altman was the primary OpenAI representative at the January 21, 2026 White House announcement of [[The Stargate Project]] — the $500 billion joint venture between OpenAI, SoftBank, Oracle, and MGX (Abu Dhabi) to build AI infrastructure across the United States. He appeared alongside Trump, Larry Ellison, and SoftBank's Masayoshi Son. The announcement positioned OpenAI as the AI model provider for the national infrastructure project.

Altman testified before the Senate Commerce Committee on May 8, 2025 — his first congressional appearance since 2023 — focused on the US-China AI competition. His framing: American AI development must not be "slowed down" by ill-considered regulations. He advocated for a single federal "light touch" framework rather than 50 state-level regulations.

The day before that testimony, he visited the Abilene, Texas Stargate data center site, calling Texas "unbelievable" in incentivizing AI projects and predicting the Abilene site would become "the largest AI training facility in the world."

His 2025 testimony was notably different in tone from his 2023 appearance (which focused on safety and regulation) — reflecting OpenAI's full pivot toward competitive acceleration framing.

## The Government Alignment

Despite OpenAI's stated safety mission, Altman has navigated the Trump administration relationship pragmatically — appearing at the White House, supporting Stargate, and avoiding public conflict with the administration while other Silicon Valley figures have been more explicitly accommodationist.

In February 2026, Altman made widely-circulated comments suggesting AI might eventually replace human CEOs — framed as self-aware candor rather than advocacy, but consistent with the accelerationist tech culture in which he operates.

## The Praxis Connection

Altman is documented as an investor in [[Praxis]] alongside Peter Thiel, Joe Lonsdale, Marc Andreessen, and the Winklevoss twins. Praxis is the Network State project exploring Greenland as territory for a private oligarch-governed city-state. Altman's Praxis investment is not incidental — it places him in the same ideological and financial network as the broader sovcorp project documented in this vault.

## Worldcoin / World

Altman co-founded Worldcoin (rebranded World) — a biometric identity and cryptocurrency project that uses iris-scanning devices ("Orbs") to create a global biometric database in exchange for cryptocurrency. Framed as providing universal basic income infrastructure for a post-AGI world, it has faced regulatory scrutiny in multiple countries for its data collection practices.

The project is relevant to vault themes: a private company building a global biometric identity layer, deploying in the Global South, funded by Silicon Valley capital.
