# Joshua Haldeman (1902–1974)

Elon Musk's maternal grandfather. Canadian chiropractor, pilot, and political radical. High-ranking leader in Technocracy Inc. during the 1930s and 40s, serving as chairman of the movement's Canadian branch. Arrested 1940, found guilty of subversion. Emigrated to South Africa 1950, explicitly citing support for the apartheid government.

Died in a plane crash in 1974, age 71.

---

## Technocracy Inc.

Haldeman led Technocracy Incorporated in Canada 1936-1941. The movement proposed replacing elected politicians with a "Technical Alliance" of engineers and scientists managing continental resources through energy accounting rather than the price system. Members wore identical gray uniforms modeled on Nazi brown and black shirts and referred to themselves by numbers rather than names — Haldeman became **"10450-1."**

The movement's territorial vision: a "Technate of America" absorbing Canada, Mexico, Central America, and **Greenland** into a single scientifically-governed superstate. This map matches territory-for-territory the acquisitions Trump's administration has pursued, and the Martian colony Elon Musk is building.

**The Canadian government banned Technocracy Inc. in 1940** as a national security threat — the organization was deemed subversive to the war effort and sympathetic to Nazi Germany. Haldeman was **arrested October 8, 1940** by the RCMP on charges of membership in an illegal organization. When arrested, **documents sympathetic to the Nazis and other subversive materials were found inside his house.** He was found guilty and briefly imprisoned, then remained on a subversion watch list for the rest of the war.

He resigned from Technocracy Inc. not over the Nazi sympathies but because the organization declared support for Soviet Russia after Germany invaded the USSR — Haldeman was anti-communist and found this intolerable.

## Social Credit Party and Antisemitism

After Technocracy, Haldeman joined the Social Credit Party of Canada (1943) and served as Saskatchewan leader. The Social Credit Party had deep antisemitic roots.

Haldeman publicly **defended the publication of the *Protocols of the Elders of Zion*** — the fraudulent document claiming an international Jewish conspiracy to rule the world. He stated that whether the document was fraudulent "is not the point," arguing the conspiracy it described was "rapidly unfolding." He framed it as directed by "international financiers, many but not all of them, Jewish."

He formally made statements discouraging *public* antisemitism in the party while privately defending the ideological content. Rabbi Irwin Gordon expressed skepticism about these disavowals at the time.

Haldeman also published *The International Conspiracy in Health* — a sequel attacking vaccines and fluoride as tools of world government, framing public health mandates as instruments of the Jewish-international conspiracy. Proto-RFK Jr., seventy years early.

## Apartheid South Africa

Haldeman's move to South Africa in 1950 was explicitly motivated by support for the apartheid government. He told Afrikaner nationalist newspaper *Die Transvaler* — described by a South African judge as "a tool of the Nazis in South Africa" — that the government's racial policies had *"encouraged me to come and settle here."*

His son-in-law Errol Musk (Elon's father) stated: *"They were very fanatical in favor of apartheid... Maye's parents came to South Africa from Canada because they sympathised with the Afrikaner government."*

Errol Musk also stated the Haldemans *"were in the German party in Canada and sympathised with the Germans"* — the closest family acknowledgment of Nazi sympathies on record.

Of Black South Africans, Haldeman wrote: *"The natives are very primitive and must not be taken seriously."*
