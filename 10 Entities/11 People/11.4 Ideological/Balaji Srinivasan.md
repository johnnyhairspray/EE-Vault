# Balaji Srinivasan

Born 1980. Former CTO of Coinbase; former general partner at Andreessen Horowitz. Author of *The Network State* (2022). Primary theorist of the "exit over voice" framework for post-democratic governance via technology.

## The Network State Theory

Srinivasan's *The Network State* (2022) lays out a five-step process for building a sovereign territory outside existing nation-states:

1. Build an online community around shared values/ideology
2. Create economic activity within the community (cryptocurrency, remote work)
3. Crowdfund physical land — purchasing territory in multiple locations globally
4. Demonstrate "proof of concept" governance that improves on existing states
5. Seek diplomatic recognition as a sovereign entity

The theory is the intellectual framework for projects like [[Praxis]] — which has raised $525M and is exploring Greenland as territory. Srinivasan is a Praxis investor alongside Peter Thiel, Marc Andreessen, Sam Altman, and the Winklevoss twins.

## Exit Over Voice

Srinivasan explicitly frames his project as "exit" rather than "voice" — the Hirschman distinction between leaving a system versus trying to reform it from within. His argument: democratic institutions are too captured and too slow to reform; the correct response is to build parallel institutions outside their jurisdiction.

This is the ideological foundation shared across the sovcorp network: rather than winning elections or changing policy, build systems that make existing democratic governance irrelevant.

*"The state is not legitimate. Build around it."*

## The Crypto-as-Sovereignty Thesis

Srinivasan frames cryptocurrency not merely as finance but as a parallel legal and property rights system — one that removes contract enforcement, property ownership, and monetary policy from state jurisdiction and places them on immutable blockchain ledgers. In this framing, crypto is infrastructure for governance, not just value transfer.

This connects directly to [[World Liberty Financial]] and the broader USD1/RWA tokenization project: an attempt to route significant economic activity through systems that operate outside or alongside traditional regulatory jurisdiction.

## Network School

In 2026, Srinivasan expanded his "Network School" — a physical educational program training "techno-administrators" to build startup societies — with campuses in Miami and Singapore. The curriculum treats governance as a technical design problem and existing nation-states as legacy systems to be replaced.
