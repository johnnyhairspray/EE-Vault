# Carl Grillmair

Born: 01/01/1959
Died: 02/16/2026

Canadian-American astrophysicist and research scientist at Caltech's Infrared Processing and Analysis Center (IPAC) since 1997. Worked on Hubble Space Telescope, Spitzer Space Telescope, NEOWISE, and NEO Surveyor. Discovered water on multiple exoplanets.

His institutional and technical significance to this cluster is specific: NEOWISE and NEO Surveyor use long-wavelength infrared detection to track objects during atmospheric transit — the same physics used in military systems for tracking satellites and hypersonic re-entry vehicles. AFOSR funds research in the same spectral bands Grillmair's work operated in.

Institutional geography matters: IPAC is managed by Caltech. NEO Surveyor is developed by JPL. [[Monica Reza]] worked at JPL. Same campus corridor in the San Gabriel Valley — the same stretch housing America's planetary defense infrastructure.

Shot on his front porch at 6:10 a.m. on February 16, 2026 — eleven days before [[William McCasland]] vanished, twelve days before Operation Epic Fury began. Suspect Freddy Snyder had been reported trespassing on Grillmair's property with a loaded unregistered rifle on December 20, 2025. Prosecutors dropped those charges by early February 2026. Snyder returned and killed him weeks later.

Caltech's public statement described him as having "passed away suddenly" — without using the word shot.
