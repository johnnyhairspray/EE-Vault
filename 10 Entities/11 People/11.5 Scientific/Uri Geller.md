# Uri Geller

Born December 20, 1946 in Tel Aviv. Israeli-British illusionist, self-described psychic, and documented CIA asset. Famous for claiming the ability to bend spoons with his mind. Central figure in the CIA's Stargate Project — the first significant test subject when remote viewing research began at Stanford Research Institute in 1972.

## The CIA Relationship

Physicists Russell Targ and Harold Puthoff at SRI tested Geller in 1972. Their conclusion in declassified documents: *"As a result of Geller's success in this experimental period, we consider that he has demonstrated his paranormal perceptual ability in a convincing and unambiguous manner."*

Geller has stated publicly he worked with the CIA for years after SRI, including operational deployments: standing outside the Russian Embassy in Mexico attempting to erase floppy discs carried by Russian agents, and attempting to telepathically influence a foreign official to sign a nuclear deal.

**The Mossad connection:** Geller stated: *"The CIA realised that what I did is real, they probably had a deal with Mossad to get me out of Israel. I worked for them for a long time."* — his account of how the CIA-Geller relationship began. Not independently verified but stated publicly by Geller in the context of 2017 CIA document declassification.

## The Skeptic Counterpart

James Randi — see [[James Randi]] — devoted significant effort to debunking Geller, arguing all his abilities were stage magic and sleight of hand. Randi demonstrated he could replicate every Geller effect through conventional conjuring. The CIA scientists, as physicists rather than magicians, were in Randi's view poorly equipped to detect skilled deception.

## The Old Stargate / New Stargate Note

Geller was the human face of the original CIA Stargate Project (1972-1995) — a $20M program to achieve information superiority through psi. The 2025 Stargate LLC ($500B AI infrastructure) shares the name but not the mechanism. Both were justified by adversary capability anxiety (Soviets in 1972; China in 2025). The strategic logic is identical: achieve information access beyond conventional limits. The name reuse appears coincidental rather than intentional.
