# USD1

U.S. dollar-pegged stablecoin issued by [[World Liberty Financial]] (DT Marks DEFI LLC — the Trump family's crypto venture). Launched January 2025. Backed primarily by short-term U.S. Treasuries and cash equivalents, with BlackRock's BUIDL fund as a key backing vehicle through the Securitize infrastructure.

## The Structure

USD1 is a reserve-backed stablecoin: every USD1 token is supposed to be backed 1:1 by a dollar-equivalent asset held in reserve. The reserve composition — primarily U.S. Treasuries — is managed through the Securitize / BlackRock BUIDL infrastructure.

**The Trump family economics:** World Liberty Financial retains 75% of protocol revenue. The Trump Organization holds a 22.5% stake in WLF's operations entity. This means every USD1 transaction generates fees that flow partly to the Trump family.

## The Abu Dhabi Convergence

In May 2025, Abu Dhabi's MGX sovereign wealth fund — controlled by [[Tahnoun bin Zayed Al Nahyan]], the UAE's national security czar — invested **$2 billion in Binance using USD1 as the settlement currency**. This was USD1's first large-scale institutional deployment.

The significance: a UAE sovereign wealth fund, controlled by the person who also invested in World Liberty Financial before Trump's inauguration, chose to settle a $2 billion transaction using a stablecoin issued by a company that generates revenue for the Trump family. Days after the WLF investment closed, Trump reversed a policy that had been blocking Binance's operations in the United States.

## The Board of Peace Integration

USD1 has been proposed as the payment infrastructure for the [[Board of Peace]]'s Gaza reconstruction disbursements — meaning aid distribution to 2+ million Palestinian civilians could flow through a financial instrument that generates fees for the Trump family.

Whether this is implemented or remains a proposal as of April 2026 requires verification.

## The Emoluments Problem

The Emoluments Clause prohibits the President from receiving financial benefits from foreign governments. A UAE sovereign wealth fund choosing to settle a $2 billion transaction using USD1 — generating WLF protocol revenue — is a direct financial benefit to the Trump family from a foreign government. Whether this triggers the Emoluments Clause is being litigated.
