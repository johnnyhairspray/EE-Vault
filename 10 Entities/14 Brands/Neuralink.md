# Neuralink

Neurotechnology company founded 2016 by Elon Musk and others. Develops implantable brain-computer interfaces (BCIs). Headquarters in Fremont, California.

## The N1 Device (Link)

Neuralink's primary product is the N1 chip — a coin-sized implant inserted by a custom surgical robot into the skull. The device reads neural signals from motor cortex neurons and transmits them wirelessly, enabling direct brain-to-device control.

**Telepathy (January 2024):** First human implant. Patient — Noland Arbaugh, diagnosed with ALS — demonstrated control of a computer cursor through thought alone. Successfully played chess and video games using neural signals.

**Clinical trials (2026):** As of early 2026, Neuralink reported 21 enrolled participants across its PRIME study. Targeting 20,000+ surgeries annually by 2031.

**Speech restoration (VOICE, March 2026):** Musk confirmed Neuralink is now capable of translating brain activity into audible words for patients who have lost speech ability — specifically for ALS and stroke patients. This is a medically significant application with documented therapeutic value.

**Blindsight:** A separate device targeting the visual cortex to restore sight by bypassing damaged eyes. Entering human trials in 2026. The device stimulates the visual cortex directly, creating perceived visual experience without functional eyes.

## The Regulatory History

FDA initially declined to approve Neuralink's first human trial application in 2022 over safety concerns about the device's lithium battery and the surgical robot's ability to extract implanted wires without damaging brain tissue. FDA approved the trial in May 2023. The approval came after Musk's political alignment with the Trump administration was well established — the regulatory relationship between Neuralink and FDA under the second Trump term is worth tracking.

## The Vault Relevance

Neuralink is documented here because it is the clearest existing example of the transhumanist layer of the broader network — the same ideological framework that produced the Enhanced Games (Peter Thiel, biological enhancement through PEDs) extended to direct neural interface. The question of who controls the data generated by a brain implant — and under what legal framework — is unresolved. Neural data has no established legal protection in the United States comparable to medical privacy law.

The "humans 2.0" framing articulated by Enhanced Games investors is the same framing implicit in consumer-facing BCIs: the human body as hardware to be upgraded, with access determined by capital.
