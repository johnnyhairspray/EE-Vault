# The Stargate Project

**Note:** This note was misfiled as "OpenAI.md" — it documents Stargate LLC, not OpenAI the organization.

Joint venture announced January 21, 2026 at the White House. Founding partners: OpenAI, SoftBank, Oracle, and MGX (Abu Dhabi sovereign capital). Projected $500 billion investment in U.S. AI infrastructure over four years.

## The Announcement

Trump, Sam Altman (OpenAI), Larry Ellison (Oracle), and Masayoshi Son (SoftBank) appeared together at the White House on January 21, 2026 — Trump's second day in office. The announcement framed Stargate as a national security project ensuring "America wins" the AI race against China.

Initial commitment: $100 billion, targeting $500 billion over four years. The scale requires approximately 10 gigawatts of power — equivalent to the combined consumption of New York City and San Diego.

## Physical Infrastructure

**Primary site:** Abilene, Texas — a $10 billion+ campus powered by a 1.2 GW natural gas and wind hybrid. As of March 2026: over 100,000 Nvidia Blackwell GPUs operational.

**National footprint:** Additional sites announced in Texas, New Mexico, Ohio, and the Midwest. Facilities constructed at "wartime speed" under a compressed timeline.

## The Abu Dhabi Capital Layer

MGX — the Abu Dhabi AI company backed by Mubadala Investment Company (part of Tahnoun bin Zayed Al Nahyan's portfolio) — is one of four founding Stargate partners. This places UAE sovereign capital inside the U.S. national AI infrastructure project announced two days after Trump's inauguration.

The same Tahnoun who purchased 49% of [[World Liberty Financial]] days before Trump's inauguration is connected to MGX, which is inside Stargate. The capital convergence between the Trump financial ecosystem and U.S. AI national security infrastructure runs through Abu Dhabi sovereign wealth.

## The Governance Structure

Stargate is a private joint venture — not a government agency. It operates on federal land with government support but is controlled by its private founding partners. Congressional oversight is limited. The structure creates a privatized national security AI infrastructure outside democratic accountability.
