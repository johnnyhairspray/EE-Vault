# Praxis

Charter city startup founded ~2019 by Dryden Brown — NYU dropout, former hedge fund analyst — originally as "Bluebook Cities." The primary operational vehicle for [[Network State]] ideology. Raised $525 million in milestone-based venture financing. As of January 2026: claims 151,068 "citizens" from 80 countries, with aggregate company valuations among members exceeding $1.1 trillion.

## The Founder

Dryden Brown. Grew up in Santa Barbara, studied Ayn Rand and Austrian economists as a homeschooled teenager, applied exclusively to Harvard/Stanford/Oxford/Cambridge, was rejected by all, attended NYU before dropping out. Developed the Praxis concept reportedly after watching protesters break shop windows in SoHo following the George Floyd murder in 2020. Encouraged staff to read **Julius Evola** — the Italian fascist metaphysician who influenced post-war neo-fascist movements. His branding guide for the original Mediterranean city concept specified *"traditional, European/Western beauty standards"* as a recruitment aesthetic and focused on attracting tech employees with *"hot girls."*

## The Location History

**2019-2024 — Mediterranean**: Original plan. Vague. Never resolved to a specific country or site.

**2019 — Greenland pivot**: Brown became interested in Greenland *"when Trump offered to buy it in 2019."* The territorial ambition of the U.S. president and the territorial ambition of the Network State movement converged on the same island simultaneously and independently.

**November 2024**: Brown traveled to Nuuk on a twin-propeller plane, met with Greenlandic politicians including Pele Broberg. *"I went to Greenland to try to buy it,"* he posted on X. Greenlandic politicians expressed interest in sovereignty, not in being bought.

**June 2025 — California pivot**: Brown announced **Atlas** — a *"defense-focused spaceport city"* near Vandenberg Space Force Base, described as *"a Jetsons/pioneer/1950s/space-futurism insane city on federal land."* Brown simultaneously stated plans for a non-U.S. city in 2026 for *"tech elites"* to relocate to if the U.S. political environment became less tech-friendly. Waiting list: 50,000 people.

## The Diplomatic Vehicle

**Ken Howery** — PayPal co-founder with Thiel, Founders Fund co-founder — was appointed U.S. Ambassador to Denmark by Trump. Reported mandate: lead Greenland acquisition negotiations. The same person who co-built Thiel's original financial infrastructure is the U.S. diplomatic point person on the specific territory Thiel's investment network wants to acquire as a charter city.

**J.D. Vance** visited Pituffik (Thule) Space Base in Greenland on March 28, 2025, and attacked Denmark: *"You have not done a good job by the people of Greenland."* The U.S. Space Force base commander who pledged *"all of our flags will fly proudly — together"* was subsequently fired.
