# LexisNexis Risk Solutions

Government and commercial risk analytics division of RELX Group. Distinct from the legal research LexisNexis (same parent company, different division). Provides public records analytics, identity verification, and background screening services.

## The LexID System

LexisNexis Risk Solutions maintains databases covering approximately 84 billion records across 10,000+ datasets — linked through a proprietary system called **LexID** that resolves records across different sources to a single individual identity profile. Claimed coverage: 98% of the U.S. adult population.

LexID is the commercial identity layer underneath federal vetting infrastructure. When government agencies run background checks, the LexID system provides the underlying data aggregation — linking public records, address history, criminal records, professional licenses, financial data, and other identifiers into unified individual profiles.

## The DCSA Personnel Vetting Contract (March 2026)

LexisNexis Special Services was awarded a $23.5 million contract by the Defense Counterintelligence and Security Agency (DCSA) for personnel vetting — providing comprehensive data collection and analysis for federal background investigations. DCSA handles security clearances for millions of federal employees and contractors.

This contract positions LexisNexis as a commercial backbone for the U.S. government's security clearance and personnel vetting apparatus — meaning private consumer data aggregated by a commercial company is directly feeding federal loyalty and security determinations.

## ProMonitor Alert

LexisNexis's continuous monitoring product — **ProMonitor Alert** — automatically flags changes in a target individual's legal, financial, or professional status to subscribing agencies. Marketed to employers, financial institutions, and government clients for "ongoing compliance monitoring."

Applied to federal personnel vetting: continuous passive surveillance of cleared government employees, automatically reporting any change in their status to security agencies. The surveillance is not event-triggered; it is always-on.

## The NSPM-7 Connection

NSPM-7's designation of anti-capitalism as a terrorism indicator creates the policy demand for the kind of continuous behavioral monitoring LexisNexis provides. Commercial databases tracking financial distress, protest affiliations, and associational data become inputs into terrorism-adjacent vetting criteria.
