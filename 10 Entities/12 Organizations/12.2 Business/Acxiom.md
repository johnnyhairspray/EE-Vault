# Acxiom

Data marketing and services company founded 1969. Now a subsidiary of Interpublic Group (IPG), one of the largest advertising holding companies. The foundational institution of the modern data broker industry.

## Historical Significance

Acxiom pioneered the aggregation of consumer data at scale — originally through physical mailing lists built from phone books, voter registration records, and payroll processing. By the 1990s it had built one of the largest consumer databases in the United States, covering most of the adult population.

Its early products were mailing list optimization tools for direct marketers. Its downstream products — consumer behavioral profiles, credit risk scores, demographic segmentation — became the substrate on which the modern ad-tech and surveillance economy was built.

## The LiveRamp Spinoff

In 2011, Acxiom launched a data connectivity division called LiveRamp, designed to "onboard" offline customer records into digital advertising systems. In 2014, Auren Hoffman sold LiveRamp to Acxiom for $310 million.

In 2018, Acxiom reversed course — selling its marketing solutions division (the original Acxiom brand) to IPG for $2.3 billion while retaining LiveRamp as the publicly traded entity. The Acxiom name now belongs to IPG; the public company is [[LiveRamp]] (NYSE: RAMP).

The restructuring effectively gave Acxiom's legacy data assets to a major advertising conglomerate while spinning the identity resolution technology into a separate public company — separating the data from the infrastructure.

## Current Role

Under IPG, Acxiom functions as a data and identity services layer for major marketing campaigns — providing consumer segmentation, audience modeling, and data management for brands and agencies. It maintains databases covering demographics, purchase behavior, life events, and inferred attributes for hundreds of millions of consumers.
