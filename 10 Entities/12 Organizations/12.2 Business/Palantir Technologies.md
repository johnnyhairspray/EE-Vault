# Palantir Technologies

Founded May 2003 by [[Peter Thiel]], [[Alex Karp]], [[Joe Lonsdale]], [[Stephen Cohen]], and [[Nathan Gettings]]. Named after the "seeing stones" of Tolkien's Lord of the Rings — artifacts of total surveillance that corrupt their users. Headquartered in Miami, Florida.

Not a tech company that sells to governments. A surveillance infrastructure company whose business model requires the continuous expansion of authoritarian policy.

---

## The Technological Republic — April 2026 Manifesto

On April 18, 2026, Palantir posted a 22-point summary of CEO Alex Karp and Nicholas Zamiska's book *The Technological Republic: Hard Power, Soft Belief, and the Future of the West* (2025) to X. The reaction was global and immediate. See [[The Technological Republic]] for full ideological analysis and comparison with NRx and Technocracy Inc.

**Key claims:**
- Silicon Valley has an "affirmative obligation to participate in the defense of the nation"
- "The question is not whether AI weapons will be built; it is who will build them and for what purpose"
- Hard power this century will be built on software
- National service should be a universal duty
- The postwar neutering of Germany and Japan must be undone
- One age of deterrence (nuclear) is ending; a new era built on AI is beginning
- Some cultures produce vital advances; others remain dysfunctional and regressive
- "We must resist the shallow temptation of a vacant and hollow pluralism"

**The critical external reading:** Eliot Higgins (Bellingcat): *"Palantir sells operational software to defence, intelligence, immigration and police agencies. These 22 points aren't philosophy floating in space — they're the public ideology of a company whose revenue depends on the politics it's advocating."*

**Backlash:** Belgian philosopher Mark Coeckelbergh (University of Vienna): "technofascism." Yanis Varoufakis: signalling willingness "to add to nuclear Armageddon the AI-driven threat to humanity's existence." Alexander Dugin: "Palantir Manifesto is much more important than Trump. Trump is insignificant pawn. Palantir is the plan to safeguard declining Western dominance by radical means."

**Timing:** Posted during ongoing US-Iran ceasefire negotiations, after Palantir systems were involved in Operation Epic Fury targeting including the Shajareh Tayyebeh girls' school strike that killed 170+ people — mostly girls under 13.

## Operation Epic Fury — February 2026

Palantir's Maven system — deeply embedded in the US military — was part of military planning for Operation Epic Fury, the US-Israel war on Iran launched February 28, 2026. The system allows soldiers to select up to 80 targets per hour in "four clicks."

Palantir systems were involved in targeted strikes including the strike on Shajareh Tayyebeh girls' school in southern Iran that killed more than 170 people, mostly girls under 13. Palantir's involvement in the targeting of Iranian government and scientific figures — including Supreme Leader Khamenei — is alleged through the same systems.

**2025 financials:** 63% revenue bump year over year. US commercial revenue up 121%. US government revenue up 52%. The Iran war and its procurement tail are expected to further accelerate government revenue.

## DOGE Master Database — 2025-2026

The Trump administration tapped Palantir — through DOGE's data centralization initiative — to compile a "master database" cross-referencing IRS records, Social Security data, immigration records, and license plate reader data. Wired described Palantir as "becoming an operating system for the entire government."

The $30M ICE ImmigrationOS contract provides near-real-time visibility into immigrant movements as the agency targets 3,000 arrests per day. Palantir integration extends to healthcare data (Tampa General, NHS), financial records, and employment data.

## Founding and CIA Origins

Thiel personally bankrolled initial development with $30M after traditional venture capital declined. [[In-Q-Tel]] — the CIA's venture capital arm — provided $2M in seed funding in 2004, giving Palantir both financial backing and direct intelligence community access before it had a single commercial product. First product: Palantir Gotham (2008), designed exclusively for intelligence and defense applications.

By 2013, a leaked document confirmed Palantir's clients included at least 12 U.S. government agencies: CIA, NSA, FBI, DHS, Marine Corps, and others. Zero revenue through the stealth pilot phase — built entirely on classified government contracts.

## The Deployment Pipeline

Palantir's surveillance architecture follows a documented pipeline: military deployment in conflict zones → domestic law enforcement → civilian infrastructure → children's platforms.

**Afghanistan (Phase I):** 80% biometric coverage grid across 32 million people. HIIDE devices deployed at checkpoints — iris scans, fingerprints, biographical data, GPS coordinates. Civilian ID checks framed as protection. The [[HIIDE]] database was later sold on eBay with 2,600+ unprotected records intact. Taliban obtained devices. Data weaponized against enrolled civilians.

**U.S. Border/ICE (Phase II):** Palantir partnered with ICE in 2013. $41M investigative case management contract (2014). April 2025: $30M ImmigrationOS contract — full pipeline from identification to removal, near-real-time tracking of self-deportations. Total Trump 2.0 contracts: $900M+. New data integrations added: IRS, Social Security Administration, license plate readers. [[Stephen Miller]] owns Palantir stock per financial disclosures — the same official setting mass arrest quotas that drive Palantir revenue.

**LAPD (Phase III):** Operation LASER predictive policing. Field Interview Cards create permanent records with no arrest required. Gang affiliation assigned by social association. Sociologist Sarah Brayne documented how link analysis flags people by proximity to targets — a woman flagged for "multiple boyfriends within same network," no crime.

**[[Persona]]/Roblox (Phase IV):** Founders Fund (Thiel's VC) funded Persona — biometric age verification. Deployed on Roblox (151M daily users, 40% under 13), Reddit, OpenAI. 269 verification checks including terrorism and espionage screening. Discord cut ties after finding Persona code on a FedRAMP government-authorized endpoint.

## Israel: Gaza as Testing Ground

January 2024: Palantir signed a "strategic partnership" with Israel's Ministry of Defense for "war-related missions." Alex Karp: "supporting Israel in every way we can."

**[[Lavender AI]]:** AI-powered database assigning Palestinians threat scores 1-100. At peak, 37,000+ Palestinians flagged as potential targets — with a documented 10% error rate built in. IDF protocols authorized killing up to 20 civilians per junior militant target. One IDF source: "I would invest 20 seconds for each target. I had zero added-value as a human, apart from being a stamp of approval."

**[[Gospel AI]]:** Automated target generation system for buildings, equipment, and personnel.

**"Where's Daddy":** System that alerts IDF when a target enters their family home — enabling strikes on civilian residences.

UN Special Rapporteur Francesca Albanese (June 2025) named Palantir an enabler of the "unlawful use of force." Karp has stated Palantir's algorithmic warfare systems are "equivalent to having tactical nuclear weapons against an adversary with only conventional ones."

## UK Infiltration

Palantir's UK strategy: enter via emergency → expand without tender → embed in critical national infrastructure.

**NHS Federated Data Platform:** £330M seven-year contract (November 2023). By end of 2024, fewer than a quarter of trusts actively using it. Staff resistance widespread. British Medical Association urged members to refuse non-direct care use (February 2026).

**UK Ministry of Defence:** £240M contract (December 2025), awarded without competitive tender. Peter Mandelson — whose firm represented Palantir — accompanied PM Starmer to Palantir's Washington HQ. No formal minutes taken. Revolving door: Palantir hired four ex-MoD officials before winning the contract; Barnaby Kistruck left as MoD Director of Industrial Strategy on August 31 — joined Palantir nine days later. He had written the UK's Strategic Defence Review.

**Total documented UK contracts:** £670M+ across 34 contracts in 10 departments.

December 2025: Internal Swiss Army report feared Palantir would pass military data to CIA and NSA. Switzerland declined to expand Palantir's role.

## The Business Model

Palantir does not accidentally end up in authoritarian contexts. The business model requires them. Every expansion of surveillance mandate — more deportations, more targeting, more patient data consolidation — is a revenue event.

The Karp manifesto makes this explicit: the ideology and the sales pitch are structurally indistinguishable. A world where AI weapons are inevitable, national service is mandatory, and Western hard power is the organizing principle of civilization is a world in maximum need of Palantir's products. The 22-point manifesto is simultaneously genuine belief and optimal marketing.
