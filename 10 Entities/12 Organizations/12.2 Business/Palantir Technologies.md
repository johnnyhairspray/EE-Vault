# Palantir Technologies

Founded May 2003 by [[Peter Thiel]], [[Alex Karp]], [[Joe Lonsdale]], [[Stephen Cohen]], and [[Nathan Gettings]]. Named after the "seeing stones" of Tolkien's Lord of the Rings — artifacts of total surveillance that corrupt their users. Headquartered in Miami, Florida.

Not a tech company that sells to governments. A surveillance infrastructure company whose business model requires the continuous expansion of authoritarian policy.

## Founding and CIA Origins

Thiel personally bankrolled initial development with $30M after traditional venture capital declined. [[In-Q-Tel]] — the CIA's venture capital arm — provided $2M in seed funding in 2004, giving Palantir both financial backing and direct intelligence community access before it had a single commercial product. First product: Palantir Gotham (2008), designed exclusively for intelligence and defense applications.

By 2013, a leaked document confirmed Palantir's clients included at least 12 U.S. government agencies: CIA, NSA, FBI, DHS, Marine Corps, and others. Zero revenue through the stealth pilot phase — built entirely on classified government contracts.

## The Deployment Pipeline

Palantir's surveillance architecture follows a documented pipeline: military deployment in conflict zones → domestic law enforcement → civilian infrastructure → children's platforms.

**Afghanistan (Phase I):** 80% biometric coverage grid across 32 million people. HIIDE devices deployed at checkpoints — iris scans, fingerprints, biographical data, GPS coordinates. Civilian ID checks framed as protection. The [[HIIDE]] database was later sold on eBay with 2,600+ unprotected records intact. Taliban obtained devices. Data weaponized against enrolled civilians.

**U.S. Border/ICE (Phase II):** Palantir partnered with ICE in 2013. $41M investigative case management contract (2014). April 2025: $30M ImmigrationOS contract — full pipeline from identification to removal, near-real-time tracking of self-deportations. Total Trump 2.0 contracts: $900M+. New data integrations added: IRS, Social Security Administration, license plate readers. [[Stephen Miller]] owns Palantir stock per financial disclosures — the same official setting mass arrest quotas that drive Palantir revenue.

**LAPD (Phase III):** Operation LASER predictive policing. Field Interview Cards create permanent records with no arrest required. Gang affiliation assigned by social association. Sociologist Sarah Brayne documented how link analysis flags people by proximity to targets — a woman flagged for "multiple boyfriends within same network," no crime.

**[[Persona]]/Roblox (Phase IV):** Founders Fund (Thiel's VC) funded Persona — biometric age verification. Deployed on Roblox (151M daily users, 40% under 13), Reddit, OpenAI. 269 verification checks including terrorism and espionage screening. Discord cut ties after finding Persona code on a FedRAMP government-authorized endpoint.

## Israel: Gaza as Testing Ground

January 2024: Palantir signed a "strategic partnership" with Israel's Ministry of Defense for "war-related missions." Alex Karp: "supporting Israel in every way we can."

**[[Lavender AI]]:** AI-powered database assigning Palestinians threat scores 1-100. At peak, 37,000+ Palestinians flagged as potential targets — with a documented 10% error rate built in. IDF protocols authorized killing up to 20 civilians per junior militant target. One IDF source: "I would invest 20 seconds for each target. I had zero added-value as a human, apart from being a stamp of approval."

**[[Gospel AI]]:** Automated target generation system for buildings, equipment, and personnel. Responsible for generating the target lists used in strikes.

**"Where's Daddy":** System that alerts IDF when a target enters their family home — enabling strikes on civilian residences.

UN Special Rapporteur Francesca Albanese (June 2025) named Palantir an enabler of the "unlawful use of force" and cited "reasonable grounds" to believe Palantir supplied the AI platform powering Lavender, Gospel, and Where's Daddy. Palantir denies direct involvement with Lavender specifically while confirming broad IDF support. Karp himself has stated Palantir's algorithmic warfare systems are "equivalent to having tactical nuclear weapons against an adversary with only conventional ones."

## UK Infiltration

Palantir's UK strategy is explicit "land and expand": enter via emergency (£1 COVID contract, March 2020) → expand without tender → embed in critical national infrastructure.

**NHS Federated Data Platform:** £330M seven-year contract (November 2023) to connect patient data across 240 NHS organizations. By end of 2024, fewer than a quarter of England's 215 hospital trusts actively using it. Staff resistance widespread — one official described Palantir as "ethically bankrupt." Another: "It makes me feel sick every time I log into the thing." British Medical Association (200,000+ doctors) urged members to refuse non-direct care use of the platform (February 2026).

**UK Ministry of Defence:** £240M contract (December 2025) for "data analytics supporting critical strategic, tactical and live operational decision making." Awarded without competitive tender. Peter Mandelson — former UK Ambassador to the U.S. whose firm Global Counsel represented Palantir — accompanied PM Keir Starmer to Palantir's Washington HQ (February 2025). No formal minutes taken.

**Revolving door:** Palantir hired four ex-MoD officials in 2025 before winning the record defence contract. Barnaby Kistruck left as MoD Director of Industrial Strategy on August 31, 2025 — joined Palantir nine days later. He had written the UK's Strategic Defence Review.

**Total documented UK contracts:** £670M+ across 34 contracts in 10 government departments, per The Nerve (February 2026). True figure likely higher — multiple contracts remain redacted.

December 2025: Internal Swiss Army report expressed fears Palantir would pass confidential military data to the CIA and NSA. Switzerland subsequently declined to expand Palantir's role.

## The Employee Open Letter

May 2025: 13 former Palantir employees — software engineers, managers, privacy and civil liberties staff — published an open letter condemning the company's work with the Trump administration. Key passage: "Big Tech, including Palantir, is increasingly complicit, normalizing authoritarianism under the guise of a 'revolution' led by oligarchs."

The letter flagged: biometric data collection on immigrant children, journalists being targeted, the deployment of former Palantir employees in DOGE. Signatories stated they joined believing in Palantir's stated commitment to protecting civil liberties — the original justification for the company's founding.

## Cambridge Analytica

Palantir employee Alfredas Chmieliauskas (London office) worked with Cambridge Analytica data scientists in 2013-2014, suggesting the personality quiz methodology that Cambridge ultimately used to harvest 50M+ Facebook profiles. Whistleblower Christopher Wylie testified to UK Parliament: "there were senior Palantir employees working on the Facebook data." Palantir denied, revised, denied again. Thiel was on Facebook's board throughout.

## The Business Model

Palantir does not accidentally end up in authoritarian contexts. The business model requires them. Every expansion of surveillance mandate — more deportations, more targeting, more patient data consolidation — is a revenue event. The Stephen Miller loop is not a side effect. It is the product.
