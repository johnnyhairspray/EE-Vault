# Oracle Corporation

Founded 1977, Austin, Texas (formerly Redwood, California). Co-founded by Larry Ellison, Bob Miner, and Ed Oates. The company was named after a CIA project. The CIA was its first customer. Neither of these facts is disputed — Oracle's own defense and intelligence page states: *"We started with the US Government as our first customer. Today, 1,000+ public sector organizations and 100% of federal cabinet agencies build, modernize, and innovate with Oracle."*

Oracle is not primarily a technology company in the consumer sense. It is **the infrastructure layer of the American state** — and by extension, of the states allied with it, contracted by it, and in some cases surveilled by it. As of 2025-2026, it is also becoming the infrastructure layer of American media, AI development, and post-war territorial governance.

## The Origin

**1977**: CIA issues a contract for a relational database under the codename **"Project Oracle."** Previous contractor's deal had lapsed. Larry Ellison and colleague Robert Miner, working at consulting firm Ampex, convinced the CIA to award them the project for $50,000. They founded **Software Development Laboratories** → **Relational Software Incorporated** → **Oracle Systems Corporation** — named directly after the CIA project.

This is not a historical footnote. The CIA as Oracle's founding customer established the template for everything that followed: a company whose commercial success was predicated on government intelligence and defense contracts, whose growth was subsidized by national security procurement, and whose leadership has never meaningfully separated the two domains.

**Board confirmation**: As of September 2025, Oracle's board includes **Leon Panetta** — former CIA Director and Secretary of Defense. The intelligence community relationship institutionalized at founding remains institutionalized at board level nearly five decades later.

## The Government Infrastructure Layer

Oracle's penetration of U.S. federal infrastructure is total:

- **100% of federal cabinet agencies** run on Oracle infrastructure
- **Top Secret/SCI cloud environments** for the Department of Defense and Intelligence Community
- **$9 billion JWCC military cloud contract** (Joint Warfighting Cloud Capability)
- **$222.53 million U.S. Army cloud deal** (2025)
- **$88 million Air Force contract** for Top Secret workloads (2026)
- **Oracle Health** (formerly Cerner, acquired $28.3B) — 25% of American hospitals, entire Veterans Affairs medical system, Department of Defense medical records
- **February 28, 2026**: Authorized to run AI on federal government data at the highest security levels — specifically Grok (xAI) on Oracle cloud processing Top Secret American intelligence data. This authorization was granted on the same day as [[Operation Epic Fury]]

David Carney, leader of Oracle's Information Assurance Center — founded two months after September 11, 2001 — was formerly the third-ranking official at the CIA, where he worked for 32 years.

## The Data Broker Apparatus

Between 2014 and 2024, Oracle built one of the most comprehensive private surveillance operations on the planet through a series of acquisitions:

- **BlueKai** — tracked browsing via invisible pixels on sites including Amazon and ESPN; linked browsing behavior to offline purchasing
- **Datalogix** — connected online behavior to credit card purchases and loyalty card data
- **AddThis** — the social sharing buttons embedded across the web; used device fingerprinting techniques ProPublica described as *"virtually impossible to block"* — found on both the White House website and YouPorn in the same sweep
- **Crosswise** — cross-device tracking

Oracle merged all acquisitions into the **Oracle Data Cloud**, building what a federal class-action lawsuit called *"digital dossiers"* on the public. The lawsuit, filed on behalf of *"worldwide Internet users who have been subject to Oracle's privacy violations,"* alleged Oracle collected data on more than half the global population without consent.

**2024**: $115 million FTC settlement for BlueKai privacy violations. The apparatus was nominally "shut down." Databases do not disappear after fines.

Total individual dossiers: approximately **5 billion people**.

[[Larry Ellison]], October 2024, to investors: Oracle's databases already contain *"most of the world's high-value private data"* and the company's entire future is running AI across it.

## Stargate

January 21, 2025 — one day after Trump's inauguration — Oracle joined OpenAI and SoftBank in the **Stargate LLC** joint venture: up to **$500 billion** in AI infrastructure investment by 2029, announced by Trump at a White House press conference. Oracle began immediate construction of AI-focused data centers. The OpenAI contract alone — approximately **$300 billion** in computing power over five years — made OpenAI Oracle's largest customer. Oracle shares jumped 36% on September 10, 2025 when the contract was disclosed, adding $89 billion to [[Larry Ellison]]'s net worth in a single session.

Stargate data centers are being built across the U.S. and internationally, including UAE Stargate (announced May 2025, opening 2026) through partnerships with G42, Nvidia, Cisco, and others.

## The Oracle-Palantir Convergence

**April 2024**: Oracle and [[Palantir Technologies]] announced a *"strategic partnership"* to deliver mission-critical AI solutions to governments and businesses. This was nearly a decade in the making — [[Larry Ellison]] held exploratory talks with [[Peter Thiel]] about **acquiring Palantir outright** in 2017.

**July 2024**: Palantir and Oracle jointly deployed Palantir's Foundry and AI platforms on Oracle's sovereign, government, and air-gapped clouds for national security clients.

**June 2025**: Oracle launched its **Defense Ecosystem** — including *"Palantir for Builders."*

The combined architecture: Oracle provides the cloud infrastructure and data layer; Palantir provides the analytical and targeting layer. Together they constitute the primary AI-surveillance stack of the U.S. national security state, deployed to allied governments, and embedded in the proposed [[Board of Peace]] Gaza governance framework.

## TikTok

January 2026: TikTok finalized a deal changing ownership of its U.S. operations. Oracle, MGX, and Silver Lake each acquired approximately 15% stakes. Oracle's role extends beyond ownership — it serves as **security provider overseeing TikTok's algorithms** for 170 million American users. Trump suggested making the algorithm *"100% MAGA."*

[[Benjamin Netanyahu]] told pro-Israel influencers in September 2025: *"Weapons change over time... the most important ones are on social media"* — describing TikTok's acquisition as *"the most important purchase going on right now."* The person overseeing TikTok's U.S. algorithm is Netanyahu's close friend and largest FIDF donor.

## The Media Empire

Oracle is the cloud infrastructure backbone for the Ellison family media consolidation. **David Ellison** (Larry's son) — through Paramount Skydance — completed a **$111 billion acquisition of Warner Bros. Discovery** on February 27, 2026, the day before Operation Epic Fury. The combined entity holds: HBO, Max, CNN, Warner Bros. Pictures, DC Comics, CBS, Paramount+, MTV, Nickelodeon, Showtime.

Paramount had already been migrating to Oracle cloud at $100 million/year. Oracle is listed as a *"neutral vendor"* in the merger filings. No data firewall between Oracle's federal, commercial, and media environments has been proposed or required.

[[Larry Ellison]] has donated or pledged at least **$350 million to the Tony Blair Institute for Global Change**. The leaked GITA (Gaza International Transitional Authority) procurement documents show its digital governance backbone — identity, border control, aid logistics — **matching the Oracle-Palantir technology stack** currently deployed in Israel's defense network.

Oracle's cloud infrastructure for the Gaza governance proposal is the same infrastructure that currently processes Top Secret U.S. government data, runs the Israeli military's operational software through Palantir's MoD partnership, and is proposed to manage the post-war population registry of Gaza. There is no data firewall between any of these environments.

## The China File

Oracle marketed **surveillance tools to Chinese police in Xinjiang** while simultaneously holding U.S. Top Secret contracts — during what the U.S. State Department has designated a genocide against Uyghur Muslims.

Oracle built **biometric ID systems for the United Kingdom** that Congress had banned from domestic deployment in the United States on civil liberties grounds. Oracle holds a **£710 million ten-year UK government contract** covering the Home Office, Department for Work and Pensions, Ministry of Justice, and Department for the Environment. Oracle earned **£100 million from the NHS** in 2025 alone.

"[[Larry Ellison]]" personally selected the location of Oracle's Beijing R&D center. By the early 2000s Oracle's database technology had become embedded in Chinese government infrastructure — including sensitive state security areas — because no quality domestic Chinese alternative existed and local officials treated Oracle as the gold standard. Oracle's "*Partner of the Year*" award went to "[[Digital China]]", a surveillance broker whose documented capabilities included IT maintenance for Chinese police and military, support for China's "[[1203 Project]]" (linking national security data infrastructure), and an integrated policing platform — all explicitly built to run on Oracle databases and operating systems. Georgetown's CSET reviewed the documentation: "*It clearly shows that Oracle did not shy away from providing crucial technological infrastructure to the Chinese party-state.*"

Ellison publicly criticized "[[Google]]" for "*facilitating the Chinese government surveilling their people*" during this same period. When The Intercept revealed Oracle employees had marketed analytics software to Chinese police and military-linked clients, Oracle's top lobbyist doxxed the reporter.

The 1203 Project, the integrated policing platform, the Digital China partnership — this is infrastructure provision, not consultation. But the practical distinction is narrow. Oracle's databases are the foundation on which Chinese state surveillance runs. Ellison selected the Beijing center location himself.
