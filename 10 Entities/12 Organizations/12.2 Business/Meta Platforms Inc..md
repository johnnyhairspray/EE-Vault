# Meta Platforms

Social media and technology company. Parent of Facebook, Instagram, WhatsApp, and Threads. Founded 2004 by Mark Zuckerberg; rebranded from Facebook to Meta in 2021. NYSE: META.

Peter Thiel was Meta's first outside investor — $500,000 for 10.2% in 2004. He sat on the board 2004–2022, advising against fact-checking political ads throughout. Left in May 2022.

## The Social Graph

Meta's core surveillance asset is the social graph — the map of human relationships, interests, behaviors, and emotional triggers constructed from billions of user interactions across its platforms.

Every interaction — likes, shares, comments, time-on-post, group membership, event attendance, cross-platform tracking via "Login with Facebook" — is a data point in a model that can predict political affiliation, religious belief, mental health status, purchasing behavior, and persuadability with documented accuracy.

"Login with Facebook" extends Meta's data collection beyond its own platforms — Meta can track user activity across third-party websites and apps that use its authentication system, even when users are not on Facebook itself.

## The Cambridge Analytica Case (2016–2018)

Cambridge Analytica harvested data from 87 million Facebook profiles — via a third-party app that exploited Facebook's API terms — to build psychographic targeting profiles for political advertising. The data was used in the 2016 Trump campaign and the Brexit referendum.

Peter Thiel was on Facebook's board during the data collection period. A Palantir employee was later reported to have helped Cambridge Analytica acquire and process the data. Thiel advised against fact-checking political ads throughout the scandal period.

The Cambridge Analytica case is the documented proof-of-concept that social graph data can be weaponized for political manipulation at scale.

## The Emotional Contagion Experiment (2014)

Without user consent or disclosure, Facebook manipulated the News Feeds of 689,003 users to study whether emotional content in feeds could influence users' own emotional expression. Published as an academic paper. Later acknowledged as an ethical violation. Peter Thiel was on the board.

The experiment established that Meta's algorithmic control over information exposure could be used to manipulate emotional states across a population — with or without user awareness.

## The 2026 Creator Pivot

Under legal and regulatory pressure in Europe and U.S., Meta shifted its content monetization toward explicit creator payments for high-engagement content — compensating creators based on interaction metrics rather than solely advertising. The structural effect: financial incentives for emotional, divisive, and high-arousal content remain baked into the platform's economics.
