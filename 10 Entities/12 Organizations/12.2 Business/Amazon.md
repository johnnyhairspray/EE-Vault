# Amazon

E-commerce and cloud computing company founded 1994 by Jeff Bezos. Parent company of [[AWS]], Ring, Whole Foods, and numerous subsidiaries. One of the five largest companies by market capitalization globally.

## Government Infrastructure ($50B Expansion)

In late 2025, Amazon announced a $50 billion investment to add approximately 1.3 gigawatts of AI and supercomputing capacity across its classified and government cloud environments — AWS Top Secret, AWS Secret, and AWS GovCloud — to support national security AI missions. This expansion positions Amazon as the primary physical infrastructure provider for the intelligence community's AI workloads.

## Amazon One (Biometric Shutdown)

Amazon One — a palm-scanning payment system deployed at Amazon stores, Whole Foods, and third-party retailers — announced it would discontinue services at all retail businesses as of June 3, 2026. Per Amazon's FAQ, all biometric user data including palm prints will be automatically deleted after shutdown.

The shutdown is notable: Amazon collected palm biometric data from millions of retail customers, linked to payment profiles and Amazon accounts. The data was collected under commercial terms, not as a surveillance program — but represented one of the largest civilian biometric databases assembled by a private retailer.

## Amazon Leo (Satellite Internet)

Project Kuiper, rebranded to Amazon Leo, is Amazon's low-earth orbit satellite internet constellation — competing directly with SpaceX's Starlink. As of early 2026, Amazon faced an FCC deadline to deploy 1,618 satellites by July 30, 2026, with launch delays creating compliance risk.

## Ring / Surveillance Infrastructure

Ring — Amazon's home security camera subsidiary — represents the largest privately-operated residential surveillance network in the United States. Ring's documented relationships with law enforcement agencies (1,800+ police partnerships at peak) created a network where private home cameras feed law enforcement requests without warrants. Amazon has tightened these policies under legal pressure but the infrastructure remains.
