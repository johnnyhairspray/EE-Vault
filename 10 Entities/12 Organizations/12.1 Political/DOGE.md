# DOGE (Department of Government Efficiency)

Established by executive order January 20, 2025 — Trump's first day of his second term. Formally titled the U.S. DOGE Service, a rebranded version of the U.S. Digital Service operating within the Executive Office of the President. [[Elon Musk]] appointed as de facto head in the capacity of Special Government Employee — a designation limiting him to 130 days of service per year.

Not a government agency in the traditional sense. A parallel power structure embedded within the executive branch, staffed by Musk loyalists and former [[Palantir Technologies]] employees, designed to dismantle the federal civil service from the inside.

## The Ideological Architecture

DOGE is the operational implementation of [[Curtis Yarvin]]'s **RAGE** framework — Retire All Government Employees — proposed as early as 2012. Yarvin's blueprint: a "hard reboot" of the federal government via mass civil service termination, replacement of career employees with political loyalists, centralization of power in the executive, and defiance of courts and Congress when they obstruct.

The Washington Post confirmed in May 2025 that Yarvin is an intellectual beacon for DOGE, with two DOGE advisers stating: **"It's an open secret that everyone in policymaking roles has read Yarvin."** And: **"Yarvin is the brain."**

The lineage is layered:

- **[[Curtis Yarvin]]** theorizes RAGE and the "butterfly revolution" — mass government purge followed by CEO-dictator consolidation
- **[[Peter Thiel]]** funds Yarvin's company, calls him "house philosopher," installs [[10 Entities/11 People/11.1 Political/JD Vance]] as political vehicle
- **[[10 Entities/11 People/11.1 Political/JD Vance]]** publicly cites Yarvin's influence, attends Yarvin's inner circle events, implements neocameralist-adjacent governance
- **[[Elon Musk]]** operationalizes RAGE as DOGE — mass firings, loyalty replacement, data seizure, court defiance
- **[[Joshua Haldeman]]** — Musk's grandfather, Technocracy Inc. Canada organizer — provides the ancestral ideological substrate: replace democratic governance with technical efficiency managed by unelected experts

[[Schedule F]], authored through [[Project 2025]] / [[The Heritage Foundation]], provides the legal scaffolding for replacing career civil servants with political appointees — the personnel mechanism DOGE requires.

Musk himself in 2020: **"Government is simply the largest corporation."** Yarvin's formalism, word for word.

## What DOGE Did

In under 130 days, DOGE:
- Eliminated 250,000+ federal jobs across agencies
- Attempted to dismantle USAID entirely (blocked by federal court)
- Seized access to Treasury Department payment systems, IRS databases, Social Security Administration records
- Fired 177 [[NNSA]] employees — including those at [[Sandia National Laboratories]], the lead design agent for the [[C-HGB]] — then reversed, rehiring all but approximately 27
- Built a master federal database aggregating sensitive personal data to speed immigration enforcement
- Staffed agencies with young, inexperienced loyalists — "special government employees" owing allegiance to Musk/Thiel networks, not democratic institutions
- Installed former Palantir employees in key positions across multiple agencies

## The Yarvin Timeline: Support → Disappointment

**January 2025 — Inauguration:**
Yarvin attends multiple exclusive inaugural events, including parties at Thiel's Washington mansion. Spotted with Grimes. Has lunch with Michael Anton (NSC). Tells Politico that Vance is "perfect" for executing his plans. Posts approvingly of the administration's first days in "The Pleasure of Error."

**March 2025 — First Doubts:**
Yarvin grades the Trump administration **C-minus**. Describes DOGE as insufficiently radical — too chaotic, not ideologically coherent. Compares DOGE operatives to **"a chimpanzee orchestra trying to play Wagner"** and **"an incel trying to chat up a woman."**

When told by a fan that Musk was "fulfilling the neo-reactionary dream," Yarvin responds: **"I wish."**

**May 2025 — Washington Post Exposé:**
The Post documents the Yarvin-DOGE connection, confirming his role as intellectual architect. Yarvin publicly scorns the implementation. His critique: DOGE was insufficiently radical on one hand and operationally inept on the other. For Yarvin, a true reboot requires thorough repudiation of the existing regime — not chaotic slash-and-burn that courts legal defeat and creates political liability.

**May 28-30, 2025 — Musk Departs:**
Musk's 130-day Special Government Employee term ends. His departure coincides with his public break with Trump over the One Big Beautiful Bill, which he criticizes as expanding the deficit. Trump gives Musk a ceremonial key. Musk posts: *"The DOGE mission will only strengthen over time as it becomes a way of life throughout the government."*

**The Correlation:**
Yarvin's disillusionment peaks in March–May 2025. Musk departs in late May 2025. The ideological architect declares the execution a failure precisely as the executor withdraws. Whether this is coincidence, convergence, or signal depends on how you read the network.

Yarvin's diagnosis is structurally significant regardless: **DOGE failed not because it went too far, but because it lacked the philosophical coherence to go far enough.** The RAGE blueprint called for a complete replacement of the state apparatus. DOGE produced chaos, lawsuits, and a 71% drop in Tesla profits — while leaving the underlying power structures largely intact.
