# Air Force Research Laboratory (AFRL)

The U.S. Air Force's primary research and development organization. Located at Wright-Patterson Air Force Base, Dayton, Ohio. Founded 1997 through the consolidation of four separate Air Force laboratories.

Mission: discover, develop, and deliver warfighting technologies for the Air Force and Space Force. Annual budget approximately $2.2B+. Encompasses nine technology directorates including materials, propulsion, munitions, sensors, and space vehicles.

## The Mondaloy Connection

The AFRL Space Vehicles Directorate — commanded by [[William McCasland]] from 2001 to 2004 — funded [[Monica Reza]]'s [[Mondaloy]] superalloy research at Aerojet Rocketdyne. McCasland's oversight of the Space Vehicles Directorate established him as the funding authority connecting Reza's materials science work to the operational [[C-HGB]] weapons program.

McCasland later served as Director of Special Programs at the Pentagon — the node connecting AFRL's classified research to operational deployment.
