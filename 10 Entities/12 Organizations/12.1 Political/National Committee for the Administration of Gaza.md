# National Committee for the Administration of Gaza (NCAG)

12-to-15-member Palestinian technocratic body appointed January 2026 to manage civil affairs of the Gaza Strip during the transitional phase under the Board of Peace framework. Chaired by Ali Shaath, a civil engineer and former Palestinian Authority deputy minister.

## Structure

The committee is organized into portfolio-based roles deliberately designed to bypass partisan political structures:
- **Security/Interior:** Sami Nasman (veteran PA officer, documented Hamas critic)
- **Economy/Industry:** Aed Abu Ramadan
- **Health:** Aed Yaghi

## The Cairo Problem

As of April 2026, most committee members remain based in Cairo — coordinating aid entry and administrative planning while the ISF works to secure operational zones that would allow their physical entry into Gaza. A governing body that cannot physically operate in its governed territory is structurally dependent on the ISF (and by extension the Board of Peace) for its own access.

## The Governance Architecture

The NCAG is the civilian administrative layer of the Board of Peace's Gaza governance framework:

**Board of Peace** (supreme authority, Trump chairman for life) → **ISF** (military security layer) → **NCAG** (civil administration) → **Palestinian Police Force** (local enforcement, ISF-trained)

The NCAG has no independent authority — it operates within the mandate set by the Board of Peace and is dependent on the ISF for physical security and access. Its "technocratic" framing depoliticizes what is effectively appointed administration by an unaccountable international body.

The Yarvin/neocameralist parallel: the NCAG is the "administrative staff" of the Gaza sovcorp — professional managers executing policy set by the sovereign (the Board).
