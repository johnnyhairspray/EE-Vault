## The Federalist Society

**Founded:** 1982 at Yale Law School by Steven Calabresi, Lee Liberman Otis, and David McIntosh as a student debate organization.

**Stated mission:** Promoting originalism and textualism in legal interpretation — the view that the Constitution means what it meant when written, and statutes mean what their text literally says.

**Actual function:** A 45-year pipeline that has pre-vetted, credentialed, networked, and delivered every significant conservative federal judicial appointment in the modern era.

**Scale:** 70,000+ members across law schools, the practicing bar, the judiciary, and government. Chapters at every major law school. Annual revenue exceeding $20 million. Alumni include six current Supreme Court justices.

### How the Pipeline Works

1. Law students join Federalist Society chapters — signals ideological reliability early
2. Clerkships with conservative judges are facilitated through the network
3. Members gain visibility through speaking circuits, publications, and conferences
4. When Republican administrations staff judicial appointments, the Federalist Society list is the primary source
5. Nominees have already been pre-vetted for ideological purity through years of network participation

This is not a conspiracy — it is a documented, openly acknowledged system. What distinguishes it is the **comprehensiveness** of the pipeline and the **discipline** of the ideological vetting. The American Bar Association evaluates competence. The Federalist Society evaluates loyalty to a specific legal philosophy — and that philosophy has been the dominant filter for Republican appointments for 40 years.
