# Bilderberg Group

Annual private forum established 1954 to foster dialogue between European and North American elites. 120-150 participants per meeting. Named after the Hotel de Bilderberg in Oosterbeek, Netherlands, where the first meeting was held May 29-31, 1954.

**Stated purpose:** Promote Atlanticism, strengthen US-European relations, prevent another world war. Current stated theme: "bolster a consensus around free market Western capitalism and its interests around the globe."

**Actual function:** The most significant annual off-the-record coordination mechanism between Western political leadership, corporate capital, financial institutions, intelligence officials, and media. No votes, no resolutions, no transcripts. Chatham House Rule — participants can use information gained but cannot attribute statements to specific individuals.

---

## Structure

- **Annual meeting:** 3-4 days, different location each year, rotating between Europe and North America
- **Attendance:** 120-150 by invitation only; mix of regulars (steering committee) and rotating participants
- **Chairman:** Henri de Castries (French businessman, former AXA CEO)
- **Steering committee:** ~36 members, two per nation from approximately 18 countries
- **Funding:** Participant organizations and corporations; not publicly disclosed in detail

The group has no formal membership category. The only official category is "member of the steering committee." Former participants receive annual conference reports in which speakers are identified only by country.

## Historical Significance

**Founded:** Prince Bernhard of the Netherlands, with CIA funding assistance (documented), in the context of Cold War Atlantic coordination.

**The euro:** Former chairman Etienne Davignon stated that Bilderberg meetings "facilitated the creation of the euro" — the most significant structural economic achievement attributed to the group.

**The admission:** Denis Healey, a founding member and steering committee member for 30 years, stated in 2001: "To say we were striving for a one-world government is exaggerated, but not wholly unfair." This is the most candid public statement from any participant about the group's actual ambitions.

**Leadership pipeline:** Research by Lukas Kantor documented that Bilderberg attendance functions as a springboard for leadership in international institutions. At the time of his 2023 research:
- **UN Secretary-General:** António Guterres — Bilderberg participant
- **IMF Managing Director:** Kristalina Georgieva — Bilderberg participant
- **NATO Secretary-General:** Jens Stoltenberg — Bilderberg participant (succeeded by Mark Rutte, also a Bilderberg regular)
- **EU Commission President:** Ursula von der Leyen — Bilderberg participant

The heads of all four major Western multilateral institutions simultaneously had Bilderberg connections. This is not coincidence — it is the pipeline functioning as designed.
