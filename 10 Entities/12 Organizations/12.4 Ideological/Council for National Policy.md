# Council for National Policy (CNP)

## What It Is

The Council for National Policy is the American conservative movement's private coordination body — the switchboard that connects donors, political operatives, religious right organizations, think tanks, and media figures three times per year at undisclosed locations, under strict secrecy rules, outside any public accountability.

Anne Nelson's *Shadow Network: Media, Money, and the Secret Hub of the Radical Right* (2019) describes it as connecting "the manpower and media of the Christian right with the finances of Western plutocrats and the strategy of right-wing Republican political operatives."

The *New York Times* called it "a little-known club of a few hundred of the most powerful conservatives in the country."

Members are explicitly instructed not to reveal their membership or even name the organization. It is not registered as a lobbying group. It does not publish its agenda or attendee lists. Its 501(c)(3) tax status was revoked by the IRS in 1992 for not operating for public benefit — and later restored. The investigative outlet **Documented** has obtained multiple leaked membership lists and recordings from private meetings that form the evidentiary basis for this note.

---

## Origins

**Founded 1981** by **Tim LaHaye** — Southern Baptist pastor, author of *The Battle for the Mind* (1980) and the *Left Behind* apocalyptic novel series. LaHaye's explicit goal: build an organizational infrastructure that could translate evangelical Christian political energy into sustained movement power, connecting wealthy donors with political operatives and movement organizations.

Early participants included: Paul Weyrich (Heritage Foundation co-founder), Phyllis Schlafly, Richard Viguerie (direct mail pioneer), Morton Blackwell, Pat Robertson, Jerry Falwell, Edwin Meese, Oliver North.

The founding context: the Reagan coalition needed a private coordination mechanism that could operate across the fragmented conservative movement — religious right, libertarian donors, neoconservatives, and Republican operatives — without the institutional constraints of formal organizations or the visibility of public advocacy.

**Ronald Reagan** spoke at CNP's 10th anniversary celebration. Every Republican presidential candidate since has sought CNP's blessing — George W. Bush's 1999 speech to CNP is credited with securing conservative movement support for his 2000 campaign. The content of that speech has never been released.

## Structure and Operations

**Meetings:** Three times per year at undisclosed locations. Closed to public and press. Members may bring guests only with unanimous executive committee approval.

**Secrecy norm:** Members instructed not to name the organization in public. Internal documents describe the secrecy as protecting "free-flowing exchange of ideas" from "liberal media bias." The practical effect is that coordination can occur without public accountability.

**Legal structure:** 501(c)(3) nonprofit (CNP) plus a 501(c)(4) lobbying arm (CNP Action). The (c)(3) cannot legally participate in electoral campaigns. Documented recordings show it does anyway.

**Size:** Approximately 400-600 members at any given time, drawn from leadership positions across conservative organizations, major donors, Republican politicians, religious leaders, and media figures.

**Function:** Not primarily a funding organization — CNP's own budget is relatively modest. Its function is **coordination**: pre-aligning strategy, introducing donors to operatives, building personal relationships that enable later collaboration, and providing a private forum where the movement's direction is set before it becomes public.

## January 6 — Documented

The CNP's role in the January 6 Capitol attack is the most significant recent documented activity and the strongest evidence that the organization operates beyond the bounds of its stated 501(c)(3) purpose.

**Pre-attack:**
- CNP members **Jenny Beth Martin**, **Charlie Kirk**, and **Ginni Thomas** publicly promoted the January 6 "Stop the Steal" rally in advance
- **Ali Alexander** — former CNP member — was a lead organizer of the event
- **Michael Flynn** — listed on CNP staff roster — gave an address at the rally saluting QAnon supporters
- CNP recordings obtained by Documented show the organization planning to "protect President Trump" and coordinating with Trump campaign allies on election contestation strategy as early as 2019

**Financial trail:**
- Ginni Thomas remained on the CNP Action board of directors through 2020 as the organization promoted election fraud conspiracies, urged Republican state legislators to reject Biden's win, and pressed Congress to count only Trump electors
- **Leonard Leo** donated to CNP Action in 2020 through his dark money network
- **DonorsTrust** — the dark money pass-through already in the vault as Leo's financial infrastructure — gave $300,000 to CNP in the same period

**Congressional accountability:**
- The January 6 House Select Committee subpoenaed CNP election expert **Cleta Mitchell**
- The committee examined 29 texts between Mark Meadows and Ginni Thomas (CNP Action board member) supporting Trump's election overturn effort
- The **Conservative Partnership Institute** — which employed Meadows after he left the White House — is closely tied to CNP; its chairman, president/CEO, senior legal partner, and senior director of policy are all prominent CNP members

## The Ginni Thomas / Leonard Leo Dark Money Scheme

Senate Judiciary Committee Chairman Dick Durbin formally documented a financial scheme involving Ginni Thomas, Leonard Leo, and major donors targeting the Supreme Court:

- In 2009, Leo helped Thomas create **Liberty Central** — a Tea Party nonprofit — with Leo as founding board member and Thomas as president
- The scheme was designed to capitalize on the anticipated *Citizens United* decision (which Leo's judicial network was simultaneously working to produce through Supreme Court appointments)
- Thomas and Leo coordinated with major conservative donors to build financial infrastructure that would influence the Court Leo was simultaneously shaping through judicial appointments

The circular logic: Leo selects the justices → justices rule in *Citizens United* → Citizens United enables unlimited dark money → dark money funds Leo's network and Ginni Thomas's organizations → Leo selects more justices.

## The Project 2025 Bridge — Verified

The immediate past President of the Council for National Policy is listed as a contributor in Project 2025's Mandate for Leadership, with board service spanning Heritage Foundation, American Conservative Union, and AEI.

The personnel overlap between CNP and Project 2025 is extensive. CNP functions as the private forum where the movement's direction is set; Project 2025 is the public policy implementation of that direction. They are not the same organization — CNP pre-dates and is broader than P2025 — but they are the same network expressing itself in different registers: private coordination (CNP) and public policy blueprint (P2025).

The Conservative Partnership Institute, described by Axios as a prime architect of "Trump 2025," is effectively a CNP subsidiary — its leadership drawn entirely from CNP membership.

## The Analytical Frame

CNP is best understood not as a conspiracy but as what Anne Nelson calls the "nerve center" — the place where the movement's fragmented actors (donors, operatives, religious leaders, media figures, political officials) achieve coordination without formal institutional structure.

The New Republic's critical reading is worth noting: CNP's influence may be overstated as a *cause* versus a *symptom* — many of its members would coordinate anyway through other channels. But the counterargument is that the private forum itself is the value: it creates relationships, aligns strategy, and enables coordination at a density and speed that wouldn't occur through public channels.

What's not debatable: CNP members were directly involved in planning and promoting January 6. CNP members fund and staff Project 2025. CNP members sit on the Supreme Court's social and financial network. CNP members drafted the executive orders implemented in Trump's second term.

Whether CNP *caused* any of this or merely *hosted* the people who did is a meaningful analytical distinction. The people are real. The meetings happened. The money flowed. The outcomes are documented.
