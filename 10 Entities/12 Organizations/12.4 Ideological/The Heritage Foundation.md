# The Heritage Foundation

## What It Is

The Heritage Foundation is the most influential conservative think tank in American history — and the most direct institutional product of the Powell Memo. Founded February 16, 1973, less than two years after Powell wrote his blueprint calling for exactly this kind of institution. It is not primarily a research organization. It is a **policy factory**: it produces the intellectual infrastructure — papers, personnel, policy briefs, personnel directories — that conservative administrations implement as governance.

Heritage's self-description: "a research and educational institution — a think tank — whose mission is to formulate and promote conservative public policies based on the principles of free enterprise, limited government, individual freedom, traditional American values, and a strong national defense."

The operational reality: Heritage writes the policy that Republican administrations implement, trains the personnel who staff those administrations, coordinates the donor networks that fund the movement, and provides the intellectual cover that makes extraction look like principle.

---

## Founding

**Paul Weyrich** — a Catholic political operative who would go on to found the Council for National Policy (1981) and coin the term "moral majority" — is the primary architect. He identified the gap Powell described: business needed a rapid-response policy institution that could move faster than academic think tanks and more aggressively than the Chamber of Commerce.

**Edwin Feulner** — Heritage's president from 1977 to 2013, the longest-serving and most consequential. Under Feulner, Heritage transformed from a small policy shop into the dominant institution of the American conservative movement.

**Joseph Coors** — Colorado beer magnate, the initial funder. Provided the seed capital that allowed Weyrich and Feulner to build the institution. Coors is the embodiment of the corporate donor Powell called for — a businessman who understood that long-term political investment was more valuable than short-term lobbying.

**Richard Mellon Scaife** — the second major early funder. The Scaife Foundations provided tens of millions over decades. Scaife's money also funded the network of state-level Heritage clones (the State Policy Network) that extend Heritage's influence to every statehouse in America.

---

## The Model Innovation: Speed and Advocacy

Heritage's founding innovation was explicitly political and strategic rather than academic. Where traditional think tanks like Brookings produced long-form research over years, Heritage produced short, aggressive policy briefs — "Backgrounders" — designed to land on a congressman's desk the morning of a vote.

Weyrich's explicit goal: "We are not in the business of providing smart men with quality control. We are in the business of providing politicians with ammunition."

This is Powell's prescription made operational: not scholarship for its own sake, but scholarship as a weapon in a political war.

## The Mandate for Leadership

Heritage's most significant product: **Mandate for Leadership**, first published in 1981 as a 1,093-page governing blueprint for the incoming Reagan administration. Reagan distributed it to every cabinet member. Approximately 60% of its recommendations were implemented within Reagan's first year.

**Mandate for Leadership 2025** — updated for Project 2025. The document that every Project 2025 analysis in this vault references is Heritage's direct descendant of its 1981 original. The same institution, the same model, fifty years later.

Project 2025's Advisory Board is essentially the Heritage alumni network expanded to include every major institution in the Powell-built infrastructure. Heritage is the backbone. See [[Project 2025]].

## The Personnel Pipeline

Heritage doesn't just produce policy — it produces **people**. The Heritage alumni network is the most comprehensive personnel pipeline in American conservative politics:

- Heritage alumni have served in every Republican administration since Reagan
- The Trump 1.0 administration placed 66 Heritage alumni in senior positions
- The Trump 2.0 administration's personnel were substantially sourced from Heritage's personnel database — built specifically for this purpose
- Heritage maintains a database of vetted, pre-screened conservative personnel ready for government placement

This is Powell's court strategy extended to the entire executive branch: not just judges, but every agency, every department, every deputy secretary.

---

## The State Policy Network

Heritage's nationwide extension: the **State Policy Network (SPN)**, a network of 64 state-level think tanks operating in all 50 states, all ideologically aligned with Heritage, all funded through the same donor networks (including DonorsTrust as the anonymous pass-through).

SPN institutions produce state-level Mandate for Leadership equivalents, supply personnel to state governments, and coordinate ALEC (American Legislative Exchange Council) — the organization that drafts model legislation for Republican state legislators.

The Powell Memo's blueprint operated at the federal level. SPN extends it to every statehouse in America.

## The Cathedral Capture Frame

Heritage is the clearest institutional expression of the Powell Memo's Cathedral capture strategy.

Powell identified the university, the media, and the courts as the Cathedral's institutional pillars — the places where legitimate knowledge and legitimate policy were produced. His prescription: don't exit these institutions; seize them or build parallel institutions that perform the same legitimizing function.

Heritage performs the legitimizing function of a university research institution — peer review replaced by ideological review, scholarship replaced by advocacy — while occupying the same cultural role. When Heritage produces a paper, it is treated as research. When Heritage alumni testify before Congress, they are treated as experts. The institution operates with the authority of scholarship while serving the function of advocacy.

This is Cathedral capture: the institutions that legitimize knowledge are occupied by the movement that Powell built, and those institutions now legitimize the movement's goals as neutral expertise.

## Connection to the Extraction Engine

Heritage's most consequential work, in terms of the Extraction Engine's operation, has been **regulatory capture and elimination**:

- Opposition to the CFPB since its founding (weakening the consumer protection agency that documents the poverty premium)
- Support for payday lending deregulation
- Opposition to pharmaceutical price negotiation
- Support for arbitration clauses eliminating class action lawsuits
- Support for right-to-work legislation weakening collective bargaining
- Chevron deference elimination (Loper Bright 2024) — removing the regulatory interpretation authority that protected consumers

Each of these positions appears in Heritage publications as principled free market advocacy. Each of them, in practice, expands the operating space of the extraction mechanisms documented in [[The Extraction Engine]].

## Current Significance (2025-2026)

Heritage is not historical. It is the governing infrastructure of the current administration:

- Kevin Roberts (Heritage president since 2021) is the architect of Project 2025
- Heritage personnel staff key positions across the Trump 2.0 administration
- Mandate for Leadership 2025 is the explicit policy document being implemented
- Heritage's donor network — routed through DonorsTrust — funds the CNP, the Federalist Society network, and the state-level infrastructure

The Powell Memo's 1971 prescription is being fully implemented in 2025-2026 with Heritage as the coordinating institution.
