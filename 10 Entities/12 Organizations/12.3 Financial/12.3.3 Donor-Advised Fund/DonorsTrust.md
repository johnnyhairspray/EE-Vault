# DonorsTrust

Donor-advised fund (DAF) founded 1999, headquartered in Alexandria, Virginia. Self-described as a *"philanthropic partner for the conservative and libertarian-minded."* Net assets: **$1.36 billion** (end of 2024). Total grants distributed: **$284.1 million in 2024** — its second-largest year since founding. Total distributed since 2020: approximately **$612 million**.

Mother Jones (2013) named DonorsTrust and its affiliate **Donors Capital Fund** *"the dark money ATM of the right."* That description has only become more accurate. DonorsTrust is the primary **anonymity mechanism** of the conservative funding network — the structural layer that allows wealthy donors to route money into political causes while breaking the traceable chain between individual donor and recipient organization.

## What a Donor-Advised Fund Does

A DAF accepts donations from individuals or foundations, pools them, and distributes them as grants to nonprofit organizations. The critical feature: **federal law does not require DAFs to name their individual donors in public filings.** Recipient organizations appear on tax returns. Donor identities do not.

This creates a two-hop anonymization:
1. A known entity (Thiel Foundation, Mercer Family Foundation, Leonard Leo's 85 Fund) donates to DonorsTrust — this hop IS traceable in tax filings
2. DonorsTrust distributes to recipient organizations — this hop IS traceable
3. But the chain from specific wealthy individual → DonorsTrust → recipient organization requires piecing together multiple 990 filings across years, and real-time tracing is nearly impossible

**In 2024**: Eight unnamed individuals made contributions totaling **$162.4 million** to DonorsTrust — a combination of seven-figure and eight-figure donations. Their identities are not publicly disclosed.

## The Funding Network

**Leonard Leo / 85 Fund**: DonorsTrust received **more than $181 million from Leo-backed groups** between 2019 and 2022. In 2022 alone, Leo's **85 Fund** contributed **$92 million** to DonorsTrust — its single largest contribution from any source. Leo is the primary architect of the conservative legal infrastructure and the man most responsible for reshaping the Supreme Court.

**Peter Thiel / Thiel Foundation**: Tax filings show **$10 million+** donated by the Thiel Foundation to DonorsTrust between 2020 and 2022. This money flows through DonorsTrust into [[Project 2025]] advisory board organizations and the broader conservative infrastructure network.

**Rebekah Mercer / Mercer Family Foundation**: Since 2016, the Mercer Family Foundation has transferred **more than $60 million in assets** to DonorsTrust — ensuring, as one analysis noted, that *"future philanthropic donations from the Mercer family will be untraceable."* The Mercers were major investors in Cambridge Analytica.

**Charles Koch / Koch Network**: Koch-linked foundations are major DonorsTrust contributors, going back decades. DonorsTrust describes itself as the *"preferred donor advised fund of the Koch political network."*

## The Vault Significance

DonorsTrust is not primarily about any single donation or recipient. It is the **infrastructure layer of the right-wing funding network** — the mechanism that makes coordinated large-scale dark money movement possible while maintaining plausible deniability about specific donor intent.

The Thiel-to-Project-2025 money trail requires the following chain:
**Thiel Foundation → DonorsTrust → Project 2025 advisory organizations**

At each hop the trail is technically traceable through 990 filings. In practice, the time lag between filing and public availability, the volume of DonorsTrust's grantmaking (300+ organizations annually), and the aggregation of multiple donors' funds make real-time accountability nearly impossible.

This is the financial equivalent of [[Persona]]'s clean room architecture: data (money) flows through a technically transparent but practically opaque intermediary, emerging at the other end without a visible individual donor fingerprint.

**The 2024 surge** is analytically significant: DonorsTrust's grants to America First Legal grew 565% in a single year, reaching $21.3 million. This is not organic growth — it is coordinated pre-election and post-election deployment of accumulated capital into Miller's litigation infrastructure at the moment of maximum political leverage. The donors who made this happen are the eight unnamed individuals who contributed $162.4 million in 2024.
