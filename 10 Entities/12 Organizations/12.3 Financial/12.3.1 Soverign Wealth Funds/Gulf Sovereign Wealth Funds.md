# Gulf Sovereign Wealth Funds

The capital layer above the individual billionaires documented elsewhere in this vault. Saudi Arabia, the UAE, Qatar, and Kuwait collectively manage sovereign wealth funds worth **over $4 trillion**. When these entities enter a market they do not tiptoe — they reshape it. They are the limited partners behind the venture capital firms that fund the tech network that installed the current U.S. administration.

This is not one note about one fund. It is a structural observation about a capital layer that the vault's existing nodes — Thiel, Ellison, Andreessen, Musk — sit beneath.

## The Structural Position

The Gulf SWFs are not the top of a conspiracy. They are the top of a **capital stack**. Their money flows into venture funds as limited partner capital. Those venture funds back tech companies. Those tech companies fund politicians. Those politicians implement policies that create government contracts. Those contracts flow back to the tech companies. The Gulf SWFs collect returns.

They do not need to coordinate the downstream effects. They need only to deploy capital into the right vehicles and let the incentive structures do the work.

## Saudi Arabia: Public Investment Fund (PIF)

**Assets**: approximately $930 billion. Run by Crown Prince Mohammed bin Salman (MBS) as the primary financial vehicle for Vision 2030 — his project to diversify the Saudi economy away from oil dependency.

**The tech entry point**: PIF committed $45 billion to SoftBank's Vision Fund in 2016 — its single largest investment, which made it the anchor LP for the vehicle that reshaped global venture capital for a decade. SoftBank's Vision Fund invested in Uber, WeWork, DoorDash, Slack, ByteDance, and hundreds of others. The Saudi state was the foundational capital behind the tech boom of 2015-2020.

**The Silicon Valley relationship**: After the 2018 Jamal Khashoggi assassination, most tech CEOs publicly distanced themselves from Saudi investment. The relationship quietly resumed. PIF's venture arm Sanabil Investments disclosed portfolio stakes in approximately 40 venture and growth funds including **Andreessen Horowitz** — one of the most politically influential VC firms in Washington and the tech network most directly connected to the Trump administration.

**HUMAIN — the $40 billion AI fund**: Announced during Trump's visit to Riyadh in May 2025. Created in partnership with Andreessen Horowitz. Targets AI infrastructure, semiconductor supply chains, and advanced computing. Makes Saudi Arabia the world's largest sovereign AI investor. The same Andreessen Horowitz that backed Praxis, the Network State, and whose partners Marc Andreessen and Ben Horowitz were key lobbyists for Vance's VP selection — is now a partner in a $40 billion Saudi AI fund. **This is the money above the money.**

**The Trump-Saudi relationship**: Trump's first foreign trip in his second term was to Riyadh. The UAE followed with a $1.4 trillion 10-year investment commitment to the U.S. economy — announced after a meeting with Trump and dinner with Vance. Jared Kushner's Affinity Partners raised $2 billion from the Saudi PIF in 2021. LIV Golf. The relationship is not arms-length.

## UAE: The Abu Dhabi Complex

Three significant funds with combined assets of approximately **$1.5 trillion**:

**Abu Dhabi Investment Authority (ADIA)** — approximately $900 billion. One of the oldest and largest sovereign wealth funds globally. Broad diversified portfolio across asset classes.

**Mubadala Investment Company** — approximately $300 billion. More operationally active than ADIA, focused on technology, aerospace, and strategic sectors. **Foundational partner in MGX**, the UAE AI company that is also an investor in [[Stargate LLC]] — meaning Abu Dhabi sovereign capital is directly inside the Trump-Ellison-Altman AI infrastructure project.

**ADQ** — approximately $200 billion. Focuses on regional infrastructure and technology.

**G42** — Abu Dhabi-based AI company backed by Mubadala. Announced UAE Stargate in partnership with Oracle, SoftBank, OpenAI, Nvidia, and Cisco in May 2025. **Larry Ellison's Oracle** is building the AI data center infrastructure. **Elon Musk's xAI** provides the model. UAE sovereign capital provides the funding and the territory.

The UAE is also the jurisdiction where **NEOM** (Saudi) and the Gulf's broader techno-state experiments are most advanced — smart cities, AI governance, biometric population management. The governance models being tested in the Gulf are the governance models proposed for Gaza and piloted through [[Oracle Corporation]] and [[Palantir Technologies]].

## Qatar: Qatar Investment Authority (QIA)

**Assets**: approximately $475 billion. Less tech-focused than Saudi or UAE vehicles, but significant stakes across global financial assets. QIA's political significance: Qatar hosts the U.S. Central Command forward headquarters at Al Udeid Air Base while simultaneously maintaining relationships with Hamas and Iran. It is the diplomatic Switzerland of the Middle East — and its sovereign capital is woven through the same global financial networks as the other Gulf funds.
