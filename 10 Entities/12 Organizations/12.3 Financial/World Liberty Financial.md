# World Liberty Financial (WLF)

## Summary

The primary DeFi interface for the Trump family and financial infrastructure for the post-sovereign economy. WLF facilitates the tokenization of Real World Assets (RWA) and the issuance of **USD1**, a dollar-pegged stablecoin. In January 2026, WLF applied for a **U.S. National Banking License** via World Liberty Trust (Zach Witkoff, president and chairman) to formally bridge on-chain liquidity with the federal financial system.

**The Trump family extraction structure (documented):**
- Trump family entity **DT Marks DEFI LLC** (Trump 70%, family 30%) receives **75% of net proceeds** from WLFI token sales
- **75% of USD1 stablecoin interest income** flows to DT Marks
- By December 2025: Trump family had profited **$1 billion** from WLF, while holding $3 billion in unsold tokens
- Token sale total: $550 million raised from public

## The Convergence Logic

### 1. The RWA Piston

**Maldives Initiative (February 18, 2026):** WLF partnered with **Securitize** and **DarGlobal** to tokenize loan revenue interests in the **Trump International Hotel & Resort, Maldives**. Allows accredited investors to trade "Revenue Interest" tokens in a $500M+ physical asset — turning Trump real estate into liquid on-chain instruments.

**RWA Suite (2026):** Co-founder Zach Witkoff confirmed at Binance Blockchain Week in Dubai (January 2026) that WLF will launch tokenized products backed by **oil, gas, cotton, and timber** — all paired with USD1 as the settlement layer. Potential expansion into real estate.

This is the RWA pivot hypothesis in action: moving from treasury-backed stablecoin toward commodity and physical asset backing.

### 2. The Institutional Rail (BlackRock/Securitize)

**The BUIDL Bridge:** Through Securitize, WLF assets are compatible with **BlackRock's BUIDL fund**. Creates a liquidity rail where tokenized RWA can be redeemed for institutional-grade US Treasuries with near-zero settlement latency. Tokens issued under **Rule 506(c)** (Regulation D) — compliant but restricted to accredited investors.

This is the structural mechanism connecting WLF's on-chain infrastructure to the legacy financial system — with BlackRock as the institutional counterparty.

### 3. The Governance Gate (Node Tier)

Per March 2026 governance proposal, holders must stake **WLFI** for 180 days minimum to participate in governance:
- **Nodes (10M+ WLFI / ~$1M):** OTC Conversion Pathways — direct 1:1 interaction with licensed market makers for USD1
- **Super Nodes (50M+ WLFI):** "Priority Partnership" status — direct access to WLF business development team and executives (confirmed March 2026)

The $5M / 6-month threshold for guaranteed executive access is a documented pay-to-play structure in a company whose co-founder is the US Special Envoy to the Middle East.

## The Sovereign Loop

### USD1 Stablecoin

USD1 is 100% backed by cash and US Government Money Market Funds. As of April 2026: **$4.186 billion market cap**, ranked 6th largest stablecoin globally.

Revenue model: $100M+ annually in interest from backing assets, 75% flowing to DT Marks (Trump entity).

### Abu Dhabi Penetration

Days before Trump's inauguration, Tahnoun bin Zayed Al Nahyan's interests (via Aryam Investment 1) purchased **49% of WLF for $500 million**. Never disclosed publicly. Tahnoun's lieutenants (Martin Edelman, Peng Xiao of G42) placed on WLF board without disclosure. → See [[The Abu Dhabi Convergence]] for full transaction chain.

MGX (Abu Dhabi state-backed, Tahnoun) used **$2 billion in USD1** to finance its Binance investment — establishing USD1 as legitimate institutional settlement currency and driving market cap to $5B+.

### The Justin Sun Situation

Sun invested $30M+ in WLFI tokens in 2025 and was named a WLF advisor. Shortly after Trump took office, the SEC backed off its investigation into Sun's companies. WLF later announced USD1 integration with Sun's Tron blockchain.

**April 2026:** WLF froze 595 million of Justin Sun's unlocked tokens (~$107 million) and initiated legal proceedings against him. Sun publicly accused WLF of embedding a hidden "freeze" function to blacklist his wallet. WLF denies. The lawsuit creates significant public conflict between WLF and its largest disclosed investor — raising governance credibility concerns.

### Board of Peace Integration

USD1 designated as the payment instrument for the Board of Peace — the post-sovereign reconstruction body overseeing Gaza. Steve Witkoff is simultaneously WLF co-founder AND US Special Envoy to the Middle East AND BoP Executive Board member. → See [[The Sovcorp Overlay]].
