# Founders Fund

Venture capital firm co-founded by Peter Thiel in 2005. $17B+ in assets under management. Focus: "hard tech" — physical technology, defense, space, biotech — as opposed to software-only investments. The primary capital vehicle for Thiel's defense-tech and surveillance portfolio.

## Notable Portfolio

**[[Palantir Technologies]]:** Co-founded by Thiel; Founders Fund an early investor. The surveillance and targeting infrastructure company holding ICE, DoD, and CIA contracts.

**Anduril Industries:** Defense tech company founded by Palmer Luckey (Oculus VR founder). Autonomous surveillance towers, drones, and border security systems. Trae Stephens (Founders Fund partner) is an Anduril co-founder.

**SpaceX:** Early investor. Musk's rocket company now providing launch infrastructure for government satellites and the Starlink network.

**Varda Space Industries / Impulse Space:** In-space manufacturing and logistics — moving manufacturing off-Earth to avoid terrestrial regulatory frameworks.

**[[Persona]]:** Biometric identity verification company. See [[Persona]] vault note for the surveillance infrastructure significance.

## The Defense-Tech Strategy

Founders Fund's portfolio reflects a deliberate investment thesis: fund the companies that provide the physical infrastructure for state power — surveillance, weapons, logistics, space access — rather than the consumer internet companies that characterized the previous generation of Silicon Valley investment.

This thesis is operationally significant: Founders Fund doesn't just benefit from government contracts flowing to its portfolio companies. It shapes what technologies are available for governments to purchase by deciding which companies to fund.

## The Growth III Fund (2026)

$4.6 billion late-stage fund closed April 2025. Being deployed across the Stargate ecosystem and defense-tech scaling — ensuring Thiel's network maintains a hardware and infrastructure position in the AI buildout.
