# CIA (Central Intelligence Agency)

U.S. foreign intelligence agency established 1947 under the National Security Act. Headquartered in Langley, Virginia. Responsible for collecting and analyzing foreign intelligence.

## The Venture Capital Arm: In-Q-Tel

**[[In-Q-Tel]]** — the CIA's venture capital arm, established 1999 — is the documented mechanism by which the agency funds dual-use private sector technology to ensure intelligence community access. Key investments:

- **Palantir Technologies (2004):** $2M In-Q-Tel seed funding was Palantir's foundational capital. Peter Thiel added $30M in 2005. The CIA effectively seeded what became the dominant surveillance software platform for U.S. law enforcement and intelligence.
- Numerous other surveillance, biometric, AI, and data analytics companies across its portfolio

## Cloud Infrastructure: C2E and C2S

**C2S (Commercial Cloud Services, 2013):** AWS awarded the CIA's first major commercial cloud contract — establishing Amazon as the intelligence community's primary cloud provider.

**C2E (Commercial Cloud Enterprise, 2021+):** Expanded multi-vendor arrangement bringing in Oracle, Microsoft, and Google alongside AWS. Oracle's March 2026 Basic Ordering Agreement extended this to enable seamless data sharing between unclassified commercial data and Top Secret intelligence domains.

## Project Maven Integration

Project Maven — the Pentagon's AI targeting program — as of March 2026 has been fully transitioned into a "Program of Record" in collaboration with CIA and NGA. This locks Palantir's Gotham as the permanent AI-assisted targeting infrastructure for intelligence community operations, domestic and international.
