# Shin Bet (Israeli Security Agency)

Israel's internal security service, equivalent to a domestic intelligence agency. Founded 1949. Responsible for counter-terrorism, counter-espionage, and protection of senior government figures. Operates primarily within Israel and the occupied Palestinian territories.

## Gaza Biometric Infrastructure

Post-October 7, Shin Bet expanded its biometric surveillance infrastructure across Gaza through two documented programs:

**Blue Wolf:** A smartphone app used by IDF soldiers to photograph Palestinians and check their status against a facial recognition database. Soldiers competed to enroll the most individuals. The database — described by soldiers as seeking "Facebook for Palestinians" — contains biometric data on a significant portion of Gaza's population.

**Red Wolf:** A more advanced version of the same system, deployed at checkpoints. Real-time facial recognition cross-referenced against a database of individuals flagged for security attention.

These systems — combined with [[Unit 8200]]'s Lavender and Gospel AI targeting systems — create a comprehensive biometric surveillance and targeting infrastructure over a civilian population.

## The Board of Peace Connection

Shin Bet is positioned as the security vetting partner for the [[Board of Peace]]'s Gaza governance framework — responsible for screening members of any proposed Palestinian administrative structure, including a new Palestinian police force. This means Israel's internal security agency would function as the gatekeeper for who is permitted to participate in Gaza's post-war political institutions.

## October 7 Accountability

Director Ronen Bar faced significant pressure over intelligence failures preceding the October 7 attack, despite multiple documented warnings that were not acted upon. Lawfare and other oversight-focused publications documented ongoing questions about leadership failures and institutional accountability within Shin Bet in the aftermath.
