# ICE (U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement)

Federal law enforcement agency under the Department of Homeland Security. Established 2003 after DHS reorganization post-9/11. Responsible for immigration enforcement and customs investigations.

## The 2026 Detention Infrastructure Build-Out

Leaked internal planning documents (reported February 2026) revealed a $38.3 billion expansion plan — the "Detention Reengineering Initiative" — replacing local jail contracts with 34 federal-owned detention hubs:

- **16 Processing Centers:** Regional facilities holding 1,000–1,500 people for 3–7 days for biometric processing, medical screening, and initial immigration processing
- **8 Detention Complexes:** Large-scale hubs holding up to 10,000 people each, with custom infrastructure including video-court rooms for remote deportation hearings

Target: 1,000,000 removals per year. ICE daily arrest quotas set by [[Stephen Miller]] at 3,000/day in May 2025 — nearly triple previous levels. Agents directed to conduct operations at churches, schools, and courthouses.

As of January 2026, approximately 2 million people had self-deported under the enforcement pressure.

## ImmigrationOS (Palantir)

Palantir's ImmigrationOS — deployed under a $30M+ ICE contract — is an AI platform aggregating data from:
- IRS records
- Social Security filings
- Passport databases
- License plate readers (FlockOS)
- Commercial ad-tech location data (including Venntel)

The system creates automated enforcement targeting, flagging non-citizens for "database inconsistencies" or "tax reporting errors" and triggering enforcement actions. Stephen Miller holds $100,000–$250,000 in Palantir stock while overseeing the immigration policy ImmigrationOS executes — a documented conflict of interest.

## Commercial Location Data

ICE purchases commercially available location data from brokers including [[Venntel]] to track and identify targets before field operations. Confirmed under oath by FBI Director Kash Patel in Senate testimony March 18, 2026. This bypasses *Carpenter v. United States* (2018) warrant requirements — data purchased commercially from a broker is not subject to the same Fourth Amendment protections as carrier location data.

## HART (Homeland Advanced Recognition Technology)

ICE's biometric verification system — integrating facial recognition, iris, fingerprint, and other biometric data — fully integrated across ICE enforcement operations by 2026. Field agents use HIIDE devices to collect biometrics and match against the HART database and commercial vetting databases including LexID.
