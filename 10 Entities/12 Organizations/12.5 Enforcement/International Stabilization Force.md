# International Stabilization Force (ISF)

Multinational security force authorized by the [[Board of Peace]] in January 2026 to maintain order in Gaza and oversee disarmament of armed factions. Commanded by U.S. Major General Jasper Jeffers.

## Structure

**Projected strength:** 20,000 troops

**Troop contributors:** Indonesia, Morocco, Kazakhstan, and Albania — notably not NATO allies or regional Arab states with direct stakes in Palestinian governance

**Mandate:** Authorized to use "all necessary measures" to protect humanitarian corridors and the administrative hubs established under the National Committee for the Administration of Gaza (NCAG)

**Palestinian Police Force:** ISF is currently training a projected 12,000-strong Palestinian police force to take over neighborhood-level enforcement as IDF units withdraw sector by sector contingent on "de-radicalization milestones"

## The Governance Architecture

The ISF is the security layer of the Board of Peace's Gaza governance structure — the kinetic component of what the vault documents as a sovcorp governance model. The force does not report to the UN, to Palestinian authority, or to any democratically accountable body. It reports to the Board of Peace.

The sector-by-sector IDF withdrawal contingent on "de-radicalization milestones" determined by the Board of Peace means continued Israeli military presence can be extended indefinitely by simply not certifying milestones as met. The ISF fills the interim space.

The contributing countries — Indonesia, Morocco, Kazakhstan, Albania — were selected partly because they have no direct stake in Palestinian statehood and no leverage to demand accountability from the Board of Peace structure.
