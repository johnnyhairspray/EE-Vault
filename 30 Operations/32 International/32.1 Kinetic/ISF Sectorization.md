# ISF Sectorization (Gaza)

The operational division of the Gaza Strip into five designated security sectors under [[International Stabilization Force]] control. Part of the Board of Peace's phase-by-phase governance transition framework.

## Current Status (April 2026)

**IDF control:** The IDF maintains control over approximately 53% of the Gaza Strip — primarily the northern zone and the Netzarim Corridor — pending satisfaction of "weapons decommissioning milestones" determined by the Board of Peace.

**Sector Alpha (Rafah):** The first sector to undergo "civilianization" in early 2026. ISF is training a projected 12,000-person Palestinian police force to replace IDF units in Rafah on a sector-by-sector basis.

**Permanent ISF basing:** Plans for a 5,000-person permanent ISF military base in Gaza are under review by the Board of Peace. A permanent base would institutionalize the ISF's presence beyond a transitional peacekeeping function.

## The Governance Architecture

The sectorization plan maps onto the Board of Peace's governance model: IDF withdrawal from each sector is contingent on "de-radicalization milestones" that the Board assesses. The Board has no defined criteria for what constitutes a milestone achieved — meaning the withdrawal timeline is entirely within the Board's discretion.

The ISF reports to the Board of Peace, not to the UN, Palestinian Authority, or any democratically accountable body. The force providing security for Gaza's civilian population during its "reconstruction" is accountable only to a private governance structure chaired by Donald Trump for life.
