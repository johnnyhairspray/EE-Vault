# The Ruthless Review (JAG Purges)

## 🔍 Summary
Ordered by Secretary of Defense **[[Pete Hegseth]]**, the "Ruthless Review" is a systematic overhaul of the military's civilian and uniformed legal offices. Its primary goal is to ensure that Judge Advocate Generals (JAGs) provide "constitutional advice" that does not interfere with the Commander-in-Chief's operational directives.

## 🧬 The "Institutional" Piston
* **The Friday Night Blitz (Feb 2025):** Hegseth simultaneously fired the top three uniformed lawyers (The Judge Advocates General or TJAGs) of the Army, Navy, and Air Force. 
* **The 2026 Expansion:** On March 11, 2026, Hegseth directed a "no-excuses review" of all staff judge advocates down to the brigade level, effectively extending the purge to the entire operational legal tier.
* **Function Stripping:** The 2026 mandate directs JAGs to focus strictly on "procurement and fiscal law" while stripping their authority to review the legality of domestic deployments or rules of engagement.

## 🔗 The "Technate" Loop
* **Legal Friction Removal:** By removing senior JAGs—who are bound by professional codes and Senate confirmation—the administration has neutralized the internal "Rule of Law" guardrail that previously audited the use of the military in domestic **[[Mass Deportation]]** missions.
* **The "Loyalty" Vetting:** Replacements are vetted via **[[NSPM-7]]** to ensure they prioritize "Executive Will" over international law or traditional military justice precedents.
