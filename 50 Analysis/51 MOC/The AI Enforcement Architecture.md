# The AI Enforcement Architecture

## The Framing

The United States federal government has assembled an AI predictive policing system with human enforcers. This is not a future risk or a theoretical concern — it is the operational reality of DHS/ICE enforcement as of 2025-2026.

The architecture consists of four interlocking layers: **identification**, **prediction**, **tracking**, and **enforcement**. Each layer is populated by named, contracted, operational systems. The human enforcer — an ICE agent, CBP officer, or deputized local police — executes the output of an algorithmic chain they cannot audit and likely do not understand.

---

## The Four Layers

### Layer 1 — Identification: Who Gets Flagged

**[[Sparta]]** and the covert watchlist enterprise (Reaper, Grapevine, Bluekey, Hummingbird, Sandcastle, Sienna, Slipstream) — flags individuals as targets. Criteria include: final orders of removal, criminal charges, anti-ICE protest activity, pro-Palestinian activism, "Antifa" designation, filming ICE officers. Generates HEL-A and HEL-C classified reports. Operates as part of an "above Top Secret watchlisting enterprise" (confirmed by senior national security officials to Ken Klippenstein).

**Zignal Labs** — monitors 8 billion social media posts daily across Instagram, X, TikTok, Facebook, YouTube, and Reddit. Feeds behavioral profiles and association networks back into the identification layer.

**ICE Tip Processing (Palantir)** — AI generative system using Palantir software to summarize, categorize, and translate tips to ICE. Converts anonymous informant reports into actionable intelligence without human review of the underlying claim.

**Wolf Pack** — See [[Wolf Pack]] vault note. Enforcement coordination layer.

---

### Layer 2 — Prediction: Where Targets Are

**[[ELITE]]** (Enhanced Leads Identification & Targeting for Enforcement) — Palantir tool that ingests watchlist flags, Medicaid enrollment addresses (80 million patients), DMV records, utility bills, court records, and other data to generate:
- Per-person dossiers with photographs
- Address confidence scores (0-100)
- Geospatial maps of "target-rich areas" for raids

An ICE agent testified under oath ELITE works "kind of like Google Maps" — agents draw shapes on the map to select neighborhoods for enforcement operations. DHS classified ELITE as "not high-impact" in its AI Use Case Inventory because its outputs "do not serve as a principal basis for decisions or actions with legal, material, binding, or significant effects on individuals." This determination was made about a tool that tells agents which neighborhoods to raid.

**[[ImmigrationOS]]** — Palantir's $30M+ master operational platform. Provides near-real-time visibility into immigrant movements. Integrates data from across the DHS ecosystem. ELITE operates within or alongside ImmigrationOS as part of the same Palantir contract architecture.

---

### Layer 3 — Tracking: Real-Time Location

**[[Flock Cameras]]** — License plate readers generating continuous movement trails. Feeds into the Palantir operational layer.

**Venntel** — Commercial location data purchased without warrants. Smartphone location data acquired from data brokers, providing movement history without judicial process.

**PenLink / Webloc** — Location tracking, no-bid 2025 ICE contract.

**Mobile Fortify** — NEC NeoFace-powered facial recognition and fingerprint app deployed to ICE and CBP agents. Used 100,000+ times since June 2025. Scans against a database of 1.2 billion face photos including U.S. citizens photographed at airports. Photos retained 15 years regardless of match. No consent mechanism. No opt-out. Deployed without completed Privacy Impact Assessments. Can be used on anyone "regardless of citizenship or immigration status."

**Clearview AI** — 60 billion scraped photos. CBP contract for "tactical targeting" and "counter-network analysis." 15 software licenses at the National Targeting Center.

**Iris Scanning (BI2 Technologies)** — Mobile iris scanning via MORIS and I.R.I.S. systems. Database of 5 million iris records from 1.5 million people. Agents can scan someone's iris from 10-15 inches away using a smartphone.

---

### Layer 4 — Enforcement: Human Executors

**ICE Enforcement and Removal Operations (ERO)** — 13,000 officers, 3,000 currently body-camera equipped. Executing algorithmic targets.

**CBP** — 67,000 agents at ports of entry, now deployed domestically. Carrying Mobile Fortify.

**Deputized local police (287(g) program)** — Local law enforcement operating under federal immigration authority with access to federal biometric surveillance tools including Mobile Fortify.

**10,000+ new ICE recruits** — Processed by AI resume screening in 2025, resulting in documented training failures. The AI that selects the enforcers is part of the same system the enforcers execute.

## The Feedback Loop

The system is self-reinforcing:

```
Watchlist flags a target
    ↓
ELITE generates confidence score + target map
    ↓
Agents conduct raid
    ↓
Mobile Fortify scans faces of everyone present
    ↓
Bystander photos enter 1.2B face database — retained 15 years
    ↓
Filming observers may be added to Sparta watchlist
    ↓
Medicaid address updates from arrestees feed future confidence scores
    ↓
Social media posts about the raid monitored by Zignal
    ↓
Loop begins again with expanded dataset
```

Every enforcement action generates data that improves the prediction that directs the next enforcement action. The system learns. The targets expand.

## The Predictive Policing Problem

Standard law enforcement operates on probable cause — a specific individual suspected of a specific act. The AI enforcement architecture operates on **statistical probability** — a neighborhood where the algorithm predicts targets are likely to be present.

The documented consequences:

- **Mobile Fortify misidentified a U.S. citizen woman twice** during an immigration raid. ICE agents treated the algorithmic result as "definitive." She was detained.
- **ELITE's own court testimony** acknowledged the tool could produce wrong addresses at high confidence scores.
- **35 DHS AI systems** classified as high-risk were deployed without completed Privacy Impact Assessments.
- **96 court orders** defied by ICE in Minnesota alone by January 2026, per federal judge Patrick Schiltz.

The human enforcer executing an algorithmic verdict cannot audit the chain of inference that produced it. They are not making a judgment — they are executing an output.

## The Official Denial Architecture

The system operates while its existence is officially denied through a nested set of semantic technicalities:

- **"No database tracking U.S. citizens"** — technically true only if "database" means the specific Terrorist Screening Dataset. The watchlist enterprise operates above that definition.
- **"ELITE is not high-impact"** — DHS's own determination, using a definition of "impact" that excludes neighborhood raids.
- **"Not a master database"** — Palantir's denial, technically accurate in that there is no single unified database. There are dozens of integrated ones.
- **"Human oversight is in place"** — standard AI deployment promise. The Mobile Fortify deployments proceeded without the privacy assessments required for high-impact AI systems.

Each denial is technically defensible within its narrow semantic frame. Together they constitute a coordinated infrastructure of plausible deniability for a system that is operationally exactly what they are denying.

## The Expansion Trajectory

The system is not stable — it is accelerating:

- **February 2026:** ICE building a new watchlisting enterprise in Northern Virginia, recruiting Top Secret-cleared criminal analysts through Xcelerate Solutions
- **February 2026:** DHS moving to consolidate facial recognition, fingerprints, iris scans, and other biometrics into a single department-wide search system
- **2025-2026:** ICE budget includes $300M+ for social media monitoring, facial recognition, license plate readers, and location data
- **2025:** DNA collection expanded — Rapid DNA machines deployed in ICE facilities; DHS proposed expanding the definition of biometrics subject to mandatory collection
- **Heritage Foundation's Michael Scardaville** — former Heritage policy analyst, now DHS Deputy Assistant Secretary for "screening and vetting," the department that wrote the biometric expansion rule. Project 2025 called for exactly this.

## The Analytical Frame

This is not immigration enforcement that uses AI tools. This is an AI predictive policing system that uses human enforcers as its execution layer.

The distinction matters because it determines accountability. When a human officer makes a wrong arrest, there is a chain of command, a decision, a person responsible. When an algorithm generates a confidence score that a human executes without auditing, accountability dissolves into the architecture. The system is designed — whether intentionally or as an emergent property — to make accountability impossible to locate.

The Powell Memo called for capturing institutions. The Sovcorp overlay is building replacements. The AI enforcement architecture is the coercive capacity layer of the emerging configuration — the part that makes resistance costly and compliance enforceable.
