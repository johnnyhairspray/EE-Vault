# Nuclear Infrastructure MOC

## The Central Thesis

The AI infrastructure build-out is approaching an energy wall. The compute required for frontier AI training and inference is growing faster than the power grid can supply it. Nuclear is the only energy source that can deliver the scale, density, and reliability that hyperscalers need. This creates a privatization opportunity — and Thiel's capital rotation pattern suggests he sees it coming.

---

## The AI Energy Wall — The Problem

Large language model training requires enormous sustained power. Data centers can't be sited near wind or solar intermittency. Natural gas requires permitting and grid connection. Nuclear — specifically small modular reactors (SMRs) — offers:

- **Density:** 24/7 baseload power at scale from a small footprint
- **Reliability:** No intermittency problem
- **Co-location potential:** SMRs can be sited adjacent to data centers
- **Carbon optics:** Hyperscalers have net-zero commitments that nuclear satisfies

Microsoft signed a deal to restart Three Mile Island Unit 1 specifically to power its data centers — a proof of concept for the hyperscaler-nuclear model. Google, Amazon, and Meta have all signed nuclear power purchase agreements in 2024-2025.

The problem: the United States' nuclear infrastructure is publicly owned, aging, heavily regulated, and bureaucratically managed through the **Department of Energy** and the **National Nuclear Security Administration (NNSA)**. The expertise is concentrated in the national laboratories — Sandia, Los Alamos, Livermore, Oak Ridge, Argonne. These are the institutions that know how to build, maintain, and operate nuclear systems. They are the institutional bottleneck.

## The DOGE-NNSA Disruption — The Setup

In February 2026, DOGE fired 177 NNSA employees including personnel at Sandia National Laboratories — the lead C-HGB design agent and one of the primary nuclear weapons design institutions in the country. All but approximately 27 were subsequently rehired after significant backlash from the nuclear weapons community.

The stated rationale: cost-cutting. The functional effect: institutional disruption at the precise moment the AI energy wall is becoming visible and nuclear privatization interest is peaking.

The pattern is the same as the Mondaloy Chain: public institutional knowledge nodes disrupted; private capital positioned to absorb the functions. DOGE, run by Musk (SpaceX, xAI), disrupted the nuclear weapons complex while Musk's other entities are competing for government contracts that require nuclear-adjacent expertise.

## Thiel's Capital Rotation — The Strategy

The documented sequence (Thiel Macro SEC filings):

- **Q1 2025:** Initiated position in Vistra — a power generation company with nuclear exposure, riding the AI energy narrative
- **Q3 2025:** Exited Vistra with approximately 42% gain — sold at the narrative peak
- **Q3 2025:** Simultaneously exited Nvidia (~$94M) and trimmed Tesla
- **Q4 2025:** Liquidated entire remaining portfolio (Microsoft, Apple, Tesla) — moved entirely to cash

**The analytical read:** This is not cautious macro hedging. This is deliberate positioning.

1. **Rode the nuclear/AI energy narrative up** — entered Vistra when the hyperscaler-nuclear story was forming
2. **Exited at peak narrative** — sold before the "overpronounced AI-driven buying" corrected
3. **Dumped all AI hardware exposure** — Nvidia, the primary beneficiary of the current AI boom, gone
4. **Went to cash** — maximum optionality, waiting for the dip

The reason to exit Nvidia specifically: hyperscalers are moving toward vertical integration — designing custom AI chips in-house via Broadcom. Nvidia's moat is narrowing. The chip layer of the AI stack is commoditizing. The energy layer is not.

**The thesis Thiel is likely running:**

- AI compute growth hits the energy wall in 2026-2028
- Nvidia-driven AI stock valuations correct when the energy constraint becomes undeniable
- Nuclear becomes the critical infrastructure layer — not optional, not deferrable
- Public nuclear infrastructure (NNSA, national labs) is disrupted, underfunded, and unable to move at the speed required
- Private capital with the right relationships (government contracts, regulatory access, technical expertise) steps in as the solution
- The dip is the entry point — cash provides optionality to buy at the bottom of the AI correction and the bottom of the nuclear build-out's capital-intensive early phase
