# The Technate Redux: Trump's Territorial Ambitions and the Continental Imagination

**Status: Working hypothesis. Territorial parallels are documented and striking. Direct ideological lineage is circumstantial except for the Haldeman-Musk thread, which is established fact.**

## The Original Blueprint

[[Technocracy Inc.]], founded by engineer Howard Scott in 1933, proposed replacing democratic governance with rule by technical experts. At the core of its geopolitical vision was the **Technate of America** — a single continental unit governed not by national borders but by resource logic and technical efficiency.

The Technate's proposed territory: all of Canada, Greenland, Central America, the Caribbean, parts of Colombia, Venezuela, and the Guianas — stretching from the North Pole to the equator, and from the Caribbean to the International Date Line in the Pacific.

The rationale, verbatim from *The Technocrat* (1937): **"The natural resources and the natural boundaries of this area make it an independent, self-sustaining geographical unit."**

How to achieve it? Technocracy Inc. was explicit: **"The government of the United States should take immediate action to acquire these territories… either by purchase, negotiation, or the force of arms."**

## The Trump Territorial Agenda

Starting in December 2024, [[Donald Trump]] publicly and repeatedly advanced the following territorial claims:

- **Canada**: "51st state," "artificially drawn line," "economic force" to merge
- **Greenland**: Would not rule out military force. "One way or the other, we're going to get it." Donald Trump Jr. visited Nuuk January 7, 2025
- **Panama Canal**: Would not rule out military force. Framed as reclaiming strategic infrastructure lost under Carter
- **Venezuela**: Called for inclusion as 51st state in March 2026 following World Baseball Classic

The justification across all four: **resource security, strategic self-sufficiency, and continental defense** — the precise logic of the 1937 Technate rationale.

## The Haldeman Thread

This is not merely structural parallel — there is a documented genealogical link.

**[[Joshua Haldeman]]**, Elon Musk's maternal grandfather, was a prominent organizer of Technocracy Inc. in Canada from 1936 to 1941. He was not a peripheral member — he was leadership. He was later arrested for subversive activities related to the movement.

**[[Elon Musk]]** leads [[DOGE]] — the Department of Government Efficiency — whose stated mission maps directly onto Technocracy Inc.'s core premise: that democratic bureaucracy is inefficient and should be replaced by technical expertise. Musk stated in February 2025: *"We really have here rule of the bureaucracy as opposed to rule of the people."* Technocracy Inc. viewed elected politicians in precisely those terms.

The lineage: Haldeman transmits Technocracy's continental and anti-democratic ideology → Musk inherits the framework, consciously or not → DOGE operationalizes the efficiency doctrine → Trump advances the territorial map.

This does not require Musk to have read his grandfather's pamphlets. Ideological transmission through family culture is well-documented in political psychology. The parallel is too precise to dismiss as coincidence.

## What Differs

The Technate was explicitly anti-capitalist — it rejected the price system entirely in favor of energy accounting. Trump's expansion is explicitly pro-capital, extractive, and resource-oriented. The mechanism differs: Technocracy wanted scientific planning, Trump wants ownership and dominance.

What they share is the **map** and the **logic of continental self-sufficiency through territorial consolidation** — independent of the global rules-based order, secured by force if necessary.

## The Three-Map Convergence (2026 Update)

The Technate Redux hypothesis gains analytical force from a third independent thread arriving at the same territory through a completely different genealogy.

**Map 1: The Technate (1940)**
[[Technocracy Inc.]] under Howard Scott. [[Joshua Haldeman]], Musk's grandfather, organizes the Canadian chapter. The map: all of Canada, Greenland, Central America, Caribbean, parts of northern South America. Rationale: *"The natural resources and natural boundaries of this area make it an independent, self-sustaining geographical unit."* Acquisition method: *"purchase, negotiation, or the force of arms."*

**Map 2: Trump's Territorial Agenda (2019-2026)**
Canada as 51st state. Greenland — military force not ruled out, *"one way or the other, we're going to get it."* Panama Canal. Venezuela. Rationale: economic security, resource self-sufficiency, continental defense. Acquisition method: economic coercion, military force not ruled out. Territory-for-territory match with the 1940 map documented above.

**Map 3: The Network State / Praxis (2019-present)**
[[Balaji Srinivasan]] formalizes [[Network State]] theory — a version of [[Curtis Yarvin]]'s neocameralist patchwork with crypto infrastructure. [[Praxis]], backed by [[Peter Thiel]]/[[Pronomos Capital]]/[[Marc Andreessen]]/Srinivasan, raises $525M to build a charter city. Target: Greenland. Founder Dryden Brown traveled to Nuuk in November 2024. Investor [[Joe Lonsdale]] calls publicly for *"expanding our country to Greenland."* [[Ken Howery]] — Thiel's PayPal co-founder — appointed U.S. Ambassador to Denmark, reportedly tasked with leading Greenland acquisition negotiations.

Brown became interested in Greenland *"when Trump offered to buy it in 2019."* The NRx venture capital network and the U.S. president identified the same territory in the same year independently.

**What This Shows**

Three intellectual genealogies — Technocracy Inc.'s continental self-sufficiency doctrine (1930s), Yarvin's neocameralist patchwork and its Network State derivatives (2008-2022), and Trump's instinctual acquisitionism (2019-present) — all converge on Greenland without coordination. They share the territorial logic without sharing the ideology.

The Technate rationale was autarky and defense. The Network State rationale is exit from democratic regulation. Trump's rationale is resource security and great-power competition. All three arrive at the same conclusion: **acquire Greenland, remove it from existing sovereign frameworks, use it as a resource base and strategic node.**

This is not coincidence requiring explanation. It is the same territorial logic expressing itself through three different operating systems across eight decades.
