# Parady La

Parady La, 46. Cambodian refugee. Died January 9, 2026, three days after entering ICE custody in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania.

## The Incident

La entered ICE custody on January 6, 2026. He was experiencing fentanyl withdrawal. According to family and advocacy groups, La requested medical assistance repeatedly over approximately 24 hours — including requesting water while vomiting — and was denied appropriate withdrawal treatment.

He was found unconscious in his cell on January 7. By the time he reached the hospital he had suffered irreversible anoxic brain injury from oxygen deprivation. He died January 9. Cause of death: complications from fentanyl withdrawal and multi-organ failure.

## Context

Fentanyl withdrawal is a documented medical emergency. Untreated withdrawal can cause severe dehydration, cardiac arrhythmia, and in cases of prolonged denial of care, death. ICE detention facilities are required by detention standards to provide medical care including withdrawal management.

La's case is one of multiple documented ICE custody deaths in the 2025–2026 enforcement surge period. The pattern across these cases — medical needs identified, care delayed or denied, deterioration, death — reflects either systematic failure of detention medical infrastructure or deliberate indifference to detainee health.
