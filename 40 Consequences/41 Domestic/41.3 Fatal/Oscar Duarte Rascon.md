# Oscar Duarte Rascon

Oscar Duarte Rascon, 59. Died September 8, 2025, after approximately one year in ICE custody in Arizona (Florence/Eloy detention facility area).

## The Incident

Duarte Rascon had documented medical conditions at the time of his detention: kidney cancer, Alzheimer's disease, and an inability to walk independently. He was detained despite these conditions and held without humanitarian release while his health deteriorated.

He died of multi-organ failure after approximately one year in custody. His case was documented by immigration advocacy organizations as an example of the administration's "zero-discretion" enforcement posture — where humanitarian release mechanisms that previously applied to terminally ill detainees were not being exercised.

## Context

ICE has authority under existing policy to release detainees on humanitarian grounds — including terminal illness and inability to care for oneself. Under the Trump administration's second-term enforcement posture, the exercise of that discretion effectively ceased.

Duarte Rascon's documented conditions — kidney cancer, Alzheimer's, inability to walk — would under prior administrations have qualified him for humanitarian release review. His death in custody after a year of deterioration reflects the operational consequence of eliminating prosecutorial discretion as a governing principle.
