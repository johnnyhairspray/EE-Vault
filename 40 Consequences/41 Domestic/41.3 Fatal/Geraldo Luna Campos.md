# Geraldo Luna Campos

Cuban national. Died January 3, 2026, at the El Paso Service Processing Center, El Paso, Texas. Manner of death: **homicide**, per autopsy findings.

## The Incident

Campos had a documented history of bipolar disorder and anxiety at the time of his detention. He was held in solitary confinement. During a struggle with guards, he became unresponsive while being physically restrained.

The Bexar County Medical Examiner ruled the cause of death "asphyxia due to neck and torso compression." The autopsy documented hemorrhages in his neck and abrasions on his chest and head — consistent with restraint asphyxia.

**DHS's initial characterization:** The Department of Homeland Security initially described the death as a suicide attempt — a narrative directly contradicted by the Medical Examiner's homicide ruling. The discrepancy between the agency's initial account and the autopsy findings was documented by reporting and advocacy organizations.

## Context

Restraint asphyxia is a documented cause of in-custody death — occurring when physical restraint of a person in a face-down position or with compression of the torso prevents adequate breathing. It has been documented in police and detention settings globally.

Campos's mental health history combined with solitary confinement placement and physical restraint represent a convergence of documented risk factors for in-custody death. The DHS mischaracterization of the cause further documents the accountability gap in ICE detention oversight.
