# Renée Good

Renée Nicole Macklin Good, 37. U.S. citizen, writer, legal observer. Fatally shot by ICE agent Jonathan Ross on January 7, 2026, in Minneapolis, Minnesota, during [[Operation Metro Surge]].

## The Incident

Good was acting as a legal observer — documenting ICE enforcement activity from her vehicle on Portland Avenue South. She was not the target of any enforcement action. Body camera footage from the agent shows Ross firing three shots into Good's vehicle as it was turning away from him. She was struck in the head. She died at the scene.

Ross was identified and charged. His defense characterized the shooting as a response to perceived threat; witnesses and review of the body camera footage disputed that characterization.

## Significance

Good's killing is the most clearly documented case in the vault of a U.S. citizen killed by federal agents while performing a constitutionally protected activity — observing and documenting law enforcement operations.

Legal observers are a recognized civil society function: trained civilians, often affiliated with the National Lawyers Guild, who document enforcement operations for accountability purposes. They wear identifying green hats and maintain a physical distance from enforcement activity.

Her death triggered the Minneapolis protests of January 2026, during which legal observer and VA nurse [[Alex Pretti]] was subsequently killed by federal agents on January 24, 2026.
