# Heber Sánchez Domínguez

Heber Sánchez Domínguez, 34. Mexican national. Died by suicide January 14, 2026, at the Robert A. Deyton Detention Center, Lovejoy, Georgia. He had been in ICE custody for six days.

## The Incident

Sánchez Domínguez was initially encountered by law enforcement following an arrest for driving without a license — a traffic infraction. Under the Trump administration's enforcement posture, the encounter escalated to federal detention. He was transferred to Deyton Detention Center.

Six days after entering custody, he was found hanging in his sleeping quarters at approximately 2:05 a.m. He was pronounced dead.

## Context

His case documents the downstream human cost of the "zero-discretion" enforcement approach: an individual arrested for a traffic infraction — driving without a license, not a violent or even significant criminal offense — placed in federal immigration detention, where the psychological weight of indefinite detention and removal proceedings contributed to his death within a week.

ICE detention centers are required to have mental health screening and suicide prevention protocols. Whether those protocols were applied in Sánchez Domínguez's case, and whether the speed of his deterioration was flagged, is not documented in available sources.

The driving-without-a-license arrest as the entry point to federal detention and death is one of the clearest illustrations of the enforcement posture's proportionality problem.
