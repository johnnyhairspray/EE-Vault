# The Birthright Challenge

Constitutional litigation challenging the Trump administration's executive order limiting birthright citizenship. The case (*Trump v. Barbara*) completed oral arguments at the Supreme Court on April 1, 2026. Decision expected June 2026.

## The 14th Amendment Question

The administration's EO argues that the 14th Amendment's citizenship clause ("All persons born or naturalized in the United States, and subject to the jurisdiction thereof, are citizens") does not extend to children born to parents unlawfully present in the United States — reinterpreting "subject to the jurisdiction thereof" to exclude undocumented individuals.

This would reverse approximately 150 years of consistent legal interpretation dating to *United States v. Wong Kim Ark* (1898), which established that birthright citizenship applied to children born on U.S. soil regardless of their parents' immigration status.

The administration's argument has been rejected by every lower court that has reviewed it. The Supreme Court agreed to hear the case.

## The Habeas Corpus Surge

Following [[Operation Metro Surge]], over 1,000 habeas corpus petitions were filed in Minnesota alone — challenging detentions made without warrants or with insufficient legal basis. This represents one of the largest habeas surges in recent U.S. history from a single geographic area.

Federal courts issued preliminary injunctions at multiple detention facilities requiring ICE to provide detainees with confidential access to legal counsel — ruling that existing conditions violated due process.

## The 8th Circuit No-Bond Ruling

In March 2026, the 8th Circuit Court of Appeals upheld the administration's mandatory detention policy for certain immigrants without bond — even without serious criminal records. Combined with the Accountability Centers civil commitment framework, this ruling provides legal authorization for indefinite detention without bail hearings across a broad category of immigration cases.

## The Denaturalization Parallel

The birthright challenge is the prospective version of the denaturalization campaign documented in the [[Stephen Miller]] vault note. Denaturalization targets existing citizens retroactively; the birthright challenge eliminates citizenship prospectively for future births. Together they represent a two-track strategy for reducing the size of the U.S. citizen population among groups the administration targets.
