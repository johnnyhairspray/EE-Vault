# Mass Deportation

The Trump administration's stated goal of deporting 1 million+ individuals annually — operationalized through the combination of ImmigrationOS AI targeting, OBBB enforcement funding, and the deployment of 12,000+ new ICE officers.

## The Scale

**1 million annual removal target:** Set by Stephen Miller's directive establishing ICE daily arrest quotas of 3,000/day (May 2025) — roughly triple prior levels. The $126 billion enforcement allocation in the [[One Big Beautiful Bill]] provides the funding infrastructure.

**Expedited removal expansion:** Standard immigration court proceedings are being bypassed through expanded Expedited Removal authorities — allowing removal without full hearings for individuals who cannot immediately prove lawful presence. This dramatically accelerates processing throughput but eliminates procedural safeguards.

**Self-deportation:** Approximately 2 million individuals had self-deported by January 2026 under enforcement pressure — a population deterrence effect the administration counts toward its enforcement record without requiring operational arrests.

## The Operational Template

[[Operation Metro Surge]] — the January-February 2026 Minneapolis deployment — is the documented proof of concept for urban mass removal: 3,000 federal agents, 3,789 arrests, $203M economic impact, two U.S. citizens killed by federal agents.

The same infrastructure (ImmigrationOS targeting, BORTAC tactical units, warehouse detention hubs, remote video deportation hearings) is deployable to any metropolitan area. Minneapolis was the test.

## The Human Cost

**ICE custody deaths:** At least 40 deaths attributed to ICE enforcement between 2025 and April 2026 — documented in the vault's Fatal Consequences notes (Renée Good, Alex Pretti, Keith Porter Jr., Parady La, Oscar Duarte Rascon, Geraldo Luna Campos, Heber Sánchez Domínguez).

**Economic disruption:** Operations in targeted cities produce documented economic contraction — businesses losing workers and customers, schools shifting to remote learning, healthcare avoidance creating downstream public health consequences.

**Workforce impact:** Deportation of individuals embedded in agricultural, construction, food service, and healthcare sectors creates supply chain disruptions documented across multiple industry analyses.

## The Legal Framework

- **January 20, 2025 EO:** "Protecting the American People Against Invasion" — declared a national emergency at the border
- **OBBB:** $126B enforcement funding, mandatory detention expansion, non-waivable fees
- **ImmigrationOS + ELITE:** AI targeting infrastructure
- **Accountability Centers:** Civil commitment facilities for hold-without-trial detention
